# L13 Data 1

Lecture 13 is the first data lecture: data is the part everyone under-explains, and it is also the part that quietly decides most of the model quality.

## Summary
- Data is the main differentiator. The lecture starts by showing how little model papers disclose, then walks from BERT-era corpora to GPT-3 and Llama-style mixtures. Source: `[0:04]` to `[6:50]`, `[42:06]` to `[59:32]`.
- WebText and Common Crawl are the pivot from curated corpora to internet-scale raw material, but Common Crawl is only useful after a lot of processing. Source: `[12:22]` to `[18:56]`.
- CCNet, C4, OpenWebText, The Pile, MassiveText, Llama, RefinedWeb, FineWeb, Dolma, DataComp, DCLM, and Nemotron-CC are successive attempts to turn the web into trainable tokens. Source: `[21:39]` to `[59:32]`.
- The filtering story shifts from rules to models: quality classifiers, fastText, and even instruction-like positives increasingly define what "good" data means. Source: `[28:29]` to `[59:32]`.
- Legal and operational constraints are not side issues. Multilinguality, copyright, fair use, and terms of use shape what can be collected and published. Source: `[1:00:14]` to `[1:18:58]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Pre-training / mid-training / post-training:** the lecture uses these stages to explain why data mixes change over time. Source: `[2:51]` to `[4:06]`.
- **BooksCorpus:** the old books corpus scraped from Smashwords that fed early BERT-style work. Source: `[7:11]` to `[8:12]`.
- **Wikipedia:** a high-citation, Creative Commons source that often serves as a surrogate for "quality." Source: `[8:20]` to `[10:09]`, `[22:37]` to `[23:11]`.
- **WebText:** GPT-2's Reddit-linked web subset, chosen for breadth and relative quality. Source: `[12:22]` to `[13:14]`.
- **Common Crawl:** an approximate web crawl, not the web itself. Source: `[13:31]` to `[18:18]`.
- **WARC / WET:** raw HTML versus lossy text extraction. Source: `[16:18]` to `[17:24]`.
- **CCNet:** Common Crawl filtered with duplication removal, language ID, and a Wikipedia-like 5-gram model. Source: `[21:55]` to `[23:30]`.
- **C4:** a rule-based Common Crawl cleanup that keeps English and discards obvious junk. Source: `[23:51]` to `[25:49]`.
- **OpenWebText / WebText2:** reproductions of WebText-like data from Common Crawl and Reddit-linked pages. Source: `[26:21]` to `[28:08]`.
- **The Pile:** a volunteer-curated blend of Common Crawl, OpenWebText, Stack Exchange, Wikipedia, arXiv, code, and more. Source: `[29:05]` to `[31:00]`.
- **MassiveText / MassiveWeb:** Gopher-era data with manual rules and SafeSearch toxicity filtering. Source: `[42:06]` to `[44:26]`.
- **Llama / RedPajama:** Common Crawl + CCNet style processing, plus reproducible open re-creations. Source: `[44:30]` to `[46:07]`.
- **RefinedWeb / FineWeb:** the thesis that sufficiently good web filtering can make extra curated sources less necessary. Source: `[46:34]` to `[49:01]`.
- **Dolma / DataComp / DCLM-baseline:** open data curation with classifier-driven filtering and aggressive pruning. Source: `[49:01]` to `[54:38]`.
- **Nemotron-CC:** a later pipeline that uses model scores, bucketed sampling, and synthetic task generation. Source: `[55:16]` to `[59:32]`.
- **Copyright / fair use / terms of use:** the legal layer that determines whether data is usable at all. Source: `[1:00:41]` to `[1:10:10]`.
- **Long context and instruction data:** mid-training often adds long context, then synthetic instruction formats like SuperNaturalInstructions and Flan. Source: `[1:10:10]` to `[1:17:41]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- Common Crawl is an approximation of the internet, but not a complete one. The crawl is selective, polite, and incomplete by design. Source: `[13:51]` to `[18:00]`.
- Quality filtering is usually surrogate-based. Wikipedia, WebText, and high-quality domains stand in for a hidden target distribution. Source: `[21:55]` to `[23:30]`, `[28:29]` to `[29:02]`.
- Rule-based and model-based filtering are complementary. One gives coverage and simplicity, the other gives sharper quality signals. Source: `[23:51]` to `[26:18]`, `[50:15]` to `[59:32]`.
- The pipeline is increasingly closed-loop. Better models now help define the next round of data. Source: `[52:22]` to `[59:32]`, `[1:13:43]` to `[1:17:23]`.
- The open-source reproduction efforts expose hidden work. "We trained on GitHub" is not a useful statement until you know the preprocessing. Source: `[39:56]` to `[41:49]`.
- Mid-training is where data gets more targeted. The lecture treats long context, math, and instruction format as later-stage data problems. Source: `[1:10:38]` to `[1:17:41]`.
- Synthetic data is not a niche hack anymore. It is a normal way to expand instruction and post-training corpora. Source: `[1:13:43]` to `[1:17:23]`.
