# L10 Inference

Lecture 10 leaves scaling laws for a bit and turns into the actual serving problem: prefill is cheap, generation is not, and the KV cache is the tax collector.

## Summary
- Inference is everywhere: chat, code completion, batch processing, evaluation, test-time compute, and even RL sampling. Source: `[0:16]` to `[2:03]`.
- The core split is prefill vs generation. Prefill is compute-limited; generation is memory-limited. Source: `[16:09]` to `[18:34]`, `[27:03]` to `[27:47]`.
- TTFT, latency, and throughput are different metrics, and batch size pushes them in different directions. Source: `[3:10]` to `[3:53]`, `[32:22]` to `[34:11]`.
- The KV cache is the bottleneck that almost every optimization in the lecture is trying to shrink. Source: `[16:00]` to `[17:27]`, `[38:26]` to `[39:24]`.
- The lecture walks through three families of fixes: better attention layouts, compression / quantization, and speculative decoding. Source: `[40:06]` to `[52:47]`, `[1:04:58]` to `[1:17:30]`.

## Key Concepts
- **TTFT:** time-to-first-token, the user-facing latency metric that matters for interactive apps. Source: `[3:10]` to `[3:27]`.
- **Latency vs throughput:** the same system can be fast for one user and efficient for many users. Source: `[3:27]` to `[3:53]`.
- **KV cache:** cached keys and values turn quadratic attention into a memory problem. Source: `[16:00]` to `[17:27]`.
- **Prefill:** parallel, compute-limited, and relatively pleasant. Source: `[17:30]` to `[18:43]`.
- **Generation:** sequential, memory-limited, and expensive. Source: `[17:40]` to `[18:34]`.
- **GQA / MLA / cross-layer attention:** different ways of shrinking the cache without killing accuracy. Source: `[40:06]` to `[47:46]`.
- **Local attention:** keep only the recent window and use global layers sparingly. Source: `[47:50]` to `[50:32]`.
- **Quantization:** lower precision reduces bandwidth pressure, but outliers and accuracy matter. Source: `[1:04:58]` to `[1:09:08]`.
- **Speculative decoding:** cheap draft model + expensive verifier gives exact samples with speedup. Source: `[1:11:24]` to `[1:17:30]`.
- **PagedAttention:** serving is also an operating-systems problem. Source: `[1:18:57]` to `[1:21:24]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The serving bottleneck is not just compute. It is memory bandwidth, fragmentation, and cache layout. Source: `[27:03]` to `[34:11]`, `[1:18:57]` to `[1:21:24]`.
- Bigger batches raise throughput, but they also raise latency and memory pressure. There is no free lunch. Source: `[32:22]` to `[34:11]`.
- A lot of the real gains come from architectural changes, not just kernels. The lecture says this explicitly near the end. Source: `[1:22:00]` to `[1:22:39]`.
- Local attention is a speed trick that can damage the very thing attention was supposed to buy: long-range context. Source: `[48:07]` to `[49:29]`.
- Speculative decoding is neat because it keeps exact sampling while stealing the parallelism of the prefill stage. Source: `[1:11:31]` to `[1:15:09]`.
