# CS336 Gems

These are cross-lecture inferences, not quotes from any single lecture. The through-line is simple: the course keeps turning model work into budget work.

## Top Insights
- **The whole stack is one budget.** Tokenization, tensor layout, GPU kernels, parallelism, serving, and evaluation all collapse onto the same question: how many bytes move, when, and where the bottleneck lives. Sources: [L01](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L01-tokenization.md#L21), [L02](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L02-neural-lm-basics.md#L22), [L05](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L05-gpus.md#L19), [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L07-parallelism-1.md#L19), [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L10.md#L25), [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L12.md#L27), [L19](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L19.md#L24).
- **Architecture converges when hardware starts voting.** Pre-norm, RMSNorm, SwiGLU, RoPE, sparse attention variants, and MoE survive because they reduce systems friction as much as they improve quality. Sources: [L03](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L03-training-from-scratch.md#L23), [L04](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L04-mixture-of-experts.md#L20), [L05](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L05-gpus.md#L19), [L19](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L19.md#L25).
- **The best signal is usually not the final score.** The course keeps preferring smoother proxies: likelihood over accuracy, calibration curves over raw benchmark hits, baselines and advantages over raw reward, and group-relative comparisons over global ones. Sources: [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L09.md#L24), [L11](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L11.md#L23), [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L12.md#L27), [L17](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L17.md#L29), [L19](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L19.md#L24).
- **Verifiability is the escape hatch.** The move from chat models to code agents and reasoning RL happens when the environment can tell you whether the model was right. Sources: [L16](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L16.md#L16), [L17](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L17.md#L28), [L18](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L18.md#L14), [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L12.md#L23).
- **Data is model design.** Filtering, dedup, and synthetic data are not cleanup chores; they define the target distribution the model is learning to inhabit. Sources: [L13](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L13.md#L33), [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L14.md#L30), [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L15.md#L27), [L19](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L19.md#L19).

## Surprising Connections
- **Local comparison keeps replacing global judgment.** GRPO uses same-question rollouts, MinHash/LSH turn pairwise similarity into buckets, and benchmark design often compares models only after normalizing for compute or use case. Sources: [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L14.md#L31), [L16](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L16.md#L19), [L17](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L17.md#L29), [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L09.md#L24), [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L12.md#L27).
- **Memory-for-compute trades recur everywhere.** Activation checkpointing, KV-cache compression, log-prob caching, speculative decoding, and recomputation are all the same move in different costumes. Sources: [L02](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L02-neural-lm-basics.md#L19), [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L07-parallelism-1.md#L23), [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L08-parallelism-2.md#L23), [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L10.md#L25), [L17](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L17.md#L32).
- **The alignment stack mirrors the distributed-training stack.** RLHF and GRPO need policy, reference, and rollout machinery just as training needs data, tensor, pipeline, and expert sharding. Sources: [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L07-parallelism-1.md#L24), [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L08-parallelism-2.md#L28), [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L15.md#L21), [L16](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L16.md#L31), [L17](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L17.md#L33).
- **Evaluation and scaffolding are the same problem at different zoom levels.** MMLU, Chatbot Arena, SWE-bench, Terminal-Bench, and Qwen Code all ask whether the measurement harness matches real usage. Sources: [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L12.md#L27), [L18](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L18.md#L24), [L19](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L19.md#L20).
- **Curation and alignment are adjacent control systems.** One shapes what the model reads, the other shapes what it says; both care about noisy labels, hidden targets, and feedback loops. Sources: [L13](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L13.md#L35), [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L14.md#L30), [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L15.md#L27), [L16](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L16.md#L28).
- **MoE is cluster design wearing an architecture hat.** Once experts are sharded, routing logic and network topology become part of the model. Sources: [L04](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L04-mixture-of-experts.md#L24), [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L07-parallelism-1.md#L27), [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L08-parallelism-2.md#L29), [L18](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L18.md#L17).

## Under-Appreciated Ideas
- **Tokenization is policy, not just preprocessing.** BPE allocates context budget across frequent and rare patterns, and the absence of a clean byte-native model makes that compromise persistent. Sources: [L01](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L01-tokenization.md#L21), [L02](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L02-neural-lm-basics.md#L22), [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L10.md#L15), [L19](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L19.md#L24).
- **`torch.compile` and Triton matter because they make the fast path legible.** The lesson is not "write custom kernels everywhere"; it is "measure first, then only go low-level when the bottleneck is real." Sources: [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L06-kernels-triton.md#L19), [L05](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L05-gpus.md#L23).
- **Repeated exposure is sometimes correct.** Mid-training can want repetition, which means dedup is not a universal good; it depends on whether the corpus is pre-training noise or a high-value refinement set. Sources: [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L14.md#L35), [L13](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L13.md#L37).
- **Benchmark coverage beats leaderboard rank.** The course keeps showing that a smooth number is only useful if it measures the right slice of reality. Sources: [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L12.md#L27), [L18](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L18.md#L25), [L19](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L19.md#L20).
- **RLHF's main failure mode is annotation quality.** The choice between PPO and DPO matters less than whether the preference data is noisy, biased, or proxy-shaped. Sources: [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L15.md#L27), [L16](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L16.md#L28), [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L12.md#L27).
- **Inference is an OS problem.** PagedAttention, batching, fragmentation, and cache layout make serving feel like memory management with logits. Sources: [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L10.md#L25), [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L06-kernels-triton.md#L20), [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L08-parallelism-2.md#L23).
- **Scaling laws are method, not magic.** Their value is in reducing uncertainty before the expensive run, not in providing prophecy. Sources: [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L09.md#L23), [L11](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L11.md#L23), [L19](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS336/lectures/L19.md#L23).
