# L03 Full Cycle of a DL Project

Lecture 3 is the project workflow lecture. It treats deep learning as an iterative system built from code, data, labels, failure analysis, and maintenance.

## Summary
- The lecture frames AI work as iterative rather than linear: build something, inspect where it fails, then change the data or the model and repeat. Source: `[1:26]` to `[2:43]`, `[7:51]` to `[9:35]`.
- Speed is the first design constraint. Getting some data in one or two days is usually better than waiting weeks for a "better" collection plan. Source: `[16:21]` to `[20:47]`.
- Data-centric AI is the explicit method: use error analysis to identify which subset of data needs improvement, not just more volume. Source: `[26:52]` to `[30:35]`.
- The lecture extends that logic to frontier models and coding assistants, where quality, subcategory choice, and curated data matter more than random internet scale. Source: `[29:11]` to `[30:35]`.
- Deployment is not the end of the loop. Data drift, concept drift, dashboards, and monitoring are part of the system. Source: `[55:13]` to `[1:06:52]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Code vs data.** Traditional software is deterministic; ML systems depend on hidden variation in the data. Source: `[1:26]` to `[2:43]`.
- **Iterative workflow.** Design, train, analyze, and revise the model or the data in a loop. Source: `[8:59]` to `[9:35]`.
- **Speed as a heuristic.** Quick data collection is preferred because it exposes failure modes earlier. Source: `[16:21]` to `[20:36]`.
- **Data collection tactics.** Opt-in collection, campus sampling, and other fast tactics beat theoretical perfection when you are trying to learn. Source: `[18:44]` to `[20:23]`.
- **Error analysis.** Inspect where the model fails, then target the weakest slice. Source: `[26:19]` to `[28:37]`.
- **Data-centric AI.** Improve the system by systematically editing the data, not only the architecture. Source: `[27:02]` to `[27:47]`.
- **Distribution shift.** Training and deployment distributions diverge as the world changes. Source: `[55:13]` to `[1:00:52]`.
- **Monitoring.** Dashboards, sampled inspection, and privacy-aware telemetry are part of maintenance. Source: `[1:01:47]` to `[1:06:52]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The lecture's preferred mode is not "collect more data" but "collect enough data to find out what is wrong." Source: `[17:13]` to `[17:37]`, `[27:26]` to `[28:37]`.
- More data is only useful if it is the right subcategory. Generic volume is a weak strategy compared with targeted coverage. Source: `[28:56]` to `[30:35]`.
- A simpler rule-based system can sometimes be more robust to drift than a neural net, even if the neural net is more powerful on paper. Source: `[1:00:30]` to `[1:01:12]`.
- Frontier-model progress is increasingly about curated data for specific verticals like coding, not random scraping. Source: `[29:25]` to `[30:09]`.
- Monitoring is a product discipline, not an afterthought. The lecture treats post-deploy surveillance as part of model quality. Source: `[1:01:47]` to `[1:06:52]`.
