# L07 Language Models and New Frontiers

This lecture starts with the part of AI people usually skip: failure modes. It treats generalization, uncertainty, adversarial examples, and data quality as the real subject, then uses diffusion and LLMs as the new frontier.

## Summary
- The lecture uses the classic `Understanding Deep Neural Networks Requires Rethinking Generalization` result to show that neural nets can memorize random labels while still failing to generalize out of distribution. Source: `[10:10]` to `[14:23]`.
- It turns that into a responsible-AI argument: confidence, uncertainty, adversarial examples, and data quality matter in autonomy, medicine, facial recognition, security, and privacy. Source: `[14:00]` to `[23:23]`.
- It treats adversarial examples as the mirrored problem of training: perturb the input instead of the weights, and small changes can flip the decision. Source: `[19:10]` to `[21:10]`.
- It frames diffusion models as the answer to unstable generative modelling: iterative denoising beats single-shot generation and avoids mode collapse. Source: `[25:17]` to `[35:22]`.
- It closes by rederiving LLMs from next-token prediction, then points at hallucination, reasoning, and tool use as the remaining frontier. Source: `[35:48]` to `[46:58]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Generalization.** Training accuracy is cheap; out-of-distribution behavior is the real test. Source: `[10:10]` to `[14:23]`.
- **Confidence calibration.** The model should know what it does not know. Source: `[14:00]` to `[14:23]`.
- **Data quality.** Garbage in, garbage out still holds. Source: `[15:01]` to `[15:37]`.
- **Adversarial examples.** Small perturbations can cause a confident model to fail. Source: `[19:10]` to `[21:10]`.
- **Uncertainty.** Safety-critical applications need calibrated outputs, not just high scores. Source: `[18:23]` to `[19:05]`.
- **Diffusion models.** Generative modelling becomes iterative denoising instead of one-shot synthesis. Source: `[25:17]` to `[29:52]`.
- **Mode collapse.** Single-shot generators can drift toward averaged outputs and lose diversity. Source: `[25:17]` to `[25:40]`.
- **Next-token prediction.** LLMs are introduced as the same old sequence game, scaled up. Source: `[35:48]` to `[41:20]`.
- **Tokenization.** Raw text becomes training material only after it is chunked into model tokens. Source: `[38:26]` to `[41:20]`.
- **Hallucination.** The frontier problem is not only generation, but grounded generation. Source: `[41:35]` to `[46:58]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The lecture treats uncertainty as a deployment problem, not a philosophical one. Source: `[18:23]` to `[19:05]`.
- Random-label fitting is the warning sign: deep nets can memorize almost anything, which is why generalization has to be measured separately. Source: `[10:23]` to `[13:00]`.
- Diffusion matters because it breaks generation into smaller reversible steps. That is the structural fix, not just a better loss. Source: `[25:24]` to `[29:52]`.
- LLMs are presented as an extension of the same next-token machinery, not as a separate species of intelligence. Source: `[35:48]` to `[41:20]`.
- Hallucination is framed as a confidence and grounding problem, not just a wording problem. Source: `[41:35]` to `[43:22]`.
