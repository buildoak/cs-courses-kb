# L03 Recurrent Neural Networks, Transformers, and Attention

This lecture is the bridge from static networks to time-indexed models: recurrence, sequence loss, gradient failure modes, and then the attention/Transformer escape hatch.

## Summary
- It opens by showing why sequence modeling matters at all: language, audio, text, biology, weather, video, and music all carry order. Source: `[3:03]` to `[5:36]`.
- It builds the RNN from first principles: hidden state, recurrence relation, shared weights, and step-by-step unrolling across time. Source: `[14:26]` to `[19:19]`.
- It shows that training works by summing loss at each time step, not just at the end of the sequence. Source: `[19:33]` to `[22:12]`.
- It then names the classic instability: exploding and vanishing gradients, which makes long-term dependencies hard to learn. LSTM is the fix the lecture calls out. Source: `[36:20]` to `[38:27]`.
- It ends by reframing the whole problem as attention: query, key, value, positional embeddings, softmax weights, and multi-head Transformers. Source: `[46:08]` to `[58:31]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Sequence modeling.** Inputs and outputs can be ordered sequences of variable length. Source: `[3:03]` to `[5:36]`.
- **Hidden state.** A memory vector carried from one time step to the next. Source: `[14:26]` to `[15:26]`.
- **Recurrence relation.** Update state from the current input plus the previous state. Source: `[15:10]` to `[15:39]`.
- **Unrolling.** View the same RNN cell across time steps as a repeated computation graph. Source: `[18:28]` to `[19:19]`.
- **Shared weights.** The same parameters are reused at every time step. Source: `[18:48]` to `[19:19]`.
- **Time-step loss.** Add up the loss across the sequence instead of supervising only the final state. Source: `[19:33]` to `[20:09]`.
- **Long-term dependencies.** Earlier tokens can determine meaning much later in the sequence. Source: `[25:54]` to `[26:25]`.
- **Vanishing / exploding gradients.** Repeated multiplication makes gradients blow up or shrink to zero. Source: `[36:20]` to `[37:29]`.
- **LSTM.** A gated recurrent architecture that selectively controls what memory is carried forward. Source: `[38:00]` to `[38:27]`.
- **Attention.** Search over the sequence by comparing queries and keys, then pull values. Source: `[49:12]` to `[56:20]`.
- **Positional embedding.** Encode order without recurrence. Source: `[51:23]` to `[52:19]`.
- **Multi-head Transformer.** Stack multiple attention heads to capture different relations. Source: `[57:20]` to `[58:31]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- RNNs are not just "for text". They are a general memory machine for any ordered stream. Source: `[3:03]` to `[5:36]`.
- The key design move is state reuse. One set of weights runs across time, which is why the same cell can read a whole sequence. Source: `[18:48]` to `[19:19]`.
- The training signal is spread across time. If you only care about the final output, you miss the fact that earlier steps still need supervision. Source: `[19:33]` to `[20:09]`.
- The RNN failure mode is not just "short memory". It is unstable gradient transport across many repeated multiplications. Source: `[36:20]` to `[37:29]`.
- Attention is basically search. The model asks a query, compares it against keys, and extracts the matching values. Source: `[49:12]` to `[56:20]`.
- Multi-head attention is not just bigger attention. It gives different relational views of the same sequence. Source: `[57:20]` to `[58:31]`.
