Same bootcamp spine as L01, but with a fresh progress arc, a live offline phone demo, and more time spent on the mechanics of optimization and regularization.

## Key Concepts
- **Feature hierarchy.** Deep learning learns edges, curves, parts, and objects instead of hard-coding them. Source: `[16:14]` to `[17:21]`.
- **Perceptron.** Weighted sum + bias + nonlinearity. Source: `[19:23]` to `[20:20]`.
- **Dense layer.** The matrix version of the same forward pass. Source: `[30:57]` to `[31:08]`.
- **Depth.** More layers mean more hierarchical nonlinear composition. Source: `[33:23]` to `[35:24]`.
- **Loss / empirical loss.** Average error over the training set. Source: `[38:37]` to `[39:42]`.
- **Backpropagation.** Compute derivatives from output back to input through the network. Source: `[46:18]` to `[46:42]`.
- **SGD.** Estimate the gradient from one randomly selected point. Source: `[46:55]` to `[47:14]`.
- **Mini-batch gradient descent.** Use a small batch to balance noise and efficiency. Source: `[47:28]` to `[48:36]`.
- **Learning rate.** Step size that can stall, converge, or explode depending on scale. Source: `[43:27]` to `[45:07]`.
- **Dropout.** Randomly zero hidden activations during training so the network cannot depend on one pathway. Source: `[1:02:27]` to `[1:03:31]`.
- **Early stopping.** Choose the checkpoint before validation loss rises. Source: `[1:04:04]` to `[1:05:54]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The impressive part of the 2020 video story is the cost structure: the result looked simple, but it took a lot of recorded audio, video, and compute to make it work. Source: `[3:39]` to `[4:31]`.
- The lecture is explicit that the old algorithms did not suddenly become better; the ecosystem around them did. Source: `[18:32]` to `[19:19]`.
- Full-batch gradients are mostly a teaching abstraction. Mini-batches are the practical middle ground between accuracy and throughput. Source: `[46:18]` to `[48:36]`.
- Adaptive optimizers matter because learning-rate selection is one of the hardest practical knobs in neural-network training. Source: `[43:27]` to `[45:58]`.
- Dropout changes the forward pass itself, so even the same example does not traverse the same network twice during training. Source: `[1:02:29]` to `[1:03:25]`.
- Early stopping is a checkpoint-selection problem: you keep training, but you do not keep the later weights. Source: `[1:04:28]` to `[1:05:54]`.
