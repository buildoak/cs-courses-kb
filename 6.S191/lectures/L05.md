# L05 Deep Generative Modeling

This lecture is the move from classification to synthesis: learn the data distribution, compress it into a latent space, regularize that space until it behaves, then generate or translate new samples from it.

## Summary
- The lecture frames generative modeling as learning the data distribution, not just mapping inputs to labels. Source: `[0:26]` to `[4:39]`.
- It uses the cave allegory to motivate latent variables and then builds autoencoders as the first practical latent-space model. Source: `[7:38]` to `[15:23]`.
- It pushes autoencoders into VAEs by adding stochastic latent variables, a prior, and KL regularization so the latent space stays usable. Source: `[15:37]` to `[27:21]`.
- It shows why latent-space geometry matters: continuity, completeness, and smooth interpolation are the difference between a toy bottleneck and a generative model. Source: `[23:01]` to `[29:47]`.
- It finishes with GANs and cycleGANs, where generation becomes a generator/discriminator game and then an image-to-image translation problem. Source: `[32:48]` to `[45:18]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Generative modeling.** Learn a probability distribution over data so you can sample new instances. Source: `[2:43]` to `[4:39]`.
- **Unsupervised learning.** Work with data without labels and infer structure directly from it. Source: `[2:33]` to `[2:52]`.
- **Latent variable.** Hidden factor that explains the observed data. Source: `[7:38]` to `[9:14]`.
- **Autoencoder.** Encoder compresses data into a latent vector; decoder reconstructs it. Source: `[9:09]` to `[15:23]`.
- **Reconstruction loss.** Train by minimizing the gap between original input and decoded output. Source: `[12:18]` to `[13:47]`.
- **VAE.** Add randomness to the latent space so the model can sample and generate, not just reconstruct. Source: `[15:37]` to `[18:47]`.
- **Prior.** Use a simple distribution, often normal, to shape the latent space. Source: `[21:16]` to `[21:58]`.
- **KL divergence.** Measure the distance between the inferred latent distribution and the prior. Source: `[22:10]` to `[22:25]`.
- **Continuity and completeness.** Nearby latent points should decode to nearby outputs, and sampling should stay meaningful. Source: `[23:01]` to `[26:20]`.
- **Reparameterization trick.** Make stochastic latent sampling trainable with backprop. Source: `[26:45]` to `[27:21]`.
- **GAN.** A generator makes fake samples while a discriminator tries to tell real from fake. Source: `[32:48]` to `[38:22]`.
- **CycleGAN.** Unpaired image-to-image translation with a cyclic loss across two domains. Source: `[41:02]` to `[45:18]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- Inference: The latent space is not only compression. It is geometry. If the geometry is bad, sampling gives nonsense. Source: `[23:01]` to `[25:28]`.
- Inference: VAEs need stochasticity, but that stochasticity creates the gradient problem that the reparameterization trick solves. Source: `[16:44]` to `[27:21]`.
- Inference: The KL term is doing shape control, not decoration. It keeps the latent distribution smooth enough to sample from. Source: `[21:16]` to `[26:20]`.
- Inference: GANs avoid explicit density estimation by turning generation into competition. That is clever, but also unstable by construction. Source: `[33:37]` to `[38:22]`.
- Inference: Smooth interpolation in random-noise space becomes smooth interpolation in the learned data manifold. That is the quiet superpower of GANs. Source: `[39:23]` to `[39:52]`.
- Inference: CycleGAN says translation is the real problem in some settings, not raw generation. The model learns how to move between domains without paired examples. Source: `[41:02]` to `[45:18]`.
