# 6.S191 Gems

## Top Insights
- Inference: 6.S191 is really about signal design. Supervised labels, time-step loss, reconstruction loss, reward, preference data, judge scores, and test-time search are all different ways of telling a model what matters. Sources: [L01](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L01.md#L8), [L03](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L03.md#L8), [L05](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L05.md#L8), [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L06.md#L8), [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L08.md#L8), [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L09.md#L10).
- Inference: Architecture is mostly inductive bias. CNNs assume locality, RNNs assume order, attention assumes searchable memory, latent models assume smooth manifolds, and RL assumes rewardable trajectories. Sources: [L03](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L03.md#L22), [L04](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L04.md#L18), [L05](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L05.md#L21), [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L06.md#L18).
- Inference: The practical bottleneck is control, not raw model size. Learning rate, minibatches, dropout, early stopping, calibration, safety layers, and evaluation loops are all control systems wrapped around the model. Sources: [L01](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L01.md#L19), [L02](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L02.md#L22), [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L07.md#L14), [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L08.md#L9), [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L09.md#L10).
- Inference: The course keeps collapsing the boundary between training and inference. Self-play, synthetic preference data, back-translation, judge models, best-of-n, and tool traces all let the system manufacture its own next training signal. Sources: [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L06.md#L32), [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L08.md#L31), [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L09.md#L8).
- Inference: Compression only works when the right invariants survive it. Flattening breaks images, pooling trades resolution for invariance, VAEs need a usable latent geometry, and LoRA / QLoRA preserve capability by compressing the update instead of the whole model. Sources: [L04](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L04.md#L17), [L05](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L05.md#L27), [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L09.md#L21).

## Surprising Connections
- Inference: Dropout, adversarial examples, and self-play are all perturbation machines. One makes the network robust to internal failure, one probes it from the outside, and one manufactures an opponent so the agent cannot coast. Sources: [L01](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L01.md#L21), [L02](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L02.md#L22), [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L06.md#L32), [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L07.md#L16).
- Inference: Attention, Q-learning, and best-of-n are all search. They score candidates in different spaces: tokens, actions, or full responses. Sources: [L03](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L03.md#L31), [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L06.md#L28), [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L09.md#L36).
- Inference: CycleGAN, ReAct, and Toolformer all solve translation between worlds. One maps image domains, one maps reasoning to action, and one maps text to external tools. Sources: [L05](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L05.md#L32), [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L08.md#L23).
- Inference: CNN feature extractors and LoRA adapters are both ways of reusing a large learned backbone while constraining what can change. The trick is not just efficiency; it is keeping the useful representation stable. Sources: [L04](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L04.md#L24), [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L09.md#L21).
- Inference: Early stopping, validation loss, reward-model uncertainty, and calibration are the same question at different stages: which version should ship. Sources: [L01](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L01.md#L22), [L02](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L02.md#L23), [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L07.md#L14), [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L09.md#L30).

## Under-Appreciated Ideas
- Inference: Learning rate is not a routine hyperparameter. It is the knife-edge between stagnation, convergence, and blow-up, and the course returns to that problem more than once. Sources: [L01](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L01.md#L27), [L02](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L02.md#L21), [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L09.md#L24).
- Inference: Data diversity matters as much as correctness. L09 says good data needs accuracy, diversity, and complexity, and synthetic data can quietly flatten the distribution you needed to preserve. Sources: [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L09.md#L7), [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L09.md#L33).
- Inference: Tokenizer design is upstream of alignment and prompting. If the text is chunked badly, later instruction tuning, chain-of-thought, and post-training can only patch the damage. Sources: [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L08.md#L17), [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L09.md#L37).
- Inference: Latent-space geometry is the real test in generative modeling. Reconstruction can look fine while sampling still fails because the manifold is discontinuous or incomplete. Sources: [L05](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L05.md#L27), [L05](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L05.md#L29).
- Inference: Reward design is the actual spec in RL. The environment designer decides what behavior is optimized, so the reward function is more than a metric; it is the control surface. Sources: [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L06.md#L30), [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L06.md#L17).
- Inference: Pooling is deliberate information loss, not a bag of tricks. The point is to buy invariance and larger effective context by throwing away detail on purpose. Sources: [L04](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L04.md#L22), [L04](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/6.S191/lectures/L04.md#L29).
