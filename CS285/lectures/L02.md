# L02 What Reinforcement Learning Is

This lecture turns RL from a story into a formal object: a sequential decision problem where the agent chooses actions, gets rewards, and tries to maximize cumulative return.

## Key Concepts
- **Supervised learning.** Input-output pairs, iid assumptions, and labeled data define the standard baseline RL departs from. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN8443RGC2E1V4TD5SPRQFQY/research-114-L02-transcript.txt` `[3:49]` to `[5:27]`.
- **IID assumption.** Supervised learning assumes examples do not influence one another and come from the same distribution. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN8443RGC2E1V4TD5SPRQFQY/research-114-L02-transcript.txt` `[4:39]` to `[5:16]`.
- **Credit assignment.** RL has to work out which earlier decisions caused a later success or failure. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN8443RGC2E1V4TD5SPRQFQY/research-114-L02-transcript.txt` `[6:23]` to `[7:10]`.
- **State, action, reward.** The state is the input, the action is the output, and the reward is the scalar signal that scores the outcome. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN8443RGC2E1V4TD5SPRQFQY/research-114-L02-transcript.txt` `[7:18]` to `[8:29]`.
- **Policy.** A policy maps states to actions and is parameterized by Theta, typically neural-network weights. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN8443RGC2E1V4TD5SPRQFQY/research-114-L02-transcript.txt` `[8:51]` to `[9:01]`.
- **Cumulative reward.** Good RL policies maximize total reward over time, not just immediate reward. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN8443RGC2E1V4TD5SPRQFQY/research-114-L02-transcript.txt` `[9:03]` to `[9:19]`.
- **Global objectives.** Some problems only make sense if the agent optimizes the system-level result, not its own local reward. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN8443RGC2E1V4TD5SPRQFQY/research-114-L02-transcript.txt` `[14:13]` to `[15:12]`.
- **Learning on top of foundation models.** RL can fine-tune language and image models so they optimize human preferences or prompt alignment rather than just next-token or pixel statistics. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN8443RGC2E1V4TD5SPRQFQY/research-114-L02-transcript.txt` `[15:15]` to `[17:49]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights

- RL lets you optimize system-level behavior that may require local sacrifice, like slowing one car to improve throughput for everyone. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN8443RGC2E1V4TD5SPRQFQY/research-114-L02-transcript.txt` `[14:13]` to `[15:12]`.
