# L22 Transfer Learning, Meta-Learning, and Meta-RL

This lecture is about reusing experience. The punchline is simple: the thing you transfer matters, and the way you package prior experience often matters more than the optimizer itself.

## Summary
- Transfer learning in RL means using experience from source MDPs to improve a target MDP. The lecture defines the usual zero-shot, one-shot, few-shot, and many-shot vocabulary around that target-task setup. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part1.txt` `[0:00]` to `[7:57]`.
- The transfer target is not fixed. You can move Q-functions, policies, models, or features, and each one brings a different inductive bias. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part1.txt` `[3:38]` to `[5:10]`.
- Domain randomization broadens the source domain so the target is more likely to fall inside the trained envelope. The lecture uses hopper mass/friction examples and says this can help even with unmodeled factors. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part1.txt` `[31:33]` to `[36:53]`.
- Meta-learning is learning to learn. In RL, the lecture frames it as learning a function that maps experience in an MDP to policy parameters that work better on the next task. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part2.txt` `[0:00]` to `[9:39]`.
- The three meta-RL views are memory-based policies, gradient-based adaptation, and latent-task inference. The lecture treats RNN meta-learners, MAML-style updates, and PEARL as different parameterizations of the same adaptation problem. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part3.txt` `[0:00]` to `[11:35]` and `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part4.txt` `[0:00]` to `[7:22]` and `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part5.txt` `[0:00]` to `[11:17]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Source domain.** Where the prior experience comes from. Source domains are the tasks you already know how to solve. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part1.txt` `[5:26]` to `[6:10]`.
- **Target domain.** The new MDP you want to solve better, faster, or with less data. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part1.txt` `[6:05]` to `[7:55]`.
- **Zero-shot / one-shot / few-shot.** The lecture uses shot count to describe how much interaction is needed before the target task works. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part1.txt` `[7:00]` to `[7:55]`.
- **Domain randomization.** Training on a broad parameter distribution so the target looks less surprising. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part1.txt` `[31:33]` to `[36:53]`.
- **Multitask transfer.** Train several tasks together so shared structure becomes reusable. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part1.txt` `[37:26]` to `[39:15]`.
- **Contextual policy.** A policy that conditions on a task context, with the context either given or inferred. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part1.txt` `[40:58]` to `[43:33]` and `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part3.txt` `[3:27]` to `[4:36]`.
- **Meta-training / meta-testing.** Train on a family of tasks, then evaluate on held-out tasks from the same distribution. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part2.txt` `[3:06]` to `[4:28]`.
- **History-dependent meta-RL.** An RNN reads transitions across episodes, keeps hidden state, and uses that state as task context. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part3.txt` `[6:03]` to `[9:25]`.
- **Gradient-based meta-learning.** Choose initial parameters so one or a few gradient steps put you near a good task-specific solution. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part4.txt` `[0:00]` to `[7:22]`.
- **Latent context `Z`.** PEARL treats task identity as an inferred latent variable and uses variational inference plus posterior sampling. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part5.txt` `[3:42]` to `[11:17]`.
- **Meta overfitting.** A meta-learner can overfit the training-task distribution and fail to adapt outside it. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part5.txt` `[10:12]` to `[11:17]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- Inference: transfer learning is really a representation question. The hard part is deciding what object carries the prior, because Q-functions, policies, models, and features all transfer differently. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part1.txt` `[3:38]` to `[5:10]`.
- Inference: randomizing the source domain can buy robustness against variables you did not randomize directly, because physical effects are correlated. The lecture uses mass, friction, damping, and armature as the example. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part1.txt` `[34:37]` to `[35:50]`.
- Inference: meta-learning in RL is just a POMDP with a hidden task variable. The "learning" problem becomes inference over task identity from transitions and rewards. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part5.txt` `[0:00]` to `[4:56]`.
- Inference: MAML's real value is not only fast adaptation, but the fact that it can keep taking extra gradient steps at test time. The RNN version cannot do that because it only has a forward pass. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part4.txt` `[5:55]` to `[6:29]`.
- Inference: PEARL is basically RNN meta-RL with a stochastic encoder. That makes exploration explicit via posterior sampling, but also makes the method vulnerable to meta overfitting. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part5.txt` `[5:40]` to `[11:17]`.
- Inference: the lecture keeps collapsing apparently different methods into the same shape: infer enough context from experience, then act as if that context were known. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part3.txt` `[3:27]` to `[4:36]` and `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L22-part5.txt` `[6:39]` to `[11:17]`.
