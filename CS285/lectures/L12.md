# L12 Model-Based RL and the Algorithm Zoo

This lecture is the taxonomy slide in full: policy gradient, value-based, actor-critic, and model-based RL. The useful bit is that model-based methods are not one thing. They can plan, backpropagate, or manufacture extra training data.

## Summary
- The lecture compares the four big RL families: policy gradient, value-based, actor-critic, and model-based RL. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L12.transcript.txt` `[0:23]` to `[1:23]`.
- Policy-gradient methods compute return by summing rewards along a rollout and then take a gradient step on `theta`. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L12.transcript.txt` `[4:41]` to `[5:09]`.
- Value-based methods fit `V(s)` or `Q(s,a)` with a neural network and define the policy implicitly as `argmax Q`. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L12.transcript.txt` `[3:56]` to `[4:37]`.
- Actor-critic combines both worlds: estimate a value function in the green box, then use it to get a better policy-gradient update. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L12.transcript.txt` `[5:22]` to `[5:45]`.
- Model-based RL learns a transition model and can use it for planning, backpropagation through dynamics, or synthetic data generation for a model-free learner. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L12.transcript.txt` `[1:23]` to `[3:52]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Rollout.** Running the policy one step at a time; the lecture uses it as the simplest policy-gradient green box. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L12.transcript.txt` `[5:01]` to `[5:19]`.
- **Policy gradient.** Directly optimize `theta` with the gradient of expected reward. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L12.transcript.txt` `[4:41]` to `[5:09]`.
- **Value-based method.** Fit `Q(s,a)` or `V(s)` and let the policy fall out of an `argmax`. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L12.transcript.txt` `[3:56]` to `[4:37]`.
- **Actor-critic.** Use a critic to get a better gradient for the actor. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L12.transcript.txt` `[5:22]` to `[5:45]`.
- **Transition model.** Learn `p(s_{t+1} | s_t, a_t)` or a deterministic next-state predictor. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L12.transcript.txt` `[1:57]` to `[2:13]`.
- **Planning.** Use the learned model directly for control, as with chess search or robot trajectory optimization. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L12.transcript.txt` `[2:29]` to `[2:58]`.
- **Backprop through dynamics.** Differentiate reward through the learned model; the lecture calls out this path explicitly. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L12.transcript.txt` `[3:02]` to `[3:24]`.
- **Synthetic data.** A learned model can generate extra training data for a model-free algorithm. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L12.transcript.txt` `[3:43]` to `[3:52]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- Model-based RL here is a compute-allocation problem, not a philosophical category. The same learned model can buy planning, gradients, or extra data. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L12.transcript.txt` `[2:29]` to `[3:52]`.
