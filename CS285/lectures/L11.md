# L11 Q Functions and Value Functions

This lecture turns the RL objective into nested expectations, then compresses that mess into `Q_pi` and `V_pi`. Once you can estimate those, policy improvement becomes a mechanical move instead of a mystery.

## Summary
- The RL objective can be expanded into nested expectations over states, actions, and future rewards. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L11.transcript.txt` `[0:18]` to `[2:05]`.
- `Q_pi(s_t, a_t)` is the expected total reward from a state-action pair, while `V_pi(s_t)` is the expected total reward from a state alone. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L11.transcript.txt` `[2:21]` to `[5:35]`.
- If you know `Q_pi`, policy improvement is straightforward: choose the action with the largest value, and repeat. That is the basis of policy iteration and the route to Q-learning. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L11.transcript.txt` `[6:22]` to `[7:24]`.
- The `Q_pi(s,a) - V_pi(s)` gap tells you whether an action is better than average, which is the signal you need for gradient updates. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L11.transcript.txt` `[7:40]` to `[8:30]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Nested expectations.** The RL objective is written recursively as expectations inside expectations over future rewards. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L11.transcript.txt` `[0:21]` to `[2:05]`.
- **Q function.** `Q_pi(s_t, a_t)` is the expected sum of future rewards after taking action `a_t` in state `s_t`. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L11.transcript.txt` `[4:21]` to `[4:58]`.
- **Value function.** `V_pi(s_t)` is the expected value of starting in `s_t` and following the policy from there. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L11.transcript.txt` `[5:00]` to `[5:35]`.
- **Policy improvement.** A better policy can be built by taking the `argmax` of `Q_pi` at each state. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L11.transcript.txt` `[6:37]` to `[7:05]`.
- **Policy iteration.** The evaluate-improve loop is the source of later Q-learning derivations. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L11.transcript.txt` `[7:13]` to `[7:24]`.
- **Advantage signal.** If `Q_pi(s,a)` is larger than `V_pi(s)`, the action is above average under the current policy. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L11.transcript.txt` `[7:40]` to `[8:14]`.
- **Gradient update.** Actions with higher-than-average value get their probability pushed up. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L11.transcript.txt` `[8:17]` to `[8:30]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- `V_pi` is not a separate target. It is just the policy-average of `Q_pi`. That compression is what makes it useful. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L11.transcript.txt` `[5:17]` to `[5:35]`.
- Policy iteration is the hidden engine here. The lecture is quietly building Q-learning from an evaluation-improvement loop. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L11.transcript.txt` `[7:03]` to `[7:24]`.
- The advantage comparison turns evaluation into control. Once you can say "better than average," you can turn that into a gradient step. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L11.transcript.txt` `[7:40]` to `[8:30]`.
- The whole RL objective collapses to the value at the start state. That is a much tighter object than the original nested-expectation mess. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L11.transcript.txt` `[5:38]` to `[5:54]`.
