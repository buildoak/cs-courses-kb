# L10 Anatomy of Reinforcement Learning Algorithms

This lecture breaks RL into three boxes: generate samples, learn something about the policy, then improve the policy. The point is not the diagram itself. The point is that every major algorithm spends its compute in a different place.

## Summary
- All the RL algorithms in the course can be decomposed into sample generation, a green-box estimation step, and a blue-box policy-improvement step. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L10.transcript.txt` `[0:18]` to `[2:09]`.
- The policy-gradient version is the lightest possible instance: run the policy, sum the rewards, then use the return gradient to make good trajectories more likely. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L10.transcript.txt` `[2:12]` to `[3:36]`.
- The model-based version swaps in a learned dynamics model `f_phi`, then backpropagates through the model and reward to improve the policy. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L10.transcript.txt` `[3:41]` to `[4:58]`.
- Compute cost depends on the environment. Real-world sampling can be brutal; simulator sampling can be cheap enough that the bottleneck moves elsewhere. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L10.transcript.txt` `[5:12]` to `[6:21]`.
- Q-learning is the extreme case: the green box does most of the work and the blue box collapses to an arg max. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L10.transcript.txt` `[7:05]` to `[7:18]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Sample generation.** Run the policy in the environment and collect trajectories from the induced distribution. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L10.transcript.txt` `[0:26]` to `[1:14]`.
- **Green box.** This can mean reward summation, value estimation, or dynamics-model fitting depending on the algorithm. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L10.transcript.txt` `[1:19]` to `[1:55]` and `[2:54]` to `[4:25]`.
- **Blue box.** This is where the policy changes, either by a gradient step or by backpropagating through a learned model. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L10.transcript.txt` `[3:13]` to `[3:22]` and `[4:29]` to `[4:50]`.
- **Trajectory distribution.** The samples are not just random experiences; they come from the policy-induced rollout distribution. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L10.transcript.txt` `[0:48]` to `[1:14]`.
- **Learned dynamics model.** In model-based RL, the green box can be a neural net that predicts `s_{t+1}` from `s_t, a_t`. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L10.transcript.txt` `[3:49]` to `[4:12]`.
- **Cost asymmetry.** The expensive part can be sample collection, model fitting, or policy improvement, depending on where the computation sits. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L10.transcript.txt` `[5:20]` to `[7:05]`.
- **Q-learning placement.** This lecture already hints at the later split: some algorithms spend most of their budget in the evaluator, not the policy update. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L10.transcript.txt` `[7:08]` to `[7:18]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The real design choice is not "model-based vs model-free" as a slogan. It is where the expensive computation lives. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L10.transcript.txt` `[5:12]` to `[7:05]`.
- Model-based RL here is basically "RL by backprop": learn a differentiable world model, then push gradients through it. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L10.transcript.txt` `[3:41]` to `[4:58]`.
- Simulator speed changes the algorithmic priority list. The same method looks brilliant in MuJoCo and wasteful on a real robot. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L10.transcript.txt` `[5:31]` to `[6:21]`.
- Q-learning is not a separate philosophy here; it is one extreme point on the same compute-allocation spectrum. Source: `/private/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84ERGK2ABJR0NX1H48RA7A/research-117/L10.transcript.txt` `[7:05]` to `[7:18]`.
