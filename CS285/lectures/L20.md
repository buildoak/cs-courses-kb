# L20 Natural Policy Gradients and Trust Regions

The problem here is geometry, not just optimization. If one parameter crushes the policy distribution and another barely moves it, a single scalar learning rate is the wrong tool.

## Summary
- Ordinary policy-gradient ascent is poorly conditioned in parameter space because different parameters change the policy distribution by very different amounts. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN852J8508KMKHW3C24QRNB0/transcripts/L20.txt` `[0:24]` to `[5:09]`.
- The fix is to constrain policy change in distribution space using KL divergence rather than raw parameter distance. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN852J8508KMKHW3C24QRNB0/transcripts/L20.txt` `[5:12]` to `[7:39]`.
- A second-order expansion of the KL constraint yields the Fisher information matrix, and the resulting update is the natural gradient `F^-1 grad J`. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN852J8508KMKHW3C24QRNB0/transcripts/L20.txt` `[8:12]` to `[10:03]`.
- TRPO is the practical trust-region version of the same idea, with conjugate gradient used to get the step without explicit matrix inversion. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN852J8508KMKHW3C24QRNB0/transcripts/L20.txt` `[10:30]` to `[11:23]`.
- The lecture closes with examples and reading suggestions, then hands off to actor-critic. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN852J8508KMKHW3C24QRNB0/transcripts/L20.txt` `[11:31]` to `[13:28]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Poor conditioning.** The gradient points mostly along the parameter that changes the distribution the most, so naive ascent wastes steps. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN852J8508KMKHW3C24QRNB0/transcripts/L20.txt` `[2:19]` to `[3:18]`.
- **KL divergence.** A parameterization-independent way to measure how much the policy actually changed. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN852J8508KMKHW3C24QRNB0/transcripts/L20.txt` `[7:13]` to `[7:59]`.
- **Fisher information matrix.** The second-order approximation of the KL constraint, estimated from samples under the current policy. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN852J8508KMKHW3C24QRNB0/transcripts/L20.txt` `[8:12]` to `[9:02]`.
- **Natural gradient.** `F^-1 grad J`, i.e. preconditioned policy gradient in policy geometry. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN852J8508KMKHW3C24QRNB0/transcripts/L20.txt` `[9:35]` to `[10:24]`.
- **Trust region.** A step constraint in policy space rather than parameter space. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN852J8508KMKHW3C24QRNB0/transcripts/L20.txt` `[10:30]` to `[11:08]`.
- **Conjugate gradient.** The practical solver that makes the natural-gradient step affordable. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN852J8508KMKHW3C24QRNB0/transcripts/L20.txt` `[10:45]` to `[11:03]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- Natural gradient is just preconditioning with the metric induced by the policy distribution. The lecture does not sell it as a new objective because it is not one. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN852J8508KMKHW3C24QRNB0/transcripts/L20.txt` `[9:42]` to `[10:03]`.
- The KL constraint matters because it measures what changed in behavior, not what changed in parameter coordinates. That is the whole point. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN852J8508KMKHW3C24QRNB0/transcripts/L20.txt` `[7:13]` to `[7:59]`.
- The toy Gaussian example is not a toy for its own sake; it exposes the same scaling bug that shows up in real policies with many parameters. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN852J8508KMKHW3C24QRNB0/transcripts/L20.txt` `[0:33]` to `[3:18]`.
- TRPO is basically automatic step-size selection in policy space, which is why it feels more stable than raw gradient ascent. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN852J8508KMKHW3C24QRNB0/transcripts/L20.txt` `[10:30]` to `[11:23]`.
- The lecture frames the update as a geometry problem first, then a solver problem. That ordering is the useful bit. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN852J8508KMKHW3C24QRNB0/transcripts/L20.txt` `[5:12]` to `[10:24]`.
