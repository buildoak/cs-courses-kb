# L17 Reward-to-Go, Baselines, and Variance Minimization

This lecture is about making policy gradients usable. The math stays the same; the trick is to throw away terms that cannot matter and subtract terms that keep the estimator centered.

## Summary
- Causality lets you drop past rewards from the policy-gradient sum; only reward-to-go matters for the action at time `t`. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RXDMMSH1Q2P6XKTC9538/transcripts/L17.txt` `[0:24]` to `[4:25]`.
- Reward-to-go is the single-sample estimate of `Q-hat` and is the quantity you actually want to weight against `grad log pi`. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RXDMMSH1Q2P6XKTC9538/transcripts/L17.txt` `[4:27]` to `[5:19]`.
- A baseline can be subtracted without biasing the gradient, because the gradient of a constant integrates to zero. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RXDMMSH1Q2P6XKTC9538/transcripts/L17.txt` `[5:36]` to `[9:27]`.
- The average reward is a decent quick baseline, but the true variance-minimizing baseline depends on squared gradient magnitude and can differ per parameter. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RXDMMSH1Q2P6XKTC9538/transcripts/L17.txt` `[9:30]` to `[14:26]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Causality.** Actions at time `t` cannot affect rewards in the past, so past rewards can be dropped from the estimator. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RXDMMSH1Q2P6XKTC9538/transcripts/L17.txt` `[0:24]` to `[3:55]`.
- **Reward-to-go.** The reward sum from the current time step through the end of the trajectory. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RXDMMSH1Q2P6XKTC9538/transcripts/L17.txt` `[3:57]` to `[5:19]`.
- **Baseline.** A constant or learned reference value subtracted from reward to center the gradient signal. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RXDMMSH1Q2P6XKTC9538/transcripts/L17.txt` `[5:22]` to `[9:27]`.
- **Unbiased estimator.** Subtracting a baseline does not change the expected gradient, even though it changes finite-sample variance. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RXDMMSH1Q2P6XKTC9538/transcripts/L17.txt` `[7:26]` to `[9:27]`.
- **Optimal baseline.** The variance-minimizing baseline is the reward weighted by the squared gradient magnitude. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RXDMMSH1Q2P6XKTC9538/transcripts/L17.txt` `[10:11]` to `[14:26]`.
- **Parameter-wise weighting.** The optimal baseline can differ across parameters, so there is not necessarily one scalar "best" baseline. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RXDMMSH1Q2P6XKTC9538/transcripts/L17.txt` `[13:24]` to `[14:26]`.
