# L15 Policy Gradient Derivation

This lecture does the algebra cleanly. It starts with the trajectory objective, applies the score-function trick, and lands at REINFORCE.

## Summary
- Policy gradient is framed as direct differentiation of the RL objective with respect to policy parameters `theta`. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L15.transcript.txt` `[0:00]` to `[0:19]`.
- The objective is the expected sum of rewards under the trajectory distribution `p_theta(tau)`, with finite-horizon policy rollouts used to estimate it. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L15.transcript.txt` `[0:29]` to `[6:04]`.
- The score-function identity rewrites the gradient so the unknown environment dynamics cancel out. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L15.transcript.txt` `[8:14]` to `[11:10]`.
- After the cancellation, only policy log-probability terms remain, which means the gradient can be estimated from samples. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L15.transcript.txt` `[10:27]` to `[12:30]`.
- The basic algorithm is REINFORCE: sample trajectories, compute the gradient estimate, and take a policy update. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L15.transcript.txt` `[12:58]` to `[13:32]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Policy `pi_theta`.** A parameterized distribution over actions conditioned on state. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L15.transcript.txt` `[0:29]` to `[1:04]`.
- **Trajectory distribution.** The probability of a whole sequence of states and actions under a policy. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L15.transcript.txt` `[1:47]` to `[2:18]`.
- **RL objective `J(theta)`.** Expected return under that trajectory distribution. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L15.transcript.txt` `[4:23]` to `[6:04]`.
- **Score-function trick.** `grad p / p` becomes `grad log p`, making the derivative sampleable. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L15.transcript.txt` `[8:14]` to `[9:36]`.
- **Log trajectory factorization.** Only the policy terms depend on `theta`; initial-state and transition terms vanish under differentiation. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L15.transcript.txt` `[9:55]` to `[11:10]`.
- **REINFORCE update.** Multiply each `grad log pi_theta(a_t|s_t)` term by the trajectory reward and step the parameters. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L15.transcript.txt` `[11:18]` to `[13:32]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The derivation does not need the transition model. The environment physics disappear algebraically, not by approximation. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L15.transcript.txt` `[9:55]` to `[11:10]`.
- The estimator is feasible but noisy. Every log-prob term gets multiplied by the trajectory reward, which is why the plain version has high variance. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L15.transcript.txt` `[11:18]` to `[13:32]`.
- The orange/green/blue-box decomposition from the earlier lecture is not cosmetic. It maps exactly onto sample generation, reward aggregation, and parameter update. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L15.transcript.txt` `[12:34]` to `[13:32]`.
- The lecture stops where implementation becomes the real issue. The math is done before the algorithm is actually good enough to ship. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L15.transcript.txt` `[13:34]` to `[13:59]`.
- Finite-horizon rollout estimation is the practical bridge between objective definition and a usable gradient estimate. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L15.transcript.txt` `[4:50]` to `[6:04]`.
