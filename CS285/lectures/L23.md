# L23 Challenges and Open Problems in Deep RL

This lecture stops being about one algorithm and becomes a map of failure modes. The useful question is not "which RL method wins?" but "what assumption breaks first?"

## Key Concepts
- **Stability.** Small hyperparameter changes should not cause catastrophic performance swings. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L23-part1.txt` `[4:46]` to `[6:25]`.
- **Sample efficiency.** RL often needs many more interactions than supervised learning, so wall-clock and environment cost matter. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L23-part1.txt` `[13:45]` to `[16:53]`.
- **Generalization.** The real issue is not just getting reward on the training setting, but surviving changes in the environment. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L23-part1.txt` `[17:58]` to `[18:20]` and `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L23-part2.txt` `[13:49]` to `[14:35]`.
- **Supervision.** Reward, demonstrations, preferences, and goals are all different ways to tell the agent what counts as success. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L23-part1.txt` `[24:15]` to `[27:59]`.
- **Offline RL.** Data can define the world even when the agent cannot interact continuously. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L23-part1.txt` `[27:21]` to `[28:23]`.
- **Hard universes.** The lecture's "Mori's paradox" framing says the real world is hard because agents must adapt, survive, and recover from unexpected states. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L23-part2.txt` `[13:23]` to `[14:35]`.
- **Behavioral priors.** Prior experience can seed exploration so the agent tries useful behaviors instead of random thrashing. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L23-part2.txt` `[19:05]` to `[20:45]`.
- **Self-supervised RL pretraining.** Goal-conditioned RL can pretrain a robot on a proxy task, then fine-tune much faster on the real task. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L23-part2.txt` `[29:16]` to `[29:55]`.
- **Interactive agents.** RL can turn language models into better teachers by optimizing for short, targeted questions instead of verbose answers. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L23-part2.txt` `[30:00]` to `[33:02]`.
- **Problem formulation.** Sometimes the right move is not to force a benchmark into standard RL assumptions, but to redefine the task. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L23-part1.txt` `[27:21]` to `[28:23]` and `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L23-part2.txt` `[36:33]` to `[37:59]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- Inference: the lecture's real target is not algorithms; it is assumption auditing. Most failures come from mismatched supervision, not just from bad optimizers. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L23-part1.txt` `[24:15]` to `[28:23]`.
- Inference: less sample-efficient methods can still win if simulation is cheap and parallel, because the cost function is not only samples but also compute. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L23-part1.txt` `[16:03]` to `[16:49]`.
- Inference: deterministic policies are bad explorers. As RL training continues, policies can become too certain, which makes naive fine-tuning painfully slow. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L23-part1.txt` `[28:29]` to `[29:24]`.
- Inference: the lecture treats human preferences as a serious substitute for reward design, not a side quest. That is the same move later used in language-model alignment. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85325VS3JT08W8NQH03D49/cs285-l22-l23/transcripts/L23-part1.txt` `[25:44]` to `[27:19]`.
- Inference: the "RL as universal learning framework" argument is really a claim about decisions. The model can be trained on low-quality data; RL chooses the action that should follow. - Inference: the lecture's big-model story is not "bigger is better" by default. It is "use big data to learn what is possible, then use limited supervision to pick what is wanted."
