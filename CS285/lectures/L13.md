# L13 Why RL Algorithms Differ

This lecture is the map before the methods. The point is not to crown a winner. The point is that RL algorithms sit on different trade-off surfaces and make different bets about the world.

## Summary
- RL methods differ mainly on three axes: sample efficiency, stability/ease of use, and the assumptions they can tolerate. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L13.transcript.txt` `[0:00]` to `[2:23]`.
- Sample efficiency is largely the on-policy/off-policy split: off-policy methods reuse old samples, while on-policy methods throw samples away after each policy change. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L13.transcript.txt` `[2:25]` to `[3:19]`.
- The sample-efficiency ranking can flip in wall-clock time when simulation is cheap; then the expensive part becomes computation, not data collection. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L13.transcript.txt` `[4:12]` to `[5:00]`.
- Convergence is a weak spot. Many practical methods only have guarantees in tabular settings, and nonlinear value fitting can diverge. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L13.transcript.txt` `[5:03]` to `[7:23]`.
- The lecture closes by naming the environment assumptions that separate methods: full observability, episodic resets, and continuity/smoothness. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L13.transcript.txt` `[7:40]` to `[9:11]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Sample efficiency.** How many environment interactions you need before the policy becomes good. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L13.transcript.txt` `[2:29]` to `[2:45]`.
- **On-policy vs off-policy.** On-policy methods need fresh samples after each policy change; off-policy methods can learn from old data. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L13.transcript.txt` `[2:47]` to `[3:19]`.
- **Algorithm spectrum.** The lecture places evolutionary methods, policy gradient, actor-critic, Q-learning, and model-based RL on one spectrum of efficiency. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L13.transcript.txt` `[3:25]` to `[4:03]`.
- **Wall-clock cost.** Sample efficiency and runtime are not the same thing. Cheap simulation can make more sample-hungry methods look better in practice. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L13.transcript.txt` `[4:16]` to `[5:00]`.
- **Convergence.** RL often uses fixed-point methods or approximate updates rather than clean gradient descent, so convergence guarantees are rare. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L13.transcript.txt` `[5:03]` to `[6:55]`.
- **Model fitting vs reward.** A better model or a lower Bellman error does not automatically mean a better policy. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L13.transcript.txt` `[6:55]` to `[7:35]`.
- **Assumptions.** Full observability, episodic resets, and continuity/smoothness each favor different method families. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L13.transcript.txt` `[7:40]` to `[9:11]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The real design question is not "model-based or model-free" as a slogan. It is where the compute and sample budget goes. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L13.transcript.txt` `[3:25]` to `[5:00]`.
- Sample efficiency can be a lie if you ignore wall-clock time. A more sample-efficient algorithm can still be slower overall. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L13.transcript.txt` `[4:12]` to `[5:00]`.
- The convergence story is harsher than the textbooks suggest. Many guarantees survive only under tabular assumptions that do not survive neural nets. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L13.transcript.txt` `[5:59]` to `[7:23]`.
- Model-based RL can converge as a supervised learning problem and still fail as an RL solution because reward improvement is a separate question. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L13.transcript.txt` `[6:27]` to `[7:35]`.
- Environment assumptions are not just theory garnish. They are the gatekeepers for which RL family is even plausible. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84RDFTW32XFS91AMF6VYQC/research-118/L13.transcript.txt` `[7:40]` to `[9:11]`.
