# L09 Advanced Policy Gradients

This lecture takes policy gradients apart and rebuilds them as constrained policy improvement. The result is a trust-region view of policy updates and, eventually, natural gradient.

## Summary
- Policy gradients can be read as softened policy iteration: estimate advantage, then improve the policy a little instead of jumping straight to the greedy `argmax`. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84E8K6FVCSWAN2FK2QBBPJ/research-116/L09/01.txt` `[1:15]` to `[7:04]`.
- The improvement objective is rewritten with importance sampling so the new policy only appears in the importance weight; if the new policy stays close enough to the old one, the surrogate tracks the true return difference. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84E8K6FVCSWAN2FK2QBBPJ/research-116/L09/02.txt` `[0:01]` to `[6:00]`.
- To control that closeness, the lecture replaces a total-variation bound with a KL trust region because KL is tractable, differentiable, and easy to optimize. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84E8K6FVCSWAN2FK2QBBPJ/research-116/L09/03.txt` `[0:01]` to `[5:43]`.
- Practical constrained optimization can be done with a Lagrangian and dual gradient descent, and the same idea shows up in guided policy search and PPO. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84E8K6FVCSWAN2FK2QBBPJ/research-116/L09/03.txt` `[3:04]` to `[5:43]`.
- Natural gradient comes from a second-order approximation of the KL constraint: the Fisher information matrix reshapes the step so sensitive parameters get smaller updates and insensitive ones get larger ones. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84E8K6FVCSWAN2FK2QBBPJ/research-116/L09/04.txt` `[0:00]` to `[15:07]`.
- The lecture ends with the practical recipe: approximate `F^{-1} g` with conjugate gradient, then use TRPO/PPO as the scalable versions of the same trust-region idea. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN84E8K6FVCSWAN2FK2QBBPJ/research-116/L09/04.txt` `[15:21]` to `[21:05]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Advantage.** The relative quality of one action versus the policy's average behavior.
- **Policy iteration.** The conceptual template behind policy improvement.
- **Importance sampling.** The trick that lets the old policy data estimate the new policy objective.
- **Total variation divergence.** A distance measure used in the theoretical bound.
- **KL divergence.** The practical trust-region metric used instead of total variation.
- **Lagrangian.** The constrained optimization form used to enforce a KL budget.
- **Dual gradient descent.** Alternating updates on policy parameters and the multiplier.
- **Fisher information matrix.** The curvature object that appears in the KL quadratic approximation.
- **Natural gradient.** The `F^{-1} g` update direction.
- **Conjugate gradient.** The practical way to compute the natural-gradient step without inverting `F` explicitly.
- **TRPO / PPO.** The practical algorithms that operationalize the trust-region idea.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The real constraint is in policy-distribution space, not parameter space. A tiny step in `theta` can still produce a huge shift in behavior.
- Natural gradient is not a heuristic tweak. It is steepest ascent under the KL metric.
- The Fisher matrix is a sensitivity map: directions that change action probabilities a lot get squashed, and directions that barely move the policy get stretched.
- The lecture's one-dimensional Gaussian example shows why vanilla policy gradient can get stuck optimizing the wrong parameter because the gradient geometry is badly conditioned.
- The trust-region story is the same old policy-iteration loop, just rewritten so the update is safe enough to trust.

## Connections
- Inference: this lecture closes the loop from L07's policy-iteration view by turning policy improvement into a constrained optimization problem.
- Inference: the importance-sampled surrogate is the policy-gradient analogue of a bootstrap target. Both are local approximations that only work if the next iterate stays close enough.
- Inference: natural gradient, TRPO, and PPO are all different answers to the same geometry problem that plain Euclidean policy-gradient steps leave unsolved.
