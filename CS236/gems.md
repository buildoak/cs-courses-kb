# CS236 Gems

These are the recurring seams, not the lecture summaries. Citations point to the lecture markdown files under `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/`.

## Top Insights

- **The course is a ladder of escape hatches.** Each family shows up after the previous one hits a wall: chain-rule likelihood in L03, latent-variable marginalization in L05/L06, invertibility in L07/L08, adversarial density ratios in L09/L10, partition functions and scores in L11-L14, then metric choice in L15/L16. Sources: [L01](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L01.md#L9), [L03](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L03.md#L13), [L05](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L05.md#L7), [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L07.md#L13), [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L09.md#L6), [L11](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L11.md#L5), [L13](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L13.md#L6), [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L14.md#L6), [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L15.md#L6), [L16](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L16.md#L8).

- **The mismatch measure is the real design choice.** The family matters only after you decide what can be compared: forward KL for MLE, ELBO for VAEs, Jacobian correction for flows, JS/f-divergences/Wasserstein for GANs, Fisher divergence and NCE for EBMs, then compression, feature-space distance, and task performance at evaluation time. Sources: [L04](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L04.md#L13), [L05](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L05.md#L17), [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L10.md#L13), [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L10.md#L14), [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L12.md#L13), [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L12.md#L17), [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L15.md#L13), [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L15.md#L15), [L16](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L16.md#L12), [L16](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L16.md#L16).

- **Training and sampling are different currencies.** Autoregressive models buy exact likelihood by paying in sequential sampling; VAEs buy tractable learning by paying in posterior inference and reparameterization; flows buy exact density by paying in Jacobian engineering; GANs buy flexible generators by paying in unstable optimization; EBMs buy explicit densities by paying in MCMC; score models buy stable training by paying at sampling time. Sources: [L03](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L03.md#L18), [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L06.md#L15), [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L06.md#L17), [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L07.md#L14), [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L08.md#L17), [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L09.md#L19), [L11](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L11.md#L15), [L11](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L11.md#L16), [L13](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L13.md#L18), [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L14.md#L18).

- **Local geometry keeps replacing global integrals.** Masking, weight sharing, reparameterization, Jacobians, density-ratio critics, integration by parts, score fields, and noise schedules are all ways to trade one impossible global object for something local enough to optimize. Sources: [L03](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L03.md#L16), [L03](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L03.md#L18), [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L06.md#L15), [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L08.md#L15), [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L10.md#L14), [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L12.md#L14), [L13](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L13.md#L13), [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L14.md#L15).

## Surprising Connections

- **Autoregressive models, flows, and diffusion are three ordering tricks.** Autoregression orders variables, flows order space through invertible coordinate changes, and diffusion orders noise scales through annealing. Sources: [L03](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L03.md#L13), [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L07.md#L18), [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L08.md#L17), [L13](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L13.md#L19), [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L14.md#L14).

- **VAE encoders and BiGAN encoders solve the same latent-code problem with different divergences.** L05/L06 approximate the posterior with KL machinery; L10 adds an encoder by turning the adversarial game onto `(x, z)` pairs. Sources: [L05](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L05.md#L16), [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L06.md#L17), [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L10.md#L16).

- **KL, JS, f-divergence, Wasserstein, Fisher divergence, and NCE are one mismatch zoo.** The lecture sequence keeps changing the witness function, but the meta-problem stays the same: compare two distributions when direct density comparison is too expensive. Sources: [L04](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L04.md#L15), [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L09.md#L17), [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L10.md#L13), [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L10.md#L14), [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L12.md#L13), [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L12.md#L17).

- **Denoising score matching, Tweedie, and diffusion are the same field-following idea at different noise levels.** The score field is learned, smoothed, and then used as a sampler through Langevin or reverse-time SDEs. Sources: [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L12.md#L12), [L13](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L13.md#L15), [L13](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L13.md#L16), [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L14.md#L13), [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L14.md#L16).

- **Evaluation mirrors training.** The same course that starts with likelihood/compression ends by warning that no single metric covers compression, sample realism, representation quality, and downstream task success at once. Sources: [L04](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L04.md#L14), [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L15.md#L13), [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L15.md#L15), [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L15.md#L16), [L16](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L16.md#L28).

## Under-Appreciated Ideas

- **The bookkeeping layers are the real inventions.** Weight tying, masking, reparameterization, coupling layers, noise schedules, and metric design are the parts that make the headline model family usable. Sources: [L03](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L03.md#L16), [L03](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L03.md#L18), [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L06.md#L15), [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L08.md#L15), [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L14.md#L15), [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L15.md#L19).

- **The course keeps shifting cost instead of deleting it.** Flows move work into Jacobians, GANs move it into optimization, EBMs move it into normalization and MCMC, score models move it into sampling, and evaluation moves it into metric selection. Sources: [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L07.md#L22), [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L08.md#L26), [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L09.md#L27), [L11](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L11.md#L20), [L13](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L13.md#L25), [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L14.md#L24), [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L15.md#L25).

- **Likelihood is a narrow proxy, not a universal ranking.** It is useful for compression and density estimation, but the evaluation lectures keep showing how easily it diverges from human usefulness, feature quality, or downstream success. Sources: [L04](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L04.md#L14), [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L15.md#L13), [L16](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L16.md#L12), [L16](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L16.md#L23).

- **Discrete data is a fault line.** Reparameterization only works cleanly for continuous latents, flows want continuity, and diffusion leans on continuous-time SDEs. Sources: [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L06.md#L16), [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L07.md#L19), [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L14.md#L16).

- **Auxiliary models are doing the actual work.** Encoders, discriminators, critics, score networks, and learned noise models are not side machinery; they are the part that makes the training signal exist. Sources: [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L06.md#L17), [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L10.md#L16), [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L12.md#L17), [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L12.md#L19), [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L14.md#L13).

## Open Questions

- **Can one family get exact likelihood, fast sampling, and latent inference at once?** The course keeps exposing tradeoffs between these three, but never produces a model that cleanly owns all of them. Sources: [L05](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L05.md#L23), [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L07.md#L22), [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L08.md#L24), [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L10.md#L21), [L11](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L11.md#L20).

- **What should dominate when the use case is downstream task success instead of sample realism?** The evaluation lectures say the answer is task-dependent, but that leaves the practical weighting problem open. Sources: [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L15.md#L28), [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L15.md#L31), [L16](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L16.md#L28), [L16](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L16.md#L34).

- **How much sampling-time compute is acceptable in exchange for better training stability?** Diffusion pays at inference, flows pay in Jacobians, and EBMs pay in MCMC; the course never states a universal budget. Sources: [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L08.md#L26), [L11](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L11.md#L16), [L13](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L13.md#L18), [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L14.md#L18).

- **Is there a clean way to handle discrete data without the continuity assumptions that reparameterization and flows rely on?** The notes mark the boundary, but not the escape hatch. Sources: [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L06.md#L16), [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L07.md#L19), [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS236/lectures/L14.md#L16).
