# L14 Energy Based Models

The playlist title says Energy Based Models, but the transcript is mostly the bridge from score matching to diffusion. Lecture 14 turns the score field into a practical sampler with noise-conditional score networks, annealed Langevin dynamics, and the reverse-time SDE view. Source: transcript `[0:05]` to `[1:24:51]`.

## Summary
- The lecture keeps the score-based thread alive by training a single network across many noise levels, which makes score estimation much more stable than a GAN-style minimax game. Source: transcript `[0:35]` to `[2:24]`, `[50:07]` to `[52:20]`.
- Annealed Langevin dynamics is the practical sampler: start from a lot of noise, denoise one scale at a time, and keep the chain in the right basin. Source: transcript `[24:28]` to `[29:49]`, `[52:26]` to `[56:13]`.
- The noise schedule matters. The lecture uses the Fokker-Planck / overlap story to explain why the intermediate noise levels need to connect smoothly. Source: transcript `[38:51]` to `[44:39]`.
- The continuous-time view rewrites the same idea as a reverse-time stochastic differential equation, which you then solve numerically. Source: transcript `[1:10:14]` to `[1:16:47]`.
- The ODE version gives a normalizing-flow interpretation: score-based diffusion can be seen as an invertible continuous-time flow with pure noise as the latent vector. Source: transcript `[1:21:55]` to `[1:24:51]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Noise-conditional score network.** One network learns scores at multiple noise scales instead of fitting a separate model per scale. Source: transcript `[33:40]` to `[35:05]`.
- **Annealed Langevin dynamics.** Sample at high noise, then descend through a ladder of lower-noise distributions while adding a little randomness at each step. Source: transcript `[24:28]` to `[29:49]`, `[52:26]` to `[56:13]`.
- **Noise schedule overlap.** The denoising ladder only works if adjacent noise levels overlap enough for the chain to move smoothly between them. Source: transcript `[41:05]` to `[43:33]`.
- **Reverse-time SDE.** Once the score is accurate, sampling becomes solving a stochastic differential equation from noise to data. Source: transcript `[1:11:19]` to `[1:14:21]`.
- **Continuous-time flow.** The ODE perspective makes the model invertible and recovers a normalizing-flow interpretation without changing the learned score. Source: transcript `[1:22:07]` to `[1:24:51]`.
- **Inference-time compute.** The model is trained with a stable score-matching objective, then spends compute at sampling time. Source: transcript `[1:20:16]` to `[1:21:18]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- Inference: annealing is the thing that rescues Langevin. The lecture's main point is not "more steps." It is "the right sequence of steps."
- Inference: the SDE view does not replace the discrete story. It explains why the discrete story works as a numerical solver.
- Inference: the flow interpretation is not cosmetic. It means the same score model can be read as a likelihood-bearing invertible mapping.
- Inference: this lecture shifts the cost from training to inference on purpose. That tradeoff is why diffusion quality can beat faster but less stable generative families.

## Referenced Papers
- **Generative Modeling by Estimating Gradients of the Data Distribution.** Song & Ermon, 2019. The noise-conditional score-network paper that motivates annealed Langevin sampling. `https://arxiv.org/abs/1907.05600`
- **Score-Based Generative Modeling through Stochastic Differential Equations.** Song, Sohl-Dickstein, Kingma, Kumar, Ermon, Poole, 2021. The continuous-time diffusion / reverse-SDE formulation. `https://arxiv.org/abs/2011.13456`
- **Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models.** Ho, Jain, Abbeel, 2020. Related discrete-time diffusion framing; useful background for the SDE story. `https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.11239`
- **Deep Unsupervised Learning Using Nonequilibrium Thermodynamics.** Sohl-Dickstein, Weiss, Maheswaranathan, Ganguli, 2015. Historical diffusion precursor for the reverse-time viewpoint. `https://arxiv.org/abs/1503.03585`
