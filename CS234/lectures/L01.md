RL is framed here as decision-making under uncertainty: an automated agent learns through experience, then the course immediately grounds that idea in real successes and in the vocabulary it will use for the rest of the sequence.

## Key Concepts
- **RL.** An agent learns through direct experience to make good decisions under uncertainty. Source: `[0:28]` to `[0:46]`.
- **Bellman.** The field's roots go back to Richard Bellman and the 1950s decision-making literature. Source: `[1:47]` to `[2:03]`.
- **AlphaGo.** Reinforcement learning is presented as part of the system that pushed Go beyond human play. Source: `[3:22]` to `[3:53]`.
- **RLHF.** ChatGPT is described as imitation learning, preference modeling, then reinforcement learning from human feedback. Source: `[6:09]` to `[9:23]`.
- **Markov state.** A state is Markov when the present is enough; Atari uses the last four frames because motion needs history. Source: `[1:00:25]` to `[1:02:13]`.
- **Partial observability.** Observations can be incomplete or noisy, so the observation stream is not always the true state. Source: `[1:05:06]` to `[1:06:29]`.
- **Policy.** A policy maps state to action and can be deterministic or stochastic. Source: `[1:11:11]` to `[1:12:17]`.
- **Evaluation vs control.** Evaluation asks how good a fixed policy is; control asks for the best policy. Source: `[1:13:07]` to `[1:14:36]`.
- **MRP.** Once a policy is fixed, the MDP reduces to a Markov reward process. Source: `[1:16:01]` to `[1:16:27]`.
- **Discount factor.** Gamma weights future reward, and gamma < 1 keeps infinite-horizon returns bounded. Source: `[1:17:21]` to `[1:18:57]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The field's hard parts are coupled but distinct: delayed consequences create credit assignment problems, exploration censors information, and generalization explodes the state space. Source: `[17:46]` to `[22:09]`.
- State is a modeling choice, not a fact. The Atari four-frame example exists because one frame cannot recover velocity. Source: `[1:01:44]` to `[1:02:13]`.
- RLHF is a three-stage pipeline, not a single trick: supervised imitation, learned reward, then RL on that reward. Source: `[6:09]` to `[9:23]`.
- The agent's model can be wrong even when the world is fixed. The lecture separates the world dynamics from the learner's internal assumption. Source: `[1:10:26]` to `[1:11:06]`.
- The course is already pointing at the next layer: MDP notation is just the scaffold for planning, policy search, and later deep RL. Source: `[1:13:42]` to `[1:18:57]`.
