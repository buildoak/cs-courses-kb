# L06 Policy Search 2

The lecture turns policy gradients into something practical: prove baselines are safe, use critics to cut variance, then constrain policy updates so the optimizer does not blow past the part of policy space you can trust.

## Summary
- A baseline that depends only on state does not bias the gradient estimator, and that proof becomes the standard advantage-form policy update. Source: `[5:03]` to `[13:58]`.
- Actor-critic splits the work: the actor is the policy, the critic estimates value, and n-step targets trade off Monte Carlo variance against TD bias. Source: `[16:10]` to `[22:00]`.
- The lecture reframes policy improvement in terms of how different two policies are on visited states, using KL divergence rather than raw parameter distance. Source: `[55:25]` to `[1:02:04]`.
- PPO operationalizes that idea with either a KL penalty or clipped probability ratios so the policy can take multiple stable gradient steps. Source: `[1:03:00]` to `[1:09:07]`.
- The final message is pragmatic: PPO is popular because it is simple, stable, and fast in practice, not because it guarantees a better optimum. Source: `[1:17:16]` to `[1:19:10]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Baseline.** A state-only subtraction term that reduces variance without changing the expected gradient. Source: `[5:03]` to `[12:38]`.
- **Advantage.** `Q(s,a) - V(s)` or return minus baseline; it measures how much better an action was than average. Source: `[13:02]` to `[13:58]`.
- **Actor-critic.** The actor is the policy; the critic is the value estimator used to guide updates. Source: `[16:22]` to `[18:40]`.
- **Critic.** A learned `V` or `Q` estimate that replaces raw returns as the policy-gradient signal. Source: `[18:45]` to `[21:53]`.
- **n-step estimator.** A middle ground between one-step TD and full Monte Carlo returns. Source: `[20:15]` to `[22:00]`.
- **KL divergence.** A statewise distance between action distributions under two policies. Source: `[55:25]` to `[1:00:22]`.
- **Trust region.** A rule for keeping policy updates inside a region where the performance bound remains meaningful. Source: `[1:00:22]` to `[1:04:09]`.
- **PPO clipping.** Cap the policy ratio between `1 - epsilon` and `1 + epsilon` so objective terms cannot explode or vanish. Source: `[1:06:22]` to `[1:09:07]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The baseline proof is just algebra around normalization: because action probabilities sum to 1, the derivative of a state-only baseline term collapses to zero. Source: `[5:53]` to `[12:28]`.
- Actor-critic is really a variance-reduction mechanism for policy gradients. It is not a separate objective; it is a better estimator. Source: `[16:10]` to `[18:40]`.
- The right question is not "how far did theta move?" but "did the policy change meaningfully on the states we actually visit?" Source: `[55:25]` to `[1:01:05]`.
- PPO is intentionally pessimistic once the ratio drifts too far from 1. That is the safety valve, not a bug. Source: `[1:06:22]` to `[1:09:07]`.
- The lecture is clear that PPO's advantage is practical: more stable updates, less tuning pain, and better sample efficiency on the tasks shown. Source: `[1:17:16]` to `[1:19:10]`.
