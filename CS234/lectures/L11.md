# L11 Bandits, Regret, and UCB

This lecture turns exploration into the cleanest possible sequential decision problem: one decision, one reward, no state transition, just regret and the question of how to keep learning without getting stuck.

## Summary
- Bandits are the single-state version of sequential decision-making, so regret becomes the main metric and greedy becomes the obvious failure mode. Source: `[11:36]` to `[20:30]`.
- Epsilon-greedy does not escape the trap: both `epsilon = 0` and `epsilon > 0` can still produce linear regret. Source: `[26:19]` to `[33:11]`.
- The lecture introduces PAC as a different success criterion, then shows how optimism under uncertainty leads to UCB1 through Hoeffding bounds and a union bound. Source: `[28:47]` to `[55:05]`.
- The proof sketch is simple in spirit: once the confidence interval shrinks below the gap, the suboptimal arm stops getting pulled, which gives logarithmic regret. Source: `[55:31]` to `[1:05:24]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Bandit.** A sequential decision problem with arms, but no state transitions. Source: `[11:36]` to `[12:35]`.
- **Stationarity.** The reward distribution for each arm is assumed not to change over time. Source: `[16:03]` to `[16:30]`.
- **Regret.** Opportunity loss relative to the best arm. Source: `[19:39]` to `[21:11]`.
- **Gap.** The reward difference between the optimal arm and a suboptimal arm. Source: `[22:22]` to `[23:19]`.
- **Greedy.** Pick the current best estimate; easy to lock onto the wrong arm forever. Source: `[17:41]` to `[19:30]`.
- **Epsilon-greedy.** Random exploration helps, but the lecture shows it is still not enough for sublinear regret in this setup. Source: `[26:19]` to `[32:25]`.
- **Sublinear regret.** The target regime: bad decisions grow slower than linearly. Source: `[25:29]` to `[26:04]`.
- **PAC.** Count epsilon-suboptimal mistakes instead of every regret unit. Source: `[28:47]` to `[35:02]`.
- **Optimism under uncertainty.** Act as if uncertain arms might be good, then let evidence shrink the optimism. Source: `[37:08]` to `[39:05]`.
- **Hoeffding inequality.** Supplies the high-probability deviation bound that turns optimism into math. Source: `[40:37]` to `[44:10]`.
- **UCB1.** Pick the arm with the highest upper confidence bound. Source: `[44:40]` to `[47:11]`.
- **Union bound.** Make the confidence interval hold for every arm at every time step. Source: `[49:53]` to `[50:39]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- Greedy is not just "less exploratory" -- it can become unrecoverable after one bad early sample. Source: `[17:45]` to `[19:30]`.
- PAC and regret count different mistakes, so the same behavior can be good under one metric and bad under the other. Source: `[33:42]` to `[35:02]`.
- The UCB bonus is not arbitrary; it is the smallest thing the proof can justify from Hoeffding. Source: `[42:56]` to `[44:35]`.
- The lower-bound / pessimistic variant can still be valid confidence math and still be terrible policy. Source: `[1:06:11]` to `[1:07:58]`.
- The proof works because every bad arm can only survive until the confidence interval collapses below the gap. Source: `[57:13]` to `[1:04:52]`.

## Connections
