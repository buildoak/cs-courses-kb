# L06 Enterprise Workflows, TapeAgents, and Web Agents

This lecture splits the problem into three layers: enterprise workflows, agent internals, and browser interaction. The useful abstraction is the tape. Logs stop being telemetry and become training data.

## Summary
- Enterprise work is full of manual, low-volume tasks that are too messy for classical automation but ideal for agents. That is the motivation for the lecture. Source: `[0:24]` to `[3:03]` in `/Users/otonashi/thinking/pratchett-os/tmp/research-123/L06.transcript.txt`.
- LLM agents are distinguished from older narrow RL agents by their ability to use broad software knowledge, API tools, and web interfaces in ordinary work settings. Source: `[6:05]` to `[9:24]`.
- TapeAgents treats the tape as the core abstraction: thoughts, actions, and environment results are recorded as structured data that downstream agents can consume. Source: `[17:21]` to `[24:36]`.
- That tape-as-data view enables auditing, debugging, prompt optimization, and distillation from expensive parent agents into cheaper student agents. Source: `[24:01]` to `[31:38]`.
- Web agents are harder than they look. They need long-context understanding, planning, correct action execution, and robustness against prompt injection. Source: `[32:12]` to `[59:53]`.
- BrowserGym, AgentLab, WorkArena, and AgentXRay are the lecture's practical answer: standardize observations, actions, traces, and evaluation so the field can actually compare results over time. Source: `[50:26]` to `[55:30]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Enterprise workflow**: a real business process with manual steps, human approvals, and lots of edge cases. Source: `[1:18]` to `[3:03]`.
- **API agent**: an LLM agent that works through structured endpoints and tools. Source: `[8:29]` to `[9:24]`.
- **Web agent**: an agent that uses the browser like a human, through clicks, forms, and navigation. Source: `[8:56]` to `[9:24]`.
- **Short-term memory**: recent actions and observations used to continue the loop. Source: `[17:21]` to `[18:22]`.
- **Long-term memory**: synthesized tools or persistent artifacts the agent can reuse. Source: `[18:11]` to `[18:22]`.
- **Tape**: a structured log of thoughts, actions, and environment responses. Source: `[21:01]` to `[22:27]`.
- **Node**: an internal agent component that decides how to prompt or act. Source: `[22:29]` to `[23:35]`.
- **GREADTH**: the quality bundle for good conversational agents: grounded, responsive, accurate, disciplined, transparent, helpful. Source: `[27:31]` to `[28:19]`.
- **WorkArena**: a benchmark for workplace tasks on ServiceNow, from simple UI actions to realistic multi-step workflows. Source: `[41:50]` to `[49:17]`.
- **BrowserGym**: a unified evaluation platform with standardized observations, actions, and human evaluation support. Source: `[50:26]` to `[52:07]`.
- **AgentLab**: the toolkit for building, understanding, reproducing, and debugging web agents. Source: `[52:10]` to `[55:30]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The tape is not a passive log. It is a training object, an optimization object, and a debugging object all at once. Source: `[21:01]` to `[24:36]`.
- Cheap agents come from distillation. The lecture's main systems claim is that a large, expensive agent can generate tapes that train a much cheaper student. Source: `[29:04]` to `[31:38]`.
- Web-agent brittleness is not just model weakness. The environment itself is huge, dynamic, visually messy, and vulnerable to injection. Source: `[37:00]` to `[39:15]`, `[58:32]` to `[59:53]`.
- The hardest failures are planning failures. Agents get stuck on local pages, invent controls, or fail to recover when the obvious path is wrong. Source: `[49:25]` to `[50:21]`.
- Safety in browser agents is multimodal now. Attacks can arrive through HTML, visible text, or malicious browser extensions, not just through prompt text. Source: `[58:32]` to `[59:53]`.
