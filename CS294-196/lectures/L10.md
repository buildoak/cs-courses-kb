# L10 Open-Source and Science in the Era of Foundation Models

Percy Liang's core claim is blunt: access shapes research. The lecture moves from API access to open weights to open source, then shows how that difference changes what kind of agent work, simulation work, and model science you can actually do.

## Summary
- The lecture opens with the gap between capability growth and access collapse. Models got better fast; access to paper, weights, code, and data did not. Source: `[00:00:01]` to `[00:00:39]` in `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85DQWKFHXYEY6J85J11FWA/f3KKx9LWntQ.txt`.
- API access still lets you build useful black-box agents, but it keeps you inside the model's existing interfaces. That is enough for some agent work, not enough for deep scientific questions. Source: `[00:01:41]` to `[00:06:27]`.
- The lecture uses two agent families as examples: problem-solving agents built from black-box APIs, and simulation agents that need deeper access to model internals and data. Source: `[00:06:27]` to `[00:22:45]`.
- The Smallville / generative agents line of work shows why interviews matter: they compress rich human context into agent state better than a flat memory stream alone. Source: `[00:15:01]` to `[00:22:45]`.
- The close is a research-program argument, not a slogan: open-weight models, open-source code, and decentralized compute pooling are the path to answering deeper questions about AI. Source: `[00:44:22]` to `[00:52:48]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Access shapes research** is the lecture's headline thesis. What you can study depends on whether you have an API, weights, or the full training stack. Source: `[00:00:42]` to `[00:01:50]`.
- **API access** is black-box measurement. You can prompt and observe, but you cannot inspect internals. Source: `[00:01:41]` to `[00:02:24]`.
- **Open-weight access** gives deeper inspection plus derivatives like distillation and fine-tuning, but it still leaves you inside the original architecture. Source: `[00:02:22]` to `[00:03:03]`.
- **Open source** means code, training code, data-processing code, and data information, not just weights. Source: `[00:40:50]` to `[00:43:06]`.
- **Problem-solving agents** are the black-box agent class. They can do a lot with API-only access, including ML engineering and cybersecurity workflows. Source: `[00:06:27]` to `[00:14:57]`.
- **Simulation agents** need richer access because the point is to approximate human behavior, not just solve a task. Source: `[00:15:01]` to `[00:22:45]`.
- **Backpack language model** is the example of an architecture-level research result that only appears if you can touch the full stack. Source: `[00:44:22]` to `[00:46:40]`.
- **NAIRR and decentralized compute pooling** are the infrastructure answer to the compute bottleneck. Source: `[00:47:07]` to `[00:52:48]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- API-only research is not shallow by default, but it is fenced in. The interface becomes the ceiling for the questions you can ask. Source: `[00:01:41]` to `[00:03:03]`.
- Open-weight models are useful even for API research, because they reveal how fixed weights shape behavior and make fine-tuning, distillation, and merging possible. Source: `[00:38:32]` to `[00:40:18]`.
- The Smallville work is not just about simulation. It is about turning human richness into an agent representation that can survive long-horizon interaction. Source: `[00:15:01]` to `[00:22:45]`.
- Interviews are the compression trick. They extract the structure that a naive memory stream would never capture on its own. Source: `[00:19:23]` to `[00:22:45]`.
- Small-scale experiments are still worth doing if they are designed with a scaling hypothesis, not as dead-end demos. Source: `[00:47:07]` to `[00:52:48]`.
