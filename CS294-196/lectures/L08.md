# L08 Towards a Unified Framework of Neural and Symbolic Decision Making

This lecture is about making planning tractable by combining LLMs with symbolic solvers. The travel-planning example is the wedge; the real point is that traces, not just final answers, are useful supervision.

## Summary
- Yuandong Tian opens with the direct claim: neural models are flexible but weak on precise planning, while symbolic solvers are strong but do not understand natural language. The lecture is about combining them. Source: `[0:01]` to `[1:49]` in `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN85D7Z10NHHFK7XC3V3H2BS/L08-transcript-ts.txt`.
- The travel-planning pipeline turns a natural-language request into JSON, hands it to a MILP solver, and returns an executable itinerary. Human evaluations on the resulting system are positive. Source: `[12:05]` to `[18:55]`.
- The lecture then shifts from solver-in-the-loop to trace-supervised learning: SearchFormer predicts the search trace first, then the final plan, which makes maze and Sokoban training more data-efficient. Source: `[21:04]` to `[32:50]`.
- DualFormer randomizes trace dropping and learns to switch between fast and slow modes automatically, instead of hard-coding a single planning regime. Source: `[32:55]` to `[41:15]`.
- The last section is the most surprising: modular addition reveals a symbolic structure inside gradient descent, with Fourier features, semiring structure, and ring homomorphisms explaining the learned solutions. Source: `[54:17]` to `[1:09:42]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Symbolic solver** is the precise planning engine. In the talk, A* and MILP are the canonical examples. Source: `[10:30]` to `[13:27]`, `[21:44]` to `[22:14]`.
- **APEC constitution** is the dialogue policy for the travel agent: accurate, proactive, efficient, adaptive, and credible. Source: `[16:42]` to `[18:18]`.
- **Agent-as-judge** uses an agent to inspect another agent's steps, rather than relying only on a final-answer judge. Source: `[19:27]` to `[20:20]`.
- **SearchFormer** predicts search traces before solutions, turning solver structure into supervision. Source: `[21:03]` to `[32:50]`.
- **ILR** or improved length ratio measures how much shorter the learned trace is relative to the A* trace. Source: `[30:47]` to `[31:19]`.
- **DualFormer** learns both fast and slow reasoning modes from a mixed trace dataset. Source: `[32:55]` to `[41:15]`.
- **Semiring structure** is the algebraic frame used to explain why gradient descent lands on symbolic-looking solutions in modular addition. Source: `[1:04:44]` to `[1:06:03]`.
- **Ring homomorphism** is the bridge between the partial solutions and the global solution in the toy math problem. Source: `[1:04:14]` to `[1:05:49]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The solver trace is not just an explanation. It is training data, and the model gets better when the trace is part of the target. Source: `[23:05]` to `[24:18]`.
- Bootstrapping on the shortest successful traces can improve both optimality and trace length at the same time. Source: `[29:01]` to `[32:37]`.
- A model can discover a fast/slow reasoning mode from mixed supervision. The switch does not have to be scripted. Source: `[35:32]` to `[38:59]`.
- The symbolic vs neural debate is too coarse. The lecture shows a case where gradient descent itself lands in a symbolic algebraic structure. Source: `[55:07]` to `[1:09:21]`.
- The "detour" through a solver is not a workaround. It is a cleaner interface for planning tasks that are hard to specify directly in natural language. Source: `[12:26]` to `[13:34]`.
