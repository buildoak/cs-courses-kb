# L01 LLM Agents, Reasoning, and Planning

The opening lecture treats reasoning as the missing gear in agent systems. The point is not just better prompts. It is control over intermediate computation.

## Key Concepts
- **LLM agents** are LLMs used as brains for reasoning and planning, coupled to external environments, tools, retrieval, memory, and other agents. Source: `[1:24]` to `[3:48]`.
- **Reasoning** is explicit intermediate computation, not just a fluent final answer. Source: `[12:05]` to `[18:22]`.
- **Chain of thought** is the general name for multi-step reasoning traces that can be injected by prompting, training, or decoding. Source: `[20:41]` to `[23:53]`.
- **Least-to-most prompting** decomposes a hard task into smaller subproblems and solves them in order. Source: `[24:03]` to `[28:42]`.
- **Analogical reasoning** asks for related problems or strategies first, then reuses them on the target problem. Source: `[34:03]` to `[37:41]`.
- **Chain-of-thought without prompting** shows that the model can sometimes surface reasoning paths directly from decoding rather than from an explicit prompt. Source: `[38:38]` to `[43:03]`.
- **Self-consistency** estimates the final answer by sampling multiple reasoning paths and marginalizing over them. Source: `[44:21]` to `[50:05]`.
- **Oracle feedback** matters for self-correction. If the model is not told the answer is wrong, the correction loop can make things worse. Source: `[53:32]` to `[57:29]`.
- **Premise order sensitivity** shows that reasoning is brittle under sentence reordering and unrelated context. Source: `[51:37]` to `[1:01:39]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- Intermediate steps are not an explanation layer. They are a computational scaffold that changes what the model can solve. Source: `[19:29]` to `[23:53]`, `[30:36]` to `[31:30]`.
- The method boundary is less important than the latent structure. Training, fine-tuning, prompting, and decoding all exploit the same fact: models do better when reasoning is made explicit. Source: `[23:35]` to `[23:53]`, `[38:38]` to `[43:03]`.
- Self-consistency is not a vote on explanations. It is a marginalization trick over hidden reasoning paths. Source: `[44:21]` to `[50:05]`.
- The lecture's negative results are the real signal. Irrelevant context, self-correction, and reordered premises expose the fragility of current reasoning systems better than clean benchmark gains do. Source: `[51:37]` to `[1:01:39]`.
- "Simple" ideas are treated as the serious ones. The lecture keeps returning to decomposition, intermediate steps, and sampling because those ideas generalize better than task-specific tricks. Source: `[24:03]` to `[25:03]`, `[47:22]` to `[50:05]`.
