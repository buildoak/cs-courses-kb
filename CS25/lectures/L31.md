# L31 Transformers United: From Large Language Models to Large Multimodal Models

This lecture treats multimodality as the same scaling story with image, video, and audio baggage.

## Summary
- The talk organizes recent language-model history into three moments: BERT and GLM, GPT-3 scaling, and the ChatGPT alignment turn. The punchline is that task adaptation is cheap, but pretraining loss is what really tracks downstream ability. Source: `[3:34]` to `[13:01]`.
- The modern language-model stack is mostly refinement, not revolution: decoder-only, pre-layer norm, RoPE, GQA, GLU, and MoE are the defaults the speaker trusts. Source: `[13:21]` to `[16:00]`.
- Training large models is presented as an infrastructure problem. DeepSpeed, ZeRO, activation checkpointing, Megatron, tensor parallelism, pipeline parallelism, and context parallelism are the real machinery behind scale. Source: `[16:14]` to `[24:28]`.
- Alignment is mostly a post-training layer: SFT, RLHF, DPO, and better human or teacher data. The speaker treats full RLHF as optional rather than mandatory. Source: `[24:36]` to `[28:25]`.
- The second half moves into multimodality: BLIP-2, LLaVA, CogVLM, CogAgent, CogView, diffusion, video generation, and the prediction that future gains will come from better data generation and better video understanding. Source: `[34:39]` to `[1:07:25]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Three moments in LM history.** BERT/GLM framed the pretraining objective question, GPT-3 made scaling laws the center of gravity, and ChatGPT made task adaptation look cheap relative to pretraining quality. Source: `[3:34]` to `[13:01]`.
- **Pretraining loss as the real score.** The speaker claims downstream performance follows pretraining loss more than parameter count. If a smaller model reaches the same loss as a larger one, they look the same downstream. Source: `[11:30]` to `[12:46]`.
- **Decoder-only as the default.** The lecture treats encoder-decoder as too structured for most modern use cases, especially chat and generation, so decoder-only becomes the preferred backbone. Source: `[14:04]` to `[15:00]`.
- **Modern transformer plumbing.** Pre-layer norm, RoPE, group query attention, GLU, and mixture-of-experts are described as the practical core of current open models. Source: `[14:35]` to `[16:00]`.
- **Infra wins the war.** DeepSpeed ZeRO shards optimizer state and weights; activation checkpointing trades compute for memory; Megatron uses tensor and pipeline parallelism; context parallelism and ring attention make long context practical. Source: `[16:14]` to `[24:28]`.
- **Alignment is a separate phase.** SFT uses high-quality human or teacher data, RLHF uses reward models and PPO, and DPO replaces that with preference pairs and a simpler update rule. Source: `[24:36]` to `[28:25]`.
- **Data is the center.** The speaker explicitly says data cleaning, filtering, and synthesizing are the most important work in LLM companies. Source: `[28:35]` to `[30:15]`.
- **Data, algorithm, architecture are convertible.** A multi-hop QA problem can be solved with graph neural nets, MCTS, or just enough context plus chain-of-thought. The lecture treats these as different placements of the same effort. Source: `[31:19]` to `[34:08]`.
- **Vision-language bridging.** BLIP-2 uses a Q-former between image encoders and LLMs; LLaVA uses a simpler projection bridge; CogVLM adds vision experts so language behavior survives multimodal tuning. Source: `[34:56]` to `[39:04]`.
- **Autoregression vs diffusion.** Autoregressive image generation is elegant but slow and lossy; diffusion wins on speed and quality for images, then gets adapted into transformer-native systems like DiT and MM-DiT. Source: `[44:26]` to `[57:42]`.
- **The next frontier is video and data generation.** The lecture ends on video understanding, embodied AI, and new ways to turn compute into high-quality data, including code execution, MCTS, and RL. Source: `[57:45]` to `[1:07:25]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The strongest claim here is not "transformers won." It is "pretraining loss is the hidden variable." That makes a lot of apparent capability jumps look like measurement artifacts from better optimization. Source: `[11:30]` to `[12:46]`.
- The lecture keeps downgrading fancy structure. A trick is good if compute is scarce, but the same trick becomes a ceiling once scaling catches up. Source: `[13:31]` to `[16:00]`.
- Open-source progress is framed as an operations problem as much as a modeling problem. The important release is not just weights; it is the data pipeline, filters, evaluation code, and transparency tooling. Source: `[41:32]` to `[45:36]`.
- Universal multimodal modeling sounds beautiful, but the speaker admits it loses to specialization. Autoregressive tokenization is cleaner on paper, while diffusion and dedicated VLMs win on actual task quality. Source: `[47:09]` to `[49:51]`.
- The lecture treats video and speech as underinvested because they need more compute and better annotation pipelines than text did. That is an explicit budget argument, not a modality argument. Source: `[1:04:33]` to `[1:07:25]`.
- The practical frontier is not just more model parameters. It is better ways to manufacture supervised signal, especially through code execution, search, and synthetic data generation. Source: `[1:06:42]` to `[1:07:25]`.

## Referenced Papers
- **GLM** - unifies BERT-style masking and autoregressive modeling into one pretraining scheme. Source: `[5:39]` to `[6:53]`.
- **InstructGPT** - shows how alignment can dramatically improve human preference over the raw pretrained model. Source: `[10:43]` to `[11:13]`.
- **BLIP-2** - uses a Q-former to bridge a frozen image encoder and an LLM. Source: `[34:56]` to `[36:15]`.
- **LLaVA** - uses a simpler projection bridge for vision-language input. Source: `[36:18]` to `[36:53]`.
- **CogVLM** - preserves language behavior while adding image understanding through vision experts. Source: `[36:57]` to `[39:04]`.
- **CogAgent** - targets high-resolution web-screen interaction with a lighter cross-attention bridge. Source: `[39:28]` to `[41:21]`.
- **CogView** - text-to-image generation with discrete image tokens and GPT-style autoregression. Source: `[44:47]` to `[46:33]`.
- **iGPT** - early autoregressive image modeling at the pixel level. Source: `[45:21]` to `[45:35]`.
- **DALL-E** - a concurrent discrete-token text-to-image line the speaker cites as the same basic idea. Source: `[46:33]` to `[46:42]`.
- **Parti** - another major text-to-image model based on the same discrete-token idea. Source: `[46:39]` to `[46:42]`.
- **DDPM** - the canonical diffusion formulation the speaker uses as the baseline. Source: `[50:16]` to `[50:26]`.
- **DiT** - transformer diffusion with AdaLN conditioning. Source: `[55:28]` to `[56:24]`.
- **Stable Diffusion 3 / MM-DiT** - a modern diffusion stack that uses vision/text experts and recaptioning from CogVLM. Source: `[57:05]` to `[57:42]`.
