# L40 Transformers United: Diffusion Models, But With Fewer Awkward Priors

This is a simplification talk. The real subject is how to delete the UNet without losing quality.

## Summary
- The talk starts with diffusion and flow matching as two ways to denoise a latent sample back into an image or video. The core move is still the same: learn the reverse path from noise to data. Source: `0:04-10:13`.
- Raw pixel modeling is a dead end for large media because the token count explodes. The answer is to compress video first, then model the compressed representation. Source: `10:52-18:32`.
- The UNet is treated as a historical artifact: useful, but too large and too bespoke to scale cleanly. Transformer backbones are easier to unify with the rest of the stack. Source: `14:49-20:03`.
- Stable Diffusion 3’s MMDiT is the current heavyweight design, but the talk spends a lot of time on simplification: parameter sharing, concatenated token spaces, and fewer special cases. Source: `50:58-57:31`.
- The future bucket is blunt: control, in-context generation, multimodal inputs, reasoning, and safety mitigation are all still open. Source: `1:05:07-1:14:23`.

## Key Concepts
- **Diffusion.** Iterative denoising from noise to data.
- **Flow matching.** A simpler path-learning variant that predicts velocity rather than only noise.
- **Temporal autoencoder.** The compression layer that makes video tokens tractable.
- **Latent space.** The model works on compressed representations, not RGB directly.
- **UNet.** The historical image-generation backbone the speaker wants to retire.
- **DiT / MMDiT.** Transformer-native diffusion backbones.
- **AdaLN.** Modulation via adaptive layer norm.
- **QK normalization.** One of the stabilizers that makes the transformer variant train.
- **ControlNet / FluxControl.** Ways to inject structure and spatial constraints.
- **Transfusion.** The idea of mixing autoregressive and diffusion-style generation.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The big win is not "better image generation." It is architectural unification. Once media is tokenized, the transformer stack starts to look like the language stack.
- Text encoder strength is not the main variable. The talk repeatedly shows that alignment with media space matters more than using the fanciest language model.
- More structure is not always better. The simplified concatenated designs sometimes train better than the more elaborate split-modality variants.
- Safety is not an add-on. The speaker folds safety mitigation into the same future bucket as training and scaling.
- Reasoning is the next obvious missing piece. The model can already generate, but it still cannot reliably self-correct obvious failures.
