# L44 Multimodal World Models for Drug Discovery

The model is a simulator when it can answer counterfactuals, not just label slides.

## Summary
- The talk defines a world model as a system that can simulate patient biology and answer counterfactual questions.
- The data stack is deliberately multimodal and spatially aligned: H&E, 16-plex protein immunofluorescence, 1,000-plex spatial transcriptomics, and whole-exome sequencing.
- A masked-autoencoder backbone with aggressive masking forces the model to infer cell state from sparse context and translate between modalities.
- The same machinery is used for counterfactual drug simulation, tumor-marker prediction, patient embedding, and genetic clustering.
- The closing move is scale plus integration: more modalities, more organs, more patients, and interpretability on top.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The useful move is not concatenation. It is learning one modality well enough to predict another.
- High masking ratios are a feature: the model learns to stay useful with almost no direct evidence.
- Synthetic neighborhoods are more valuable than generic synthetic data because they preserve real spatial context.
- H&E can recover patient genetics in latent space because paired multimodal training forces biology into the representation.
- The main challenge is not just prediction. It is choosing the right intervention and the right scale of simulation.
- Once the model can run what-ifs, it stops being a classifier and starts being a search engine for biology hypotheses.
