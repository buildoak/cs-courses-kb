# L11 Transformers United: From Seq2Seq to GPT

This lecture starts with the transformer ancestry, then turns into a `nanoGPT` walk-through and ends by arguing that GPT behaves like a general-purpose computer. Source: `[15:09]` to `[22:31]`, `[31:01]` to `[39:16]`, and `[55:43]` to `[1:00:31]`.

## Summary
- The talk traces the line from early neural language modeling to seq2seq translation, then to attention, then to the 2017 transformer architecture. Source: `[15:09]` to `[22:31]`.
- The `nanoGPT` walk-through shows a decoder-only transformer as token embeddings, positional embeddings, transformer blocks, causal self-attention, and an LM head. Source: `[31:01]` to `[37:04]`.
- Causal masking is the core implementation detail that prevents the model from peeking ahead, while BERT and encoder-decoder models change the masking and attention pattern. Source: `[37:08]` to `[45:32]`.
- The same recipe extends to images, audio, reinforcement learning trajectories, and molecules by turning them into token sequences. Source: `[52:57]` to `[55:37]`.
- GPT-3 makes the case for in-context learning: the model looks like it is running a learned inner-loop optimizer, which is why the lecture calls it a general-purpose computer. Source: `[55:43]` to `[1:00:31]` and `[1:03:00]` to `[1:05:37]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Neural language modeling.** The lecture starts with the 2003 move from classic NLP to neural next-word prediction. Source: `[15:09]` to `[15:39]`.
- **Seq2seq.** The 2014 encoder-decoder setup solves variable-length translation. Source: `[15:43]` to `[16:35]`.
- **Encoder bottleneck.** Packing an entire source sentence into one vector is the pressure point that attention fixes. Source: `[16:37]` to `[17:02]`.
- **Soft attention.** The decoder learns where to look in the source sentence by computing a weighted sum over encoder states. Source: `[17:03]` to `[18:25]`.
- **Attention as the core idea.** `Attention Is All You Need` deletes the RNN scaffolding and keeps attention plus a few stabilizing ingredients. Source: `[20:11]` to `[22:31]`.
- **Multi-head attention.** Parallel heads let the model carry several relation types at once. Source: `[21:24]` to `[21:35]`.
- **Communication vs computation.** Attention passes messages; the MLP transforms what each node already holds. Source: `[22:47]` to `[23:07]` and `[38:31]` to `[38:49]`.
- **Decoder-only GPT.** A language model uses only the decoder side, because there is no external source sentence to condition on. Source: `[31:30]` to `[35:02]`.
- **Tokenization and context length.** Text is converted to integer tokens, sliced into blocks, and trained in batches. Source: `[32:00]` to `[34:42]`.
- **Positional encoding.** Attention sees sets, so order has to be injected as trainable position vectors. Source: `[35:38]` to `[36:16]`.
- **Causal masking.** The model must not access future tokens when predicting the next one. Source: `[38:55]` to `[39:16]` and `[40:38]` to `[41:03]`.
- **BERT and encoder-decoder.** Encoder-only models remove the mask, while encoder-decoder models add cross-attention back in. Source: `[44:49]` to `[45:32]`.
- **Multimodal tokenization.** Images, spectrograms, action trajectories, and molecules can all be treated as sequences of tokens. Source: `[52:57]` to `[55:37]`.
- **In-context learning.** GPT-3 improves as examples accumulate in the prompt, without gradient updates. Source: `[55:43]` to `[57:18]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The transformer won by convergence. One architecture became the default because it could be copy-pasted across tasks with only minor data-shaping changes. Source: `[13:57]` to `[15:06]`.
- Attention is not the whole model. Residual paths, layer norms, and the MLP stack are part of why the architecture trains so well. Source: `[21:17]` to `[22:31]`.
- The transformer is a shallow, wide graph, which is exactly what GPUs and backprop want. Source: `[58:31]` to `[1:04:08]`.
- GPT-style in-context learning looks like a learned optimizer inside the activations, even though the outer loop is still ordinary gradient descent. Source: `[56:37]` to `[57:57]`.
- The lecture's computer metaphor is not decorative. Prompt in, program runs, document completes. Source: `[59:55]` to `[1:00:12]`.

## Connections
- To Bengio et al. 2003: the first neural language-model paper the lecture names as a starting point. Source: `[15:09]` to `[15:39]`.
- To seq2seq and Bahdanau attention: the soft-search fix for the encoder bottleneck becomes the seed of modern transformer attention. Source: `[16:37]` to `[19:24]`.
- To BERT and GPT: encoder-only and decoder-only models are just masking and attention variants on the same block. Source: `[44:49]` to `[45:32]`.
- To Whisper, Decision Transformer, and AlphaFold: the same sequence machinery transfers to speech, RL, and biology. Source: `[52:57]` to `[54:21]`.
- To efficient hardware design: the lecture keeps comparing transformers to neural GPU-style thinking and GPU-friendly graphs. Source: `[58:41]` to `[1:04:08]`.
