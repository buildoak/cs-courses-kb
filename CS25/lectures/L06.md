# L06 Perceiver IO: Cross-Attention to a Latent Bottleneck

This lecture keeps the transformer's generality but moves the expensive part into a small latent space.

## Summary
- Standard transformers are general but expensive: attention is quadratic in input size, so the talk starts by asking how to preserve generality without paying that full cost. Source: `[3:03]` to `[5:57]`.
- Perceiver replaces input self-attention with cross-attention into a learned latent array, which makes the input side linear and lets the model do quadratic work only in latent space. Source: `[10:43]` to `[17:25]`.
- The latent array is a learned set of inducing points or queries, and the model can stack self-attention only after the input has been compressed. Source: `[11:07]` to `[17:25]`.
- On images, multimodal arrays, token-free language, and optical flow, the architecture is competitive with specialized systems when the setup is right. Source: `[26:23]` to `[28:31]`, `[31:02]` to `[36:08]`, `[46:01]` to `[51:13]`.
- Perceiver IO extends the same cross-attention idea to outputs, which makes dense decoding, masked language modeling, multitask classification, and other structured outputs possible. Source: `[40:04]` to `[47:10]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Cross-attention bottleneck.** Replace full self-attention over the input with attention from a smaller learned query set. Source: `[10:43]` to `[12:15]`.
- **Latents.** Learned query vectors that act like an initial state or inducing points. Source: `[11:07]` to `[11:22]`.
- **Latent self-attention.** Once the input is compressed, the model can do deep transformer-style processing in latent space. Source: `[16:20]` to `[17:25]`.
- **Weight sharing in depth.** Reusing the same cross/self-attention blocks makes the stack look RNN-like and helps on small data. Source: `[18:15]` to `[18:33]`.
- **2D Fourier features.** Image positions are encoded with dense sinusoidal features; learned positions are also explored. Source: `[18:54]` to `[20:30]`.
- **Modality-specific position encodings.** Different modalities get their own learned tags before concatenation. Source: `[31:02]` to `[32:05]`.
- **Perceiver IO.** Add output queries so the same architecture can decode arbitrary output structures. Source: `[40:04]` to `[42:44]`.
- **Token-free language modeling.** Mask bytes or characters directly instead of relying on tokenization. Source: `[43:21]` to `[46:00]`.
- **Optical flow.** Dense per-pixel correspondence becomes a strong test of whether the latent bottleneck preserves fine detail. Source: `[48:02]` to `[51:13]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The latent bottleneck is not just compression. It is a way to move the quadratic cost into a controllable smaller space while keeping a general interface for many modalities. Source: `[11:47]` to `[12:15]`.
- Learned positional encodings are the "purest" version of the idea because they expose less structural prior, but 2D Fourier features still work better in practice on images. Source: `[27:57]` to `[29:19]`, `[35:04]` to `[36:08]`.
- Tokenization is convenient, not sacred. It wastes capacity on rare words, is brittle to whitespace, and blocks clean multilingual transfer. Source: `[43:21]` to `[45:16]`.
- Perceiver IO shows that a general bottleneck architecture can still preserve fine spatial detail, including optical flow and small water droplets. Source: `[50:10]` to `[52:05]`.
- The architecture is general enough that the same mechanism can serve as encoder, processor, and decoder. That is the real trick. Source: `[40:37]` to `[42:44]`.

## Connections
