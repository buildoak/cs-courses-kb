# L17 Biomedical Transformers: Clinical QA, Proteins, and Genomics

This lecture says biomedicine is a transformer problem because the data are sequences, the tasks are language-heavy, and the real bottleneck is evaluation. Scale helps, but the important work is benchmarking, alignment, and long-range modeling. Source: `[6:31]` to `[9:30]`, `[14:18]` to `[18:22]`, `[48:00]` to `[1:04:50]`.

## Summary
- Medical AI is mostly narrow and task-specific today. The lecture wants systems that can talk to doctors, patients, and researchers without collapsing into a single canned workflow. Source: `[7:07]` to `[8:13]`.
- MultiMedQA unifies seven medical QA datasets across professional, research, and consumer settings, then adds human evaluation from clinicians and lay users. Source: `[10:12]` to `[14:07]`.
- FLAN-PaLM plus prompting and self-consistency gets large gains on short-form medical QA, including a more than 17 percent jump on USMLE MedQA. Source: `[15:59]` to `[18:12]`.
- Med-PaLM uses prompt tuning with a small set of clinician examples to close the gap to expert answers and produce better long-form responses. Source: `[22:32]` to `[32:11]`.
- The protein and genomics sections show why architecture still matters: long-context transformers, sequence annotation, polished reads, and long-range enhancer modeling all need better sequence handling, not just more scale. Source: `[48:00]` to `[1:04:50]`.

## Key Concepts
- **MultiMedQA.** A benchmark that mixes USMLE-style questions, PubMed questions, and consumer health questions into one evaluation suite. Source: `[10:12]` to `[10:58]`.
- **Human evaluation.** Clinicians score factuality, reasoning, harm, and bias; lay users score helpfulness and actionability. Automated metrics are not enough. Source: `[13:19]` to `[14:07]`.
- **Self-consistency and selective deferral.** The model generates multiple decodes, uses the vote distribution as uncertainty, and defers when confidence is low. Source: `[17:46]` to `[20:25]`.
- **Instruction prompt tuning.** Freeze the base model, learn a small prompt vector set, and update in hours instead of days. Source: `[23:01]` to `[24:00]`.
- **Med-PaLM.** FLAN-PaLM plus clinician instructions, exemplars, and prompt tuning to align the model to the medical domain. Source: `[24:12]` to `[24:49]`.
- **Protein sequence modeling.** Proteins are long sequences with global interactions, so the talk argues that sparsity priors can be the wrong bias. Source: `[48:11]` to `[49:56]`.
- **ProtNLM.** The protein annotation system that turns amino-acid sequences into textual descriptions and queryable functions. Source: `[50:49]` to `[53:24]`.
- **DeepConsensus.** The sequence-polishing model for PacBio reads. The talk frames it in the same sequencing stack as DeepVariant. Source: `[53:25]` to `[58:18]`.
- **Enformer.** The genomics model that uses CNN layers plus transformers and relative position encoding to model long-range enhancer-promoter interactions. Source: `[58:44]` to `[1:03:56]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The medical benchmark is not "nice to have" machinery. The lecture treats evaluation as the missing product layer that decides whether the model is useful or nonsense. Source: `[8:37]` to `[9:30]`, `[21:22]` to `[22:31]`.
- The model learning to say "I do not know" is a clinical feature, not a bug. Deferral is part of safe use. Source: `[18:00]` to `[20:25]`.
- General-purpose LLMs already encode a surprising amount of clinical knowledge. The real issue is alignment to domain-specific safety requirements. Source: `[17:19]` to `[18:22]`, `[34:37]` to `[35:26]`.
- The lecture quietly changes the story on long-form medical answers: physicians are precise but hard to read, and LLMs may be useful as translators of doctor-speak into lay language. Source: `[31:37]` to `[32:11]`.
- In proteins, the right inductive bias is not sparse attention for its own sake. Biology often wants global interaction modeling, so low-rank long-context attention can beat more fashionable sparse variants. Source: `[48:24]` to `[50:16]`.
- In genomics, the distance that matters is not linear sequence distance. Enhancers can be far away in 1D but close in 3D, so relative position encoding and long context are the real weapon. Source: `[1:00:00]` to `[1:03:56]`.
