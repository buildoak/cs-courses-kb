# L29 Demystifying Mixtral of Experts

This is the MoE lecture in two halves: what sparse routing buys you, and what it quietly costs.

## Summary
- Mistral 7B starts with a strong dense baseline: group-query attention, sliding-window attention, and a compact transformer stack. Source: `[2:04]` to `[6:41]`.
- Mixtral / Mistral 8x7B swaps the MLPs for sparse MoE routing with top-2 experts, then gets a large jump on knowledge-heavy tasks. Source: `[7:06]` to `[12:28]`.
- The headline win is performance per active parameter, not free lunch compute: inference is cheaper per token, but routing adds memory and communication cost. Source: `[12:35]` to `[16:40]`.
- The talk spends a lot of time killing myths: 8 experts per layer, 46B total params, and no simple "one expert = one domain" mapping. Source: `[14:16]` to `[29:29]`.
- The most interesting result is interpretability, but the gating patterns still look more like distributed features than clean semantic specialists. Source: `[19:14]` to `[29:29]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Group-query attention.** A middle ground between multi-head and multi-query attention, used in Mistral 7B. Source: `[2:17]` to `[2:45]`.
- **Sliding-window attention.** Lower layers attend to a short recent span, then deeper layers propagate information forward. Source: `[2:57]` to `[3:21]`.
- **Mixture of experts.** A router sends each token to a small subset of experts instead of all dense weights. Source: `[7:50]` to `[9:19]`.
- **Top-2 gating.** The router assigns gating weights, picks the top two experts, and mixes their outputs. Source: `[8:23]` to `[9:19]`.
- **SwiGLU experts.** Each expert applies a SwiGLU transform before the gated outputs are combined. Source: `[9:11]` to `[9:19]`.
- **Active parameters vs total parameters.** Mixtral uses about 12.9B active parameters per token even though the full model is much larger. Source: `[12:10]` to `[12:28]`, `[15:14]` to `[15:37]`.
- **MoE-fying MLPs.** The lecture frames MLP layers as knowledge storage, so widening them with experts should increase memorized capability. Source: `[10:28]` to `[12:28]`.
- **MoE-fying attention.** Replacing Q/K/V with switch layers is presented as an open research question, but bf16 stability is a problem. Source: `[12:35]` to `[14:03]`.
- **Expert parallelism.** The systems side of MoE is about data, model, and expert parallelism, plus communication costs. Source: `[7:25]` to `[7:46]`, `[40:40]` to `[41:37]`.
- **Compression.** MoE sparsification is not the same as dense model pruning; the lecture treats extreme compression as unresolved. Source: `[17:37]` to `[19:04]`.
- **Interpretability.** Sparse gates expose which expert was chosen, which makes analysis possible but still messy. Source: `[19:14]` to `[29:29]`.
- **Load balancing.** The router should avoid overloading one expert while others idle. Source: `[16:43]` to `[17:34]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The main gain from MoE is not "more compute" but "more stored knowledge per active token." Source: `[10:39]` to `[12:28]`.
- The expert-count myth matters because the real unit is per-layer experts, not one global pool. The network has 32 x 8 experts, not just 8. Source: `[14:16]` to `[15:07]`.
- Sparse routing can still concentrate load in ugly ways. The "expert 3 does all the work" story is a warning that gating is not automatically semantically clean. Source: `[27:18]` to `[28:32]`.
- Domain labels are too coarse. The talk suggests experts probably learn lower-level features, not neat human categories like "medical" or "coding." Source: `[26:21]` to `[29:07]`.
- MoE is awkward on edge devices because you still need to load all experts into memory, even if only a subset is active at inference time. Source: `[31:38]` to `[32:14]`.
- Communication cost grows with token routing across GPUs and nodes, so MoE is as much a systems problem as a modeling trick. Source: `[40:40]` to `[41:37]`.
- Mixture of depth is the clean comparison point: it changes how many parameters a token sees, while Mixtral changes which experts those parameters come from. Source: `[38:45]` to `[39:46]`.
