# L43 Biomedical Transformers: Clinical Knowledge, Evaluation, and Med-PaLM

Medical AI is a sequence problem until it turns into a safety problem.

## Summary
- Transformers fit biomedicine because clinical notes, EMRs, proteins, and genomes all behave like sequences.
- The real bottleneck is not just scale; it is evaluation, alignment, and workflow fit.
- MultiMedQA and clinician/lay-user review replace single accuracy numbers with a more honest benchmark.
- FLAN-PaLM and Med-PaLM show that prompt tuning plus clinician exemplars can move the needle quickly.
- Near-term deployment looks like note drafting, summary generation, and translation, not autonomous diagnosis.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- General-purpose LLMs already know more medicine than the first benchmark numbers suggest.
- Accuracy is the wrong primary metric for clinical use; harm, bias, and usefulness matter more.
- The best immediate product is translation of doctor-speak into patient-speak.
- Smaller domain models may still win on some tasks, but prompt tuning keeps the general-model path viable.
- Public medical datasets stay small because the real data live in privacy-restricted silos, so evaluation may have to move to the data instead of the data moving to the benchmark.
- Hallucination is not a side issue here. It is part of the safety envelope.
