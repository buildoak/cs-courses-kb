# L24 Beyond LLMs: Agents, Emergent Abilities, Intermediate-Guided Reasoning, BabyLM

This lecture splits into four ideas that all point the same way: bigger models help, but structure, feedback, and compute allocation matter more than raw scale alone.

## Summary
- The lecture opens with emergent abilities: model performance can look flat, then jump at a critical scale threshold. Source: `[0:33]` to `[1:54]`.
- Chain-of-thought is framed as intermediate-guided reasoning, with Tree of Thought, Socratic Questioning, PAL, PoT, and Faith and Fate as extensions. Source: `[7:46]` to `[14:56]`.
- BabyLM asks a different question: what if pretraining budgets looked more like child language exposure than trillion-token web scrape runs? Source: `[15:20]` to `[19:14]`.
- The agent section treats a useful agent as a full system, not a prompt: model, memory, tools, planning, personalization, hierarchy, and security. Source: `[19:31]` to `[60:07]`.
- The closing message is simple: reliability, feedback loops, observability, and kill-switches are the real blockers. Source: `[50:32]` to `[60:07]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Emergent abilities.** Capabilities like few-shot performance or chain-of-thought can appear as sudden phase transitions instead of smooth curves. Source: `[0:33]` to `[7:24]`.
- **Intermediate-guided reasoning.** Chain-of-thought is the umbrella term the talk prefers for explicit multi-step reasoning traces. Source: `[7:46]` to `[10:18]`.
- **Scratchpad reasoning.** Humans do not think as rigidly as a template; the lecture uses "scratchpad" to argue for more flexible reasoning structures. Source: `[11:37]` to `[12:05]`.
- **Tree of Thought.** Branching search over reasoning paths with lookahead and backtracking. Source: `[12:05]` to `[12:31]`.
- **Socratic Questioning.** Recursive decomposition into subproblems, then composition back into the original answer. Source: `[12:34]` to `[13:07]`.
- **Program-Aided Language Models.** The model emits code as an intermediate step and offloads execution to a runtime. Source: `[13:10]` to `[13:51]`.
- **Program-of-Thought.** The same idea, but the intermediate reasoning is code-first rather than natural-language-first. Source: `[13:51]` to `[14:09]`.
- **Faith and Fate.** Another intermediate-reasoning line that uses computation graphs. Source: `[14:35]` to `[14:56]`.
- **BabyLM.** A challenge to train smaller models on child-scale data budgets, mostly transcribed speech and mixed domain text. Source: `[15:20]` to `[19:14]`.
- **Agent architecture.** The model is treated as a CPU-like compute unit, with memory, tools, a planning layer, and execution loops on top. Source: `[21:36]` to `[23:06]`, `[34:57]` to `[36:17]`.
- **Personalization.** The lecture splits user-agent alignment into explicit preference teaching and passive observation. Source: `[37:40]` to `[41:24]`.
- **Multi-agent systems.** Parallelism, specialization, and communication are the reason to move beyond one agent. Source: `[43:01]` to `[46:33]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- CoT is not just a prompt trick. The lecture treats it as a way to surface latent computation that is already inside the model. Source: `[8:53]` to `[9:19]`.
- The limitation is structural, not just parametric. Smaller models fail because they loop, miss symbols, and cannot reliably converge on a plan. Source: `[10:18]` to `[11:01]`.
- CoT is best for tasks with a stable multi-step structure. Open-ended generation is a poor fit. Source: `[11:07]` to `[11:57]`.
- BabyLM is about democratization as much as capability. Smaller models are easier to run, align, interpret, and share. Source: `[16:24]` to `[17:41]`.
- An agent without feedback just wanders. The lecture calls out looping and divergence as the failure mode that kills early systems like AutoGPT. Source: `[50:32]` to `[53:27]`.
- The real agent is an operating system, not a chat box. Context window, memory, browser, file system, and tools all become parts of the stack. Source: `[53:42]` to `[60:07]`.
- Multi-agent systems are basically parallel compute plus communication contracts. The hardest part is not task execution; it is synchronization and verification. Source: `[43:01]` to `[50:15]`.
