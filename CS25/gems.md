# CS25 Gems

CS25 reads like a timeline of the transformer becoming a general interface. The course keeps reducing everything to representation, memory, compute placement, and data quality, then asks which scaffolds are still worth keeping. Sources: [course.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/course.md#L17), [course.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/course.md#L35)

## Top Insights

- **The arc is interface-driven, not architecture-driven.** Language, vision, audio, control, retrieval, and biology all become transformer-shaped once the input is tokenized, patchified, discretized, or otherwise made sequence-like. The repeated move is not "use transformers everywhere"; it is "recode the problem until the transformer can own the interface." Sources: [course.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/course.md#L19), [L03.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L03.md#L26), [L09.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L09.md#L26), [L31.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L31.md#L26), [L39.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L39.md#L27), [L47.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L47.md#L27)

- **Structure is a temporary bargain.** The course keeps showing the same pattern: locality, sparsity, bottlenecks, bidirectional encoders, and other hand-built priors help when compute is scarce, then become baggage once scale catches up. Sources: [L03.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L03.md#L28), [L05.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L05.md#L26), [L06.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L06.md#L24), [L31.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L31.md#L26), [L33.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L33.md#L27), [L46.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L46.md#L25)

- **Memory is the hidden backbone.** Attention is retrievable context, induction heads turn prompts into lookup tables, RAG externalizes facts, and agent memory turns a chat model into a system that can persist across turns and tasks. The biology lectures push the same idea into sparse distributed memory and hippocampal-style retrieval. Sources: [L01.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L01.md#L25), [L08.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L08.md#L26), [L25.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L25.md#L28), [L34.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L34.md#L24), [L36.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L36.md#L25), [L42.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L42.md#L25)

- **Data quality outruns raw scale whenever humans judge the output.** Chatbots, translation, medicine, pretraining, and alignment all keep converging on the same answer: filtering, curation, preference data, and better evaluation move the ceiling more than just adding more tokens. Sources: [L12.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L12.md#L27), [L22.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L22.md#L23), [L23.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L23.md#L26), [L28.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L28.md#L27), [L32.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L32.md#L26), [L43.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L43.md#L28)

- **Once models can act, the unit becomes a system.** Agents need tools, memory, protocols, verification, and feedback loops. Benchmark answer quality stops being the right ceiling once the model has to search, plan, call tools, and survive in the world. Sources: [L24.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L24.md#L27), [L27.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L27.md#L28), [L35.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L35.md#L24), [L36.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L36.md#L25), [L37.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L37.md#L22)

- **The science lectures are not side quests.** Medicine, biology, drug discovery, and robotics all use the same move: convert sparse multimodal observation into a world model that can predict, simulate, or plan under uncertainty. Sources: [L17.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L17.md#L23), [L19.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L19.md#L24), [L39.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L39.md#L27), [L44.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L44.md#L27), [L45.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L45.md#L27), [L47.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L47.md#L27)

## Surprising Connections

- **Attention, RAG, and sparse distributed memory are the same retrieval story at different scales.** Self-attention makes context retrieval local to the model, RAG pushes retrieval outside the weights, and the SDM lecture gives the geometric / biological picture of why softmax looks the way it does. Sources: [L01.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L01.md#L25), [L25.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L25.md#L28), [L42.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L42.md#L25)

- **Induction heads, maieutic prompting, and chain-of-thought all expose latent computation.** One lecture treats prompts as lookup tables, another forces the model to justify itself, and others treat CoT or test-time search as compute that happens after pretraining. Sources: [L08.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L08.md#L26), [L13.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L13.md#L25), [L16.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L16.md#L23), [L24.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L24.md#L27), [L27.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L27.md#L28)

- **Return conditioning, RLHF, and product design are the same interface problem.** Return tokens steer behavior in offline RL, RLHF steers behavior through preference data, and product-shaped RL turns the UI into part of the training loop. Sources: [L04.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L04.md#L24), [L12.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L12.md#L27), [L35.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L35.md#L24)

- **Video generation, diffusion, robotics, and biology all collapse into representation choice plus compression.** Once the modality is encoded well, the same transformer machinery can simulate, translate, or control. Sources: [L39.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L39.md#L27), [L40.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L40.md#L25), [L41.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L41.md#L25), [L44.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L44.md#L27), [L45.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L45.md#L27), [L47.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L47.md#L27)

- **MoE, sparse attention, and Perceiver bottlenecks all bet on selective activation.** They differ in where the sparsity lives, but each says the same thing: do not light up all context, all experts, or all latent slots at once. Sources: [L05.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L05.md#L26), [L06.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L06.md#L24), [L29.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L29.md#L27), [L42.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L42.md#L25)

- **Small-model and open-model work is where the field can still see cause and effect.** BabyLM, distilled teachers, 7B/13B alignment, filtered pretraining, and open release tooling all live where iteration is still legible. Sources: [L16.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L16.md#L23), [L22.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L22.md#L23), [L28.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L28.md#L27), [L32.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L32.md#L26), [L34.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L34.md#L24), [L43.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L43.md#L28)

## Under-Appreciated Ideas

- **Masking is not a footnote.** Causal masks, masked-language-model objectives, and aggressive masking all define what the model is allowed to know. They are rules of the game, not cosmetic training tricks. Sources: [L01.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L01.md#L25), [L11.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L11.md#L29), [L17.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L17.md#L27), [L39.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L39.md#L27), [L44.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L44.md#L27)

- **Benchmarks compress the wrong thing.** Emergence, translation, medicine, and chatbot quality all change when the scoring rule changes, the evaluator changes, or the data is dirty. Sources: [L13.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L13.md#L25), [L22.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L22.md#L23), [L23.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L23.md#L26), [L43.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L43.md#L28)

- **Distillation preserves structure, not just accuracy.** It transfers reasoning behavior, commonsense graphs, sparse-expert behavior, and agent skills into systems that are easier to inspect and run. Sources: [L13.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L13.md#L27), [L16.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L16.md#L23), [L29.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L29.md#L27), [L35.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L35.md#L24), [L36.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L36.md#L25)

- **Deferral is a capability.** In medicine and alignment, saying "I do not know" or refusing dangerous output is part of the product, not a failure state. Sources: [L12.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L12.md#L27), [L17.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L17.md#L24), [L43.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L43.md#L28)

- **The architecture often matters as an interface contract.** Return tokens, action tokens, expert routing, bottlenecks, and discrete audio codes are all ways of constraining what the model can do so the downstream system stays trainable. Sources: [L04.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L04.md#L24), [L05.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L05.md#L26), [L06.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L06.md#L24), [L09.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L09.md#L26), [L15.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L15.md#L25)

- **Long context is not free memory.** Once sequences get long enough, the model recreates retrieval, ordering, and context-selection problems inside the window. Sources: [L25.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L25.md#L28), [L33.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L33.md#L27), [L41.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L41.md#L27), [L45.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L45.md#L28)

- **Biological analogies are useful because they separate content from structure.** Sparse distributed memory, hippocampal navigation, and transformer attention all point at the same move: store the parts that matter and reconstruct the rest from context. Sources: [L18.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L18.md#L22), [L42.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L42.md#L25), [L38.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L38.md#L28)

## Open Questions

- **When does a prior stop being helpful and start becoming debt?** The course keeps praising scale-driven simplification, but it also keeps finding cases where locality, sparsity, or modularity are still the right answer. Sources: [L03.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L03.md#L28), [L05.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L05.md#L26), [L06.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L06.md#L24), [L31.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L31.md#L26), [L33.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L33.md#L27), [L46.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L46.md#L25)

- **What is the right benchmark for an agent?** The lecture set wants memory, tool use, verification, and real-world trust, but most public metrics still score short answers or sandbox tasks. Sources: [L24.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L24.md#L27), [L35.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L35.md#L24), [L36.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L36.md#L25), [L37.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L37.md#L22)

- **Does long context subsume retrieval?** RAG, sparse attention, and prompt memory all suggest "maybe", but the lectures also keep emphasizing index design, ordering, governance, and external truth. Sources: [L25.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L25.md#L28), [L33.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L33.md#L27), [L42.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L42.md#L25)

- **Where is the hard boundary between data curation and model improvement?** In the corpus, better filters, better labelers, and better preference data often look like the real algorithm. How much of the remaining gap is still just a data problem? Sources: [L12.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L12.md#L27), [L22.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L22.md#L23), [L23.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L23.md#L26), [L28.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L28.md#L27), [L32.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L32.md#L26), [L43.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L43.md#L28)

- **Can multimodal world models stay controllable as they get more realistic?** The biology, video, and robotics talks all push toward simulation, but also expose harder questions about intervention, verification, and long-horizon consistency. Sources: [L39.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L39.md#L27), [L41.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L41.md#L27), [L44.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L44.md#L27), [L45.md](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS25/lectures/L45.md#L27)

- **Are the biological readings explanatory or just fertile metaphors?** Sparse distributed memory and hippocampal models are elegant, but the real test is whether they predict new behavior or just retell the transformer story in a different accent.
