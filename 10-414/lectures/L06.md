# L06 Fully Connected Networks / Optimization / Initialization

Lecture 6 stitches the pipeline together: fully connected layers, matrix broadcasting, optimization, and the initialization rules that keep deep nets alive.

## Summary
- With autodiff in place, the fully connected network definition gets simpler: `z_{i+1} = sigma_i(z_i W_i + b_i)`, repeated across layers. Source: transcript `[0:33]` to `[4:10]`.
- Bias terms force the matrix-form discussion into broadcasting territory. Needle keeps that explicit with `reshape` plus `broadcast` instead of NumPy-style implicit magic. Source: transcript `[5:21]` to `[13:14]`.
- The lecture then reviews optimization methods: gradient descent, Newton's method, momentum, Adam, and why stochastic mini-batches dominate practice. Source: transcript `[14:38]` to `[1:08:01]`.
- Zero initialization fails in deep networks because it kills symmetry and leaves gradients at zero. Source: transcript `[1:10:29]` to `[1:14:03]`.
- Random initialization works only when the variance is right. For ReLU nets, `2 / n` preserves activation scale, which is why Kaiming normal initialization shows up as the practical default. Source: transcript `[1:14:17]` to `[1:26:08]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Fully connected network / MLP.** The repeated affine + nonlinearity stack. Source: transcript `[1:08]` to `[4:10]`.
- **Bias term.** Adds a per-output offset and must be broadcast across the batch dimension. Source: transcript `[3:47]` to `[8:24]`.
- **Broadcasting.** Explicit reshape/broadcast steps in Needle instead of hidden NumPy rules. Source: transcript `[9:16]` to `[13:14]`.
- **Gradient descent.** The baseline update rule the rest of the section compares against. Source: transcript `[16:24]` to `[19:01]`.
- **Newton's method.** Faster in the toy convex case, but too expensive for deep learning at scale. Source: transcript `[24:19]` to `[37:34]`.
- **Momentum.** A first-order method that smooths the zig-zag of plain gradient descent. Source: transcript `[37:40]` to `[50:11]`.
- **Adam.** A first-order adaptive method that tracks gradient moments and rescales updates. Source: transcript `[50:11]` to `[1:00:51]`.
- **Mini-batch SGD.** The practical update rule for deep learning. Source: transcript `[1:03:33]` to `[1:08:12]`.
- **Initialization variance.** The scale of the initial random weights matters as much as the random seed itself. Source: transcript `[1:14:17]` to `[1:26:08]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- Broadcasting is not just syntax sugar. In a framework it becomes an explicit tensor operation that must also differentiate correctly.
- Newton's method is useful here mostly as a contrast class. It clarifies what gradient descent gives up in exchange for cheaper iterations.
- SGD's noise is not a bug. The lecture leans on the fact that many small noisy steps can beat one expensive exact step.
- Zero init fails because every hidden unit stays identical, so the network never breaks symmetry.
- The variance choice matters because weights do not move far from their initial scale during training. That makes initialization a first-order design decision, not a footnote.
