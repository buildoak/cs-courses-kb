# CS330 Gems

## Top Insights
- The course keeps relocating task information instead of just growing model size. Multi-task sharing, task descriptors, prompt examples, latent `z/phi`, episodic memory, and skill latents are all different places to answer "what task am I on?" When that information can be cached in the backbone or `Theta`, the model memorizes instead of adapting. - The strongest methods align training computation with test-time computation. ProtoNets train a softened nearest-neighbor rule, masked autoencoders and BERT train the fill-in-the-missing-pieces operation they will need later, black-box meta-learning trains on support/query episodes because that is the inference interface, and Bayesian meta-learning turns task inference into posterior inference. - Task construction is the hidden half of the problem. Contrastive pairs, clustering-based pseudo-tasks, masked-word tasks, label shuffling, and skill discovery all manufacture supervision that the model cannot reliably extract on its own. - Most "meta" methods are really prior-learning methods. They do not just adapt faster; they change what counts as a plausible task, domain, or exploration trace before evidence arrives. ## Surprising Connections
- Prompting is episodic meta-learning wearing text clothes. L02 says task descriptors behave like prompts; L04 says support examples are the data the learner conditions on; L18 shows prompt examples reveal both task and format. Sources: `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L02.md:25-26`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L04.md:24-26`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L18.md:24-30`.
- Contrastive learning, prototypical networks, and task construction all answer the same question: what counts as the right "same"? L07 chooses positives by augmentation or adjacency; L06 turns that relation into a metric-space classifier; L09 and L20 manufacture episodes to make the relation explicit. Sources: `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L07.md:21-25`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L06.md:23-28`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L09.md:21-25`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L20.md:38-41`.
- Reconstruction, masking, and memorization control are three versions of the same anti-shortcut trick. Bottleneck autoencoders still hash, masked prediction forces missing-structure inference, and meta-learning task exclusivity prevents the model from stashing task identity in `Theta`. Sources: `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L08.md:22-26`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L09.md:21-24`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L20.md:36-37`.
- Bayesian meta-learning and meta-RL differ mainly in what the latent belief controls. In one case the posterior guides predictions; in the other it guides action selection and exploration. Sources: `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L11.md:23-36`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L12.md:23-29`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L19.md:37-45`.
- Transfer learning and model soups connect through geometry, not just representations. Fine-tuned models often share basins, and interpolation or averaging can recover accuracy without extra inference cost. Sources: `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L03.md:25-28`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L16.md:23-29`.
- Domain adaptation, generalization, and lifelong learning are the same shift problem with different access to target data. One gets unlabeled target examples, one gets none, and one gets a stream over time. Sources: `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L13.md:22-26`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L14.md:24-28`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L15.md:26-30`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L17.md:25-26`.

## Under-Appreciated Ideas
- `Non-parametric` methods are still learned; they just move the learning into the embedding and similarity function instead of task-specific weights. Source: `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L06.md:23-28`.
- Task relatedness is not fixed. The same pair can help early and hurt later, so the right amount of sharing changes over optimization. Sources: `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L02.md:27-29`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L03.md:27-28`.
- Low-dimensional latents are not automatically useful. The real issue is whether the latent preserves reusable structure or just compresses to a hash. Sources: `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L08.md:22-23`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L11.md:23-29`.
- Uncertainty matters only when it changes action. L12 makes active learning the real test, and L19 turns uncertainty into exploration policy under partial observability. Sources: `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L12.md:25-29`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L19.md:37-45`.
- The bottleneck often moves away from model size. Meta-learning hits memory and graph-depth limits, transfer saturates with scale, and learned optimizers bring compute costs back in. Sources: `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L10.md:22-27`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L16.md:27-29`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L17.md:28-31`.
- Segmentation is part of learning. `MOCA` does not just learn across tasks; it learns where the tasks begin and end. Sources: `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L20.md:38-39`.
- Data diversity and benchmark design become more important after scale. Bigger pretraining alone saturates, so curation and evaluation quality take over. Sources: `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L16.md:29-36`, `/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS330/lectures/L17.md:30-38`.
