# CS330 L12: Bayesian Meta-Learning and Uncertainty

## Summary
- Bayesian meta-learning treats task inference as posterior inference over task-specific parameters.
- The lecture splits uncertainty into aleatoric noise and epistemic uncertainty, then argues that the latter is the real Bayesian prize.
- Black-box Bayesian meta-learning uses latent-variable VI; optimization-based methods reinterpret MAML as MAP under a prior.
- Ensembles, SVGD-style particle methods, and amortized inference are the main routes to non-Gaussian posteriors.
- Standard accuracy benchmarks are not enough. Calibration, mode coverage, and active learning behavior matter more.

## Key Concepts
- **Epistemic uncertainty.** Uncertainty about the model or function.
- **Aleatoric uncertainty.** Uncertainty from the data itself.
- **Bayesian black-box meta-learning.** Latent-variable inference over task-specific parameters.
- **Bayesian optimization-based meta-learning.** Treat inner-loop adaptation as posterior inference.
- **Task-specific posterior.** A distribution over `phi_i` rather than a single point estimate.
- **MAP estimate.** The point-estimate approximation to a posterior.
- **Bayesian ensembles.** Multiple independently trained models as samples from a distribution.
- **Stein Variational Gradient Descent.** Particle-based method that pushes ensemble members apart.
- **Amortized inference.** Use a learned inference process instead of per-task optimization only.
- **Calibration and coverage.** Better tests than raw accuracy for uncertainty-aware methods.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- `q` is not a side network in the black-box formulation. It is the inner loop.
- MAML is Bayesian only in the loose MAP sense until you actually preserve a distribution over task parameters.
- The useful question is not "does it classify well?" but "does it know when it does not know?"
- Accuracy alone can hide the failure mode: a method may be right on average and still useless for active learning.
- Ensembles remain strong because epistemic uncertainty is hard to approximate with one Gaussian posterior.
- A toy ambiguous task can be more revealing than a real benchmark if it actually stresses mode coverage.
- Active learning is the sharpest practical test in the lecture: good uncertainty should improve faster than random querying.
