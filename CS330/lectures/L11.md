# CS330 L11: Variational Inference and Generative Models

## Summary
- Latent variable models explain complex data by inserting an unobserved `z` that makes the distribution easier to represent.
- The lecture builds the ELBO from the intractable marginal likelihood and turns it into a tractable optimization target.
- Amortized variational inference replaces one variational distribution per datapoint with an inference network.
- The reparameterization trick is the key move that makes stochastic latent sampling differentiable.
- The VAE view is simple: encode to a latent distribution, decode back, and regularize with KL so the model does not collapse into an identity map.

## Key Concepts
- **Latent variable model.** A model with hidden variables that explain the observed data.
- **Conditional generative model.** A model for `p(x | z)` or `p(y | x, z)`.
- **Posterior approximation.** Replace the true posterior with a simpler variational distribution `q`.
- **ELBO.** Lower bound on the log likelihood that becomes the training objective.
- **Entropy and KL divergence.** The two terms that control reconstruction quality and latent regularity.
- **Amortized VI.** Use one inference network instead of one variational distribution per example.
- **Inference network.** A neural network that predicts the mean and variance of `q(z | x)`.
- **Reparameterization trick.** Rewrite sampling as `mu + epsilon * sigma` so gradients flow.
- **Variational autoencoder.** The reconstruction-plus-KL interpretation of the latent-variable objective.
- **Continuous vs discrete latents.** Gaussian latents are easy; discrete latents need other tricks.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The latent variable usually has no semantic label. It only needs to carry whatever information `x` cannot explain.
- In conditional models, `z` is the extra uncertainty channel. It should capture the ambiguity that the input leaves unresolved.
- The ELBO is not just an inequality trick. It is the practical bridge from an intractable integral to sample-based training.
- Amortization is a scale move as much as an inference move. Without it, deep latent models would need one fitted posterior per example.
- The KL term is doing real work: it keeps the latent space from becoming a pure copy path.
- The reparameterization trick is special because the noise source is independent of the trainable parameters.
- Continuous Gaussian latents are the easy path. Discrete latents need straight-through estimators, vector quantization, or REINFORCE-style gradients.

## Connections
- `Lecture 12` reuses the same VI machinery, but swaps the latent `z` for task-specific parameters `phi`.
- Autoregressive models sit outside the latent-variable family but belong to the same broader generative modeling toolbox.
- GANs, normalizing flows, and diffusion models are adjacent families with different likelihood and training tradeoffs.
- `VQ-VAE`-style discrete latents are the natural escape hatch when Gaussian latents are too restrictive.
- The lecture is the generative-model half of the Bayesian story that becomes explicit in the next lecture.
