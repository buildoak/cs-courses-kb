# L07 GPT: Autoregressive Language Modeling

GPT is the course's causal transformer story: score the next token from left to right, mask the future, train with teacher forcing, then switch to sampling and beam-style search at generation time. The lecture starts from the loss, not the branding, and keeps returning to the same point: GPT is conditional language modeling inside a transformer stack. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L07.transcript.txt`, `[0:04]` to `[4:56]`.

## Summary
- GPT is introduced through the autoregressive loss function, with the model scoring the next token against the whole vocabulary from prior context only. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L07.transcript.txt`, `[0:19]` to `[1:06]`.
- The transformer version of GPT adds a causal attention mask so the model can look backward but never into tokens it has not generated yet. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L07.transcript.txt`, `[2:56]` to `[4:53]`.
- Training uses teacher forcing: the model consumes the gold previous token during training, even though generation must feed back its own predictions. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L07.transcript.txt`, `[5:02]` to `[9:55]`.
- At generation time, the lecture separates the model's score vector from the decision rule that turns scores into tokens, which can be greedy, sampled, or beam-searched. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L07.transcript.txt`, `[10:08]` to `[10:49]`.
- The lecture closes by treating GPT-1, GPT-2, and GPT-3 as scale steps in the same family, then points to open alternatives that are already competitive in size. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L07.transcript.txt`, `[11:40]` to `[13:33]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Autoregressive loss.** The objective predicts the next token from the prefix, not from the full sequence. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L07.transcript.txt`, `[0:19]` to `[1:17]`.
- **Causal mask.** Attention is allowed to move backward in time only, which prevents leakage from future tokens. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L07.transcript.txt`, `[4:04]` to `[4:53]`.
- **Teacher forcing.** Training uses the gold previous token even when the model made a wrong prediction at the last step. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L07.transcript.txt`, `[5:02]` to `[8:39]`.
- **Generation loop.** The model predicts a score distribution, a token is chosen, then that token is fed back in as context for the next step. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L07.transcript.txt`, `[8:43]` to `[10:49]`.
- **Sampling and beam search.** The lecture distinguishes a local choice rule from sequence-level search over several steps. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L07.transcript.txt`, `[10:08]` to `[10:31]`.
- **Fine-tuning hook.** The default downstream signal comes from the final output state, but pooling across all states is an alternative. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L07.transcript.txt`, `[10:53]` to `[11:32]`.
- **Model scaling.** GPT-1, GPT-2, and GPT-3 are presented as bigger instances of the same design, not different objectives. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L07.transcript.txt`, `[11:49]` to `[12:54]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- Inference: generation is a decision rule layered on top of scores, not something intrinsic to the transformer itself. The same distribution can feed greedy decoding, sampling, or beam search. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L07.transcript.txt`, `[10:08]` to `[10:49]`.
- Inference: teacher forcing is what makes the training loop tractable. Once generation starts, the model must live with its own outputs and cannot borrow the gold next token anymore. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L07.transcript.txt`, `[7:56]` to `[9:40]`.
- Inference: the final hidden state is the simplest fine-tuning interface, but not the only one. Pooling over all states is a real option if the task needs more sequence information. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L07.transcript.txt`, `[10:53]` to `[11:26]`.
- Inference: scale changes the practical interface to the model. GPT-3 is not only larger; it is presented as a model whose prompting behavior starts to matter as a user-facing capability. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L07.transcript.txt`, `[12:25]` to `[13:33]`.
