## Summary
- ELECTRA is framed as a response to two BERT problems: the masked-token mismatch between pretraining and finetuning, and the fact that MLM only gives learning signal on about 15% of tokens.
- The model uses a small generator to create corrupted tokens and a discriminator to detect which tokens were replaced.
- The discriminator, not the generator, is the reusable pretrained artifact.
- The best tradeoff comes from a weak generator and a stronger discriminator; the paper’s evidence shows that more prediction work can be better than less.
- Across compute budgets, ELECTRA beats BERT and related variants on GLUE.

## Key Concepts
- **Replaced-token detection:** the discriminator learns to identify original versus substituted tokens.
- **Generator / discriminator split:** the generator produces candidate corruptions; the discriminator learns the downstream representation.
- **Masked-token mismatch:** masked tokens appear in pretraining but not in finetuning, which may weaken transfer.
- **Data efficiency:** ELECTRA tries to make more of each sequence matter during training.
- **Compute budget:** the paper measures quality against pre-train FLOPs, not just model size.
- **Adversarial ELECTRA:** a generator that actively tries to fool the discriminator is interesting, but not the best variant in the lecture’s summary.
- **Prediction density:** the lecture keeps returning to the idea that more prediction targets can improve performance.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- A weaker generator can be better, because the discriminator needs hard negatives to do useful work.
- ELECTRA’s main gain is not architectural novelty per se; it is denser supervision.
- The lecture implies that BERT’s MLM wasted signal, and that some of BERT’s gains were available with a better training objective alone.
- Efficiency is treated as a question of where learning signal lands, not just how many parameters are in the model.

## Connections
- RoBERTa fixes the first BERT limitation in the setup; ELECTRA attacks the other two.
- The corruption-and-repair pattern rhymes with later seq2seq denoising objectives like BART.
- The compute obsession here flows directly into the next lecture on distillation.
- ELECTRA fits the course’s broader move from architecture novelty toward training-objective design.
