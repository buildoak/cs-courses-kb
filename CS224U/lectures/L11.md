## Summary
- Seq2seq is presented as the natural framing for translation, summarization, free-form QA, dialogue, semantic parsing, and code generation.
- The lecture contrasts classic RNN seq2seq systems with transformer-era options: encoder-decoder, decoder-only, and hybrid encode-then-decode setups.
- T5 turns task descriptions into natural-language prefixes, which is an early glimpse of prompt-style control.
- BART pretrains by corrupting sequences and teaching the model to reconstruct them.
- Both models show that the training objective matters as much as the architecture.

## Key Concepts
- **Seq2seq task family:** any problem where one structured input becomes one structured output.
- **Encoder-decoder:** the canonical transformer architecture for sequence transduction.
- **Decoder-only language model:** a GPT-style alternative that can still handle many seq2seq problems.
- **Task prefixes:** natural-language instructions that condition the model before the actual input.
- **Corrupted-sequence pretraining:** pretraining on damaged input teaches the model how to restore structure.
- **Text infilling:** one of BART’s most effective corruption strategies.
- **Sentence shuffling:** another BART corruption strategy that forces broader reconstruction.
- **Fine-tuning without corruption:** corruption is mostly a pretraining trick, not a finetuning requirement.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- Dialogue is treated as a seq2seq problem, which makes this lecture a bridge to conversational AI rather than just translation.
- T5’s task prefixes are one step away from the instruction-tuning mindset that shows up later in the course.
- The lecture treats task phrasing as structured input, not as cosmetic wording.
- BART and ELECTRA share a deeper theme: corrupt the input, then learn what good sequences look like.
- Decoder-only models are not rejected here; they are just one point in a larger design space.

## Connections
