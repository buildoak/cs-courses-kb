## Key Concepts
- **Natural language understanding.** The lecture defines NLU by contrast: systems can sound fluent and still fail on simple, structured questions. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L01.transcript.txt`, `[4:05]` to `[8:54]`.
- **Symbolic systems.** Chat-80 is used as a reminder that older NLP systems could reason deeply inside a narrow frame and still collapse outside it. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L01.transcript.txt`, `[4:29]` to `[5:20]`.
- **Self-supervision.** Modern models learn from co-occurrence patterns in symbol streams instead of from hand-labeled examples. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L01.transcript.txt`, `[21:35]` to `[23:03]`.
- **Distributional learning.** The lecture treats distributional learning as the mechanism that lets models absorb structure from text, code, and other symbol sequences. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L01.transcript.txt`, `[21:35]` to `[23:07]`.
- **Contextual representations.** ELMo, BERT, and GPT shift the field from one-vector-per-word thinking to representations shaped by context. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L01.transcript.txt`, `[24:06]` to `[25:24]`.
- **Pretraining.** Large models become useful because they carry a pretrained state into a new task instead of starting from scratch. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L01.transcript.txt`, `[18:31]` to `[20:19]`.
- **Prompting and in-context learning.** GPT-3 turns prompting into a usable interface: the model is put in a temporary state and asked to continue or answer. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L01.transcript.txt`, `[33:07]` to `[34:38]`.
- **RLHF.** Human-written examples and human rankings push the model beyond raw co-occurrence statistics. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L01.transcript.txt`, `[36:47]` to `[38:22]`.
- **Chain-of-thought prompting.** Step-by-step prompts can elicit latent reasoning that plain prompts miss. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L01.transcript.txt`, `[38:27]` to `[40:52]`.
- **Explainability.** The lecture explicitly points toward methods that let us inspect models instead of treating them as black boxes. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L01.transcript.txt`, `[45:59]` to `[46:18]`.
- **Modularity.** The course treats modularity as an open question, not a settled architecture choice. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L01.transcript.txt`, `[44:21]` to `[46:18]`.
- **Retrieval augmentation.** The course roadmap includes database-backed systems as one way to make model behavior more grounded. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L01.transcript.txt`, `[48:18]` to `[48:38]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The same co-occurrence machinery can explain idioms, routine completions, and some factual answers; the model is not switching mechanisms between those cases. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L01.transcript.txt`, `[30:22]` to `[31:29]`.
- Prompting is not model training in disguise. It is temporary state shaping, and that changes how control works. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L01.transcript.txt`, `[33:07]` to `[34:00]`.
- Scale buys capability, but it also centralizes power and hides internal structure. The lecture flags both problems. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L01.transcript.txt`, `[27:01]` to `[29:55]`, `[45:21]` to `[46:18]`.
- The lecture treats modularity as a live research question: maybe it emerges from data, maybe it needs to be analyzed back out of the model. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L01.transcript.txt`, `[44:21]` to `[46:18]`.
- Smaller models that can be inspected may matter more for analysis than giant closed APIs. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L01.transcript.txt`, `[28:38]` to `[29:48]`, `[45:39]` to `[46:18]`.
