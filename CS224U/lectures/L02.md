# L02 Distributed Word Representations: From Counts to Context

This lecture is a history of how NLU representations got less hand-built and more learned. It starts with sparse feature vectors, moves through count-based distributional methods, and ends at contextual tokens, subword units, and fine-tuned transformers. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[46:03]` to `[1:09:31]`.

## Summary
- The lecture begins by laying out the course's main units and previewing the kind of hard semantic testing the class will do with COGS and ReCOGS. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[0:05]` to `[9:17]`.
- The first representation stage is hand-engineered sparse features; the next is count-based weighting with PMI and TF-IDF; then dimensionality reduction with PCA, SVD/LSA, and LDA. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[46:10]` to `[48:16]`.
- Word2Vec, GloVe, and related learned embeddings make representation itself a learned object instead of a fixed table of counts. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[49:01]` to `[49:49]`.
- The real break comes with contextual representations: words are no longer single vectors, tokenization becomes subword-aware, and positional encoding becomes part of the model. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[51:09]` to `[1:06:29]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Sparse feature representations.** Early NLP represented words with hand-designed yes/no feature functions. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[46:10]` to `[46:53]`.
- **PMI and TF-IDF.** The lecture treats these as the first serious count-based distributional methods. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[47:01]` to `[47:44]`.
- **Dimensionality reduction.** PCA, SVD/LSA, and LDA compress count data while preserving useful similarity structure. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[47:53]` to `[48:16]`.
- **Learned embeddings.** Autoencoders, Word2Vec, and GloVe learn dense representations from co-occurrence data instead of hand-designing them. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[49:01]` to `[49:34]`.
- **Contextual representations.** The lecture argues that one vector per word is too rigid for words like "break," which change sense across contexts. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[51:11]` to `[55:33]`.
- **Tokenization.** BERT-style models split words into subword pieces instead of forcing unknown-token fallbacks. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[1:03:53]` to `[1:05:30]`.
- **Positional encoding.** Sequence position becomes part of the representation, so the same word in a different slot is not identical. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[1:05:35]` to `[1:06:29]`.
- **Pretraining.** The lecture treats Dai and Le, CoVe, ELMo, BERT, and GPT as milestones in the same move toward reusable representations. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[56:06]` to `[57:45]`.
- **Attention.** Attention lets later tokens look back across earlier states instead of relying only on the final hidden state. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[1:00:37]` to `[1:02:01]`.
- **Fine-tuning.** The lecture describes fine-tuning as backpropagating task loss through the pretrained model, sometimes with some parameters frozen and some updated. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[1:07:29]` to `[1:12:40]`.
- **In-context learning.** GPT-3 makes prompting a first-class interface: the model is put in a temporary state and asked to continue or answer. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[1:07:43]` to `[1:09:31]`.
- **RLHF.** Human-written outputs and human rankings supply the preference signal that self-supervision cannot provide. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[37:03]` to `[38:22]`.
- **Modularity and explainability.** The lecture frames model inspection as a way to test whether structure is emerging naturally. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[44:21]` to `[46:18]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- Distributional semantics is not just about "similar words." The higher-order neighborhood is the real signal. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[47:27]` to `[48:50]`.
- Tokenization choices encode bias. WordPiece, BPE, and unigram tokenization are modeling decisions, not preprocessing trivia. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[1:10:02]` to `[1:11:07]`.
- Subword splitting is a feature, not a bug, once context is modeled richly enough. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[1:03:53]` to `[1:05:30]`.
- Fine-tuning is an optimization problem with knobs, not a clean yes/no switch. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[1:11:34]` to `[1:13:30]`.
- Scale widens capability but also changes who can participate. The lecture is explicit about centralization and the value of smaller, inspectable models. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[27:01]` to `[29:55]`, `[45:39]` to `[46:18]`.
- The move from static vectors to contextual vectors is not just a model upgrade. It is a change in what counts as "a word representation" at all. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L02.transcript.txt`, `[51:11]` to `[55:33]`.
