# L03 Sentiment Analysis: The Bakeoff Contract

This screencast is less about sentiment itself than about the discipline surrounding sentiment work. The assignment is a bakeoff with public test-set boundaries, explicit baselines, and one original system at the end. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[0:27]` to `[16:58]`.

## Summary
- The assignment is multi-domain ternary sentiment over DynaSent Round 1, DynaSent Round 2, and SST, with mystery examples held back for the bakeoff. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[1:47]` to `[3:58]`.
- The first task builds sparse linear classifiers from feature functions; the lesson is to use unit tests and make the featurization better, not to overcomplicate the model. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[5:06]` to `[9:32]`.
- The second task fine-tunes a BERT mini-model: batch tokenization, representation extraction, masking, and a BERT classifier module. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[9:39]` to `[12:40]`.
- The third task asks for an original ternary sentiment system, a textual description of that system, and a single bakeoff submission file. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[12:52]` to `[16:12]`.
- The screencast keeps returning to scientific discipline: do not tune on public test sets, respect the file names, and run the evaluation once. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[3:21]` to `[4:10]`, `[15:23]` to `[16:12]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Multi-domain sentiment.** The task spans several sentiment sources so the model has to generalize across domains. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[1:47]` to `[3:16]`.
- **Ternary labels.** Positive, negative, and neutral are the three sentiment classes used in the assignment. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[1:51]` to `[1:57]`.
- **DynaSent Round 1 and Round 2.** These provide naturally occurring and adversarially written sentiment data. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[2:02]` to `[2:22]`.
- **Stanford Sentiment Treebank.** The lecture notes that SST is reloaded into the same ternary setup. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[2:26]` to `[2:39]`.
- **Feature functions.** The first homework task starts with a unigram baseline and asks for a better tokenizer-based feature map. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[5:57]` to `[6:40]`.
- **Linear classifiers.** Sparse features are paired with a lightweight scikit-learn baseline before any transformer work begins. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[5:06]` to `[5:24]`, `[7:59]` to `[9:17]`.
- **Batch tokenization.** Hugging Face tokenizers handle batched text inputs and keyword arguments in the BERT task. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[9:58]` to `[10:25]`.
- **Hidden-state extraction.** The second task asks you to extract representations from BERT and use them as features for sentiment. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[10:38]` to `[11:05]`.
- **Masking.** The BERT module needs masking logic before it can be used as a classifier. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[11:07]` to `[11:48]`.
- **BERT classifier module.** The core fine-tuning task is to finish the init and forward methods of an `nn.Module`. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[11:16]` to `[11:48]`.
- **Classifier interface.** The optional wrapper exists so you can train and assess your own model cleanly. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[12:06]` to `[12:40]`.
- **Original system.** The assignment rewards creative exploration, not just baseline reuse. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[12:52]` to `[14:06]`.
- **Bakeoff submission.** The final file must contain a `prediction` column with positive, negative, or neutral strings. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[15:23]` to `[16:12]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The assignment is really a contract about experimental practice. The model matters, but the discipline around it matters more. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[3:21]` to `[4:10]`, `[13:21]` to `[16:12]`.
- The public test sets are intentionally dangerous. The screencast treats cheating as a scientific failure, not just a course policy violation. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[3:21]` to `[3:58]`.
- The original-system writeup is part of the evaluation signal. It is how the team learns what you tried, even if the final model is not the winner. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[13:23]` to `[15:12]`.
- Tokenization is not a small implementation detail. In the BERT task it is one of the main engineering steps. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[9:58]` to `[10:25]`.
- The bakeoff is a bounded experiment: tune on train/dev, submit once, then stop. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83G7Q58GKQQ1C68WBZ6438/L03.transcript.txt`, `[3:21]` to `[4:10]`, `[15:23]` to `[16:12]`.
