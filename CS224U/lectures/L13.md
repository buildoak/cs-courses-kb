# L13 Model Evaluation: Baselines, Curves, and Random Seeds

This lecture treats evaluation as engineering, not ceremony. A score only matters relative to baselines, a real search budget, dev-set curves, and the instability introduced by random initialization. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83TXKDJPHK87ERMGKN5WFG/transcripts/L44.transcript.txt`, `[0:05]` to `[21:45]`; notebook: <https://notebook.community/cgpotts/cs224u/evaluation_methods>.

## Key Concepts
- **Baselines.** Random, oracle, and task-specific baselines define what counts as progress. Source: transcript `[0:52]` to `[5:33]`.
- **Ablations.** Removing a component is a baseline move, not a side quest. Source: notebook lines 52-53 and 63-66.
- **Hyperparameter optimization.** Search over settings is part of model selection, and it must happen on train/dev data, not the test set. Source: notebook lines 165-185.
- **Cross-validation.** Repeated splits are the cleanest way to compare systems when the data and budget allow it. Source: notebook lines 171-176 and 201-215.
- **Confidence intervals.** Score distributions matter more than a single number when runs vary. Source: notebook lines 201-215.
- **Wilcoxon signed-rank test.** Useful when you can afford many repeated splits. Source: notebook lines 216-228.
- **McNemar's test.** Useful when repeated training is too expensive and you can compare prediction vectors directly. Source: notebook lines 229-245.
- **Incremental dev-set testing.** Re-evaluate during training so you can see the real shape of learning. Source: notebook lines 246-305.
- **Early stopping.** Pick the iteration that maximizes dev performance, not the one that happens to finish last. Source: notebook lines 314-318.
- **Random initialization.** Seed choice matters enough to deserve explicit reporting and multiple runs. Source: notebook lines 326-364.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- A baseline is not a footnote. It is the measurement frame that tells you what the model is actually doing. Source: transcript `[0:52]` to `[5:33]`.
- Tuning on the test set is not just sloppy. It destroys the meaning of the test set. Source: notebook lines 165-185 and 371-372.
- A single final score can hide the most important part of training: whether the model was actually improving, overfitting, or collapsing. Source: notebook lines 246-325.
- Seed variance can turn two "different" models into the same model statistically. Source: notebook lines 328-364.
- Inference: evaluation protocol is part of the scientific claim. The lecture treats metric choice, split choice, and run count as methodological decisions, not bookkeeping. Source: transcript `[20:37]` to `[21:45]`; notebook lines 367-374.
