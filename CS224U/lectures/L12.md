## Summary
- Distillation is the course’s efficiency move: make models smaller while preserving performance.
- A student can learn from teacher labels, score vectors, hidden states, embeddings, or even counterfactual interventions.
- Standard distillation freezes the teacher; co-distillation, multi-teacher distillation, and self-distillation relax that boundary.
- The lecture’s concrete takeaway is that BERT can be compressed aggressively without destroying GLUE performance.
- Distillation is framed as behavior transfer and regularization, not just compression.

## Key Concepts
- **Teacher / student:** a large model teaches a smaller one.
- **Frozen teacher:** standard distillation usually treats the teacher as fixed.
- **Hard-label distillation:** the student imitates the teacher’s predicted labels.
- **Soft-label distillation:** the student matches the teacher’s output score distribution.
- **Representation matching:** deeper distillation can align hidden states or embeddings.
- **Cosine loss:** one way to pull teacher and student representations together.
- **Multi-teacher distillation:** multiple teachers can contribute to one student.
- **Co-distillation / online distillation:** teacher and student are trained together.
- **Self-distillation:** a model distills from its own components.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- Teacher predictions can act as a regularizer when gold labels are noisy or hard to learn.
- The deeper the distillation signal, the more teacher access you need at training time.
- Counterfactual distillation is trying to preserve causal structure, not just outputs.
- The lecture treats distillation as a practical deployment tool, not an academic compression trick.

## Connections
- Distillation and ELECTRA are both answers to the same compute-efficiency pressure around BERT-era models.
- GLUE is the shared benchmark that keeps the efficiency claims grounded.
- The student/teacher pattern fits the course’s broader theme of reusable pretrained artifacts.
- This lecture closes the loop on the sequence from large pretraining to practical model size.
