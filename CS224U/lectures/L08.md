# L08 BERT: Masked Language Modeling and Fine-Tuning

BERT is GPT's bidirectional sibling. The lecture keeps the comparison simple: GPT predicts forward, BERT reconstructs masked tokens from both sides, then reuses the same transformer stack for downstream tasks through a CLS-based fine-tuning head or pooled sequence representations. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[0:04]` to `[6:47]`.

## Summary
- BERT is introduced as a transformer stack built around a CLS token and bidirectional output states. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[0:18]` to `[2:04]`.
- The core objective is masked language modeling: hide some tokens, then reconstruct them from the full surrounding context. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[2:07]` to `[4:54]`.
- The original BERT paper also adds next sentence prediction to push discourse-level information into the representation. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[5:00]` to `[5:34]`.
- Fine-tuning usually starts from the CLS output, but pooling over all output states is presented as a valid alternative. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[5:44]` to `[6:47]`.
- The lecture ends by naming BERT's limitations: pretrain/fine-tune mismatch, low prediction density, and dependence assumptions that later papers attack. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[9:27]` to `[11:35]`.

## Key Concepts
- **CLS token.** BERT uses a fixed first token whose final state becomes the cheap sequence summary for classification-style tasks. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[0:42]` to `[0:47]`, `[5:53]` to `[6:17]`.
- **Masked language modeling.** The model learns from corrupted tokens only, using bidirectional context to reconstruct what was hidden. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[2:07]` to `[4:54]`.
- **Mask token / corruption.** The objective works by inserting a special token or random replacement, then predicting the original token. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[2:38]` to `[3:16]`.
- **Next sentence prediction.** Sentence pairs are labeled IsNext or NotNext to teach the model some discourse structure. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[5:00]` to `[5:34]`.
- **Fine-tuning heads.** The standard setup attaches a small classifier to CLS, but mean or max pooling over all states is also viable. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[5:44]` to `[6:47]`.
- **WordPiece tokenization.** BERT avoids unknown-token collapse by splitting words into subword pieces. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[6:56]` to `[7:24]`.
- **Sequence-length limit.** The lecture flags 512 tokens as a hard practical ceiling in the original release family. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[8:51]` to `[9:05]`.
- **Known limitations.** The lecture points to pretrain/fine-tune mismatch, token sparsity in the loss, and masked-token independence as the main BERT issues. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[9:27]` to `[11:35]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- Inference: BERT spends compute on every time step but gets an explicit learning signal for only the masked minority. That is why the objective feels wasteful even when it works. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[4:21]` to `[4:54]`.
- Inference: the CLS token is not just a special symbol. It is a representation bottleneck that makes downstream classification cheap and stable. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[6:00]` to `[6:17]`.
- Inference: the mask token creates a train/fine-tune mismatch because the model sees the token during pretraining and never sees it during task adaptation. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[9:55]` to `[10:20]`.
- Inference: BERT's masked-token assumption misses dependencies among the masked items themselves, which is why "New York" is the example that makes the flaw obvious. Source: `/tmp/agent-mux-501/01KN83SXT1BE435BE87XRG7E2F/L08.transcript.txt`, `[10:55]` to `[11:35]`.
