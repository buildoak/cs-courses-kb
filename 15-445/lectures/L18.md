# L18 Write-Ahead Logging & ARIES Recovery

This lecture pair is the durability contract. Write the log first, let the buffer pool steal pages when it needs to, then rebuild the database after the crash.

## Summary
- No-steal/no-force is easy to reason about but expensive. Write-ahead logging lets the engine steal pages and delay dirty-page flushes while still preserving crash safety. Source: `[15:23]` to `[18:18]` of L21.
- Log records carry transaction IDs, before/after images, and LSNs. Page LSN and flush LSN enforce the write-ahead rule. Source: `[32:33]` to `[43:50]` of L21, `[9:01]` to `[14:36]` of L22.
- Checkpoints keep the log from becoming unbounded recovery work. Fuzzy checkpoints are the practical version because they do not stop the world. Source: `[1:00:14]` to `[1:03:59]` of L21, `[30:00]` to `[41:43]` of L22.
- ARIES recovery runs analysis, redo, and undo. CLRs make undo safe to repeat if recovery itself crashes. Source: `[42:03]` to `[57:57]` of L22, `[59:04]` to `[1:06:55]` of L22.
- Shadow paging is the contrast case: simpler recovery, worse runtime behavior. Source: `[7:38]` to `[8:03]` of L21, `[20:30]` to `[21:07]` of L21.

## Key Concepts
- **Write-ahead log.** Changes are recorded in the log before the modified page is allowed to hit disk. Source: `[32:33]` to `[33:58]` of L21.
- **Steal / no-steal.** Steal lets the buffer manager evict dirty pages from uncommitted transactions if the log is safe. Source: `[15:23]` to `[16:24]` of L21.
- **Force / no-force.** Force makes commit slower because every dirty page must be written out before the transaction returns. Source: `[15:21]` to `[16:18]` of L21.
- **LSN.** Log sequence numbers order the log records and let recovery know what has already been applied. Source: `[9:01]` to `[14:36]` of L22.
- **Page LSN / flush LSN.** Page LSN marks the last log record that changed a page; flush LSN tells the engine how far the log is safely on disk. Source: `[10:06]` to `[14:36]` of L22.
- **Transaction table.** Tracks the status and last LSN for each live transaction during normal operation and recovery. Source: `[33:30]` to `[34:44]` of L21, `[11:55]` to `[12:43]` of L22.
- **Dirty page table.** Tracks which pages may need redo during recovery and the earliest log record that dirtied them. Source: `[33:30]` to `[34:44]` of L21, `[35:07]` to `[36:13]` of L22.
- **CLR.** Compensation log records record undo work so recovery can restart without redoing the same undo twice. Source: `[19:57]` to `[25:17]` of L22.
- **ARIES phases.** Analyze discovers the state at crash time, redo rebuilds history, and undo cleans up losers. Source: `[42:03]` to `[57:57]` of L22.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- WAL is not just a recovery mechanism. It is what lets the buffer manager make better eviction choices.
- Checkpoints do not make recovery free. They make it bounded.
- ARIES replays history first on purpose. That is what makes undo idempotent.
- The log is a compact durability record, but it is also a control plane for recovery and replay.
- Shadow paging looks cleaner on paper, but it pushes the pain into the runtime path and copy cost.
