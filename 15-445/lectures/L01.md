# L01 Course Overview and Logistics

This lecture is mostly the contract: what the course is, what it is not, and how the semester will run. The relational model only appears at the end, as the next stop.

## Summary
- The course is about DBMS internals, not database administration. The first homework touches SQL, but the real work is building a database system in C++. Source: `[4:09]` to `[4:53]`, `[8:39]` to `[8:50]`.
- Logistics are explicit: Piazza for discussion, Gradescope for submissions, Canvas for grades, and the waitlist is not controlled by the instructor. Source: `[1:57]` to `[3:59]`.
- A database is defined as an organized collection of related data that models some part of the real world. Source: `[5:41]` to `[7:16]`.
- The lecture keeps pushing toward the relational model and relational algebra, which are the real foundation of the course. Source: `[20:30]` to `[20:47]`.
- Questions during lecture are encouraged; post-class Q&A is explicitly discouraged so the recording captures the answer once. Source: `[3:03]` to `[3:47]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Database vs DBMS.** A database is the data model and contents; the DBMS is the system that stores, queries, and manages it. Source: `[5:41]` to `[7:16]`.
- **Course scope.** This is a systems course. It will teach internals, query execution, and how to build a DBMS, not how to run one as an admin. Source: `[4:09]` to `[4:53]`.
- **Student workflow.** Piazza handles class questions, Gradescope handles assignments, and Canvas carries grade visibility. Source: `[3:00]` to `[3:59]`.
- **Waitlist reality.** Enrollment is handled administratively once the class is full. Source: `[1:57]` to `[2:57]`, `[3:09]` to `[3:26]`.
- **Project shape.** The course is programming-heavy and expects C++. Source: `[8:39]` to `[8:50]`, `[10:26]` to `[10:31]`.
- **Lecture etiquette.** Interruptions are good during class; asking after class is not the preferred path. Source: `[3:03]` to `[3:47]`.
- **Roadmap.** Next lecture starts the relational model and relational algebra directly. Source: `[20:30]` to `[20:47]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The lecture is not "housekeeping" in a neutral sense. It is setting the operating model for the course: fast questions, recorded answers, and a strong bias toward systems thinking.
- The waitlist discussion is a reminder that the instructor does not control all resources. The course is being run under real enrollment constraints, not wishful ones.
- The "interrupt me in lecture" rule is there to improve the artifact, not just the live room.
- The course is opinionated about what counts as the subject. If you want database usage, the instructor points you elsewhere.
