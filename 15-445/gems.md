## Under-Appreciated Ideas
- PAX is the practical middle ground. A lot of "column stores" are really PAX-like layouts with better marketing. Source: [L06.md:30](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/15-445/lectures/L06.md#L30)
- `mmap` is a trap for DBMSs. Once you need observability, flush control, and policy, the generic OS abstraction stops being enough. Source: [L07.md:30](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/15-445/lectures/L07.md#L30)
- Sibling pointers are concurrency infrastructure, not just range-scan sugar. Source: [L09.md:29](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/15-445/lectures/L09.md#L29)
- `Kill yourself and retry` is often the cheap path. The lecture treats retry as a deliberate coordination strategy, not a bug. Source: [L11.md:31](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/15-445/lectures/L11.md#L31)
- Checkpoints bound recovery; they do not eliminate it. CLRs make recovery itself restartable.
