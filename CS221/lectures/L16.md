# L16: K-means

## Summary
- K-means is introduced as unsupervised clustering: the model gets unlabeled data and tries to group points by structure alone. Source: `[0:05]` to `[3:38]`.
- The objective is simple but important: minimize squared distance between each point and the centroid of its assigned cluster. Source: `[5:27]` to `[6:54]`.
- Once assignments are fixed, the centroid update is closed form: take the mean of the assigned points. Source: `[8:22]` to `[9:19]`.
- The algorithm alternates between assignment and centroid updates until convergence, which makes it a classic alternating-minimization loop. Source: `[13:00]` to `[14:35]`.
- K-means converges to a local minimum, not necessarily the global one, so initialization and restarts matter. Source: `[14:42]` to `[19:15]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Unsupervised learning.** The lecture contrasts labeled supervised learning with unlabeled clustering. Source: `[2:21]` to `[3:38]`.
- **Assignment vector `z_1 ... z_n`.** Each training point gets a cluster ID from `1..K`. Source: `[3:36]` to `[4:09]`.
- **Centroid `mu_k`.** A cluster is represented by a concrete point in space, ideally close to the points in that cluster. Source: `[4:15]` to `[5:18]`.
- **K-means objective.** Sum of squared distances from each point to its assigned centroid. Source: `[5:27]` to `[6:54]`.
- **Alternating minimization.** Fix centroids, reassign points; fix assignments, recompute centroids. Source: `[7:09]` to `[14:35]`.
- **Closed-form centroid update.** The mean minimizes squared distance for a fixed cluster assignment. Source: `[8:37]` to `[9:19]`.
- **Local minimum.** The algorithm is guaranteed to converge, but only to a local optimum. Source: `[14:42]` to `[15:02]`.
- **Initialization.** Multiple random restarts and k-means++ are practical responses to bad basins. Source: `[16:16]` to `[16:56]`.
- **Downstream representation.** Clustering can produce useful features for later supervised models. Source: `[18:48]` to `[19:12]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The hard part is not either half-step. Fixing assignments or fixing centroids is easy. The difficulty is the joint problem. Source: `[8:22]` to `[9:38]`, `[13:00]` to `[14:35]`.
- The centroid update is exact, not heuristic. The mean is the optimizer of the squared-distance objective, so the algorithm is not guessing there. Source: `[8:37]` to `[9:19]`.
- Initialization is part of the algorithmic design, not a cosmetic detail. A bad start can lock you into a visibly worse clustering. Source: `[15:04]` to `[16:11]`.
- K-means++ is really a diversification rule for seeds. It spreads centroids out so they can cover the data instead of collapsing onto one dense pocket. Source: `[16:26]` to `[16:56]`.
- The lecture is doing two jobs at once: discovery on unlabeled data and feature construction for later supervised work. Source: `[18:24]` to `[19:12]`.
