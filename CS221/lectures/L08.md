# L08: Group Distributionally Robust Optimization

## Summary
- The lecture opens with Gender Shades as the cleanest example of average accuracy hiding group-level harm. Source: `[0:25]` to `[2:06]`.
- Group-labeled regression adds a group variable `g` to each example so the loss can be measured per group, not just globally. Source: `[2:37]` to `[5:32]`.
- The key new objective is maximum group loss, `TrainLossMax`, which makes the worst-off group visible. Source: `[6:28]` to `[10:16]`.
- Minimizing average loss favors the majority group; minimizing maximum group loss shifts the solution toward balance. Source: `[10:56]` to `[12:40]`.
- The update rule for group DRO is simple: find the worst group right now and take the gradient on that group only. Source: `[13:21]` to `[15:00]`.
- The lecture ends by naming the hard parts the toy setup leaves out: intersectionality, unknown groups, and test-set overfitting. Source: `[16:24]` to `[17:32]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Gender Shades.** The motivating case study for disparity across skin tone and gender in commercial face classifiers. Source: `[0:25]` to `[1:58]`.
- **Per-group loss.** Average loss computed only over examples from one group `g`. Source: `[6:28]` to `[7:48]`.
- **Average loss.** Standard training objective that averages over all training examples. Source: `[5:21]` to `[6:11]`.
- **Maximum group loss.** Pointwise maximum of the per-group losses, used as the fairness-aware objective. Source: `[8:28]` to `[10:16]`.
- **Group DRO.** Group distributionally robust optimization, defined here as minimizing the maximum group loss. Source: `[11:34]` to `[12:05]`.
- **Argmax group `g*`.** The currently worst group; the gradient of the max comes from that group. Source: `[13:21]` to `[14:25]`.
- **Intersectionality.** Groups can be composed of multiple attributes, so coarse group labels are not enough. Source: `[16:24]` to `[16:53]`.
- **Gradient descent, not SGD.** The lecture explicitly says the max-over-groups objective does not fit the simple SGD update pattern. Source: `[15:01]` to `[15:20]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- Fairness here is not a post-processing patch. It is an objective choice. If you optimize the average, the majority wins. Source: `[11:02]` to `[12:40]`.
- The worst-group objective is piecewise simple. You do not differentiate the whole max expression; you differentiate the active worst group. Source: `[13:21]` to `[14:54]`.
- The lecture's fairness story is really a framing story too. It says the bigger question may be whether the technology should be used at all. Source: `[2:10]` to `[2:36]`.
- Group DRO improves balance but accepts a tradeoff: average loss gets worse when the solution moves toward the worst-off group. Source: `[11:49]` to `[12:05]`.
- The lecture is careful about scope. It talks about training loss only, not test performance, and flags that as a simplification. Source: `[17:02]` to `[17:18]`.
