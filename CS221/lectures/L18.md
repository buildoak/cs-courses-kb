# L18: Search 2 - A*

## Summary
- The lecture resumes the search story and then moves from uniform-cost search to heuristic search. Source: `[1:16:42]` to `[1:17:11]`.
- A heuristic `h(s)` is an estimate of future cost, and A* is UCS run on a reweighted cost function that incorporates that estimate. Source: `[57:01]` to `[1:03:47]`.
- Consistent heuristics obey triangle inequality and `h(goal) = 0`, which is what makes the transformed costs behave. Source: `[1:04:01]` to `[1:06:59]`.
- Admissibility is the weaker statement `h(s) <= true future cost`; consistency implies it. Source: `[1:14:38]` to `[1:15:17]`.
- Good heuristics come from relaxation: remove walls, remove constraints, or split the problem into easier independent subproblems. Source: `[1:16:13]` to `[1:21:45]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Heuristic `h(s)`.** An estimate of future cost from a state. Source: `[57:01]` to `[57:28]`.
- **A*.** UCS with heuristic-adjusted edge costs, not a separate search universe. Source: `[58:04]` to `[1:03:47]`.
- **Reweighted edge cost.** `cost' = cost + h(successor) - h(state)`. Source: `[58:21]` to `[59:06]`.
- **Consistent heuristic.** Satisfies triangle inequality and has zero heuristic at the goal. Source: `[1:04:01]` to `[1:06:59]`.
- **Admissible heuristic.** Never overestimates true future cost. Source: `[1:14:38]` to `[1:15:17]`.
- **Relaxation.** Solve an easier version of the problem and reuse its solution as a heuristic. Source: `[1:16:13]` to `[1:17:11]`.
- **Manhattan distance.** The closed-form heuristic for the relaxed grid and puzzle examples. Source: `[1:17:13]` to `[1:18:13]`, `[1:20:26]` to `[1:20:59]`.
- **Independent subproblems.** When constraints are removed, each tile or object can be scored separately. Source: `[1:20:24]` to `[1:21:08]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- A* is not greedy best-first search in disguise. The heuristic only changes the order in which UCS settles states; the final answer is still optimal when the heuristic is consistent. Source: `[58:04]` to `[1:03:47]`, `[1:07:01]` to `[1:10:28]`.
- Consistency is the key property because it preserves the non-negative structure that UCS needs. Source: `[1:04:01]` to `[1:10:28]`.
- The best heuristic is the most informative one that is still cheap to compute. The lecture says larger admissible heuristics are better because they shrink the explored region. Source: `[1:12:10]` to `[1:15:17]`.
- Relaxation is the standard heuristic factory. Remove constraints until the problem becomes closed-form or decomposable, then reuse that solution. Source: `[1:16:13]` to `[1:21:45]`.
- The heuristic does not change the final path cost; it changes how much of the graph you have to see before you can prove the answer. Source: `[1:03:27]` to `[1:03:47]`, `[1:12:10]` to `[1:13:47]`.

## Connections
- This lecture depends on the state/search model from Search 1. Without state, there is nothing to reweight. Source: `[1:13:00]` to `[1:20:16]` from L17.
- Dijkstra/UCS is the baseline; A* is the informed version. The lecture makes that connection explicit. Source: `[57:54]` to `[1:03:47]`, `[1:13:00]` to `[1:13:47]`.
- Manhattan distance is the canonical grid heuristic because a relaxed obstacle-free world has a closed-form solution. Source: `[1:17:13]` to `[1:18:13]`.
- The puzzle and tram examples are the same move in two skins: delete constraints until the future cost becomes easy to compute. Source: `[1:18:13]` to `[1:21:45]`.
