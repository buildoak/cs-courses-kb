# L19: Markov Decision Processes 1 - Value Iteration

## Summary
- MDPs are the stochastic version of search: states, actions, chance nodes, transition probabilities, rewards, and a discount factor. Source: `[0:15]` to `[0:39]`, `[2:12]` to `[2:32]`, `[18:30]` to `[18:41]`.
- A policy `pi` maps states to actions. Running it generates an episode, utility is the discounted sum of rewards, and value is expected utility. Source: `[3:16]` to `[6:19]`, `[31:56]` to `[37:38]`.
- The volcano and tram examples show why uncertainty matters. The same action can be right or wrong depending on slip or failure probabilities. Source: `[5:12]` to `[7:53]`, `[21:49]` to `[28:10]`.
- Policy evaluation computes `V_pi` and `Q_pi` with a recurrence over chance nodes. Source: `[47:50]` to `[49:14]`.
- Value iteration swaps the fixed policy for a max over actions to compute `V_opt` and `Q_opt`. Source: `[59:21]` to `[1:06:54]`, `[1:13:43]` to `[1:14:16]`.
- The lecture closes by revisiting relaxations as heuristic factories and clarifying structured perceptron convergence. Source: `[1:14:28]` to `[1:22:46]`.

## Key Concepts
- **MDP.** A graph with states, actions, chance nodes, transition probabilities, and rewards. Source: `[1:55]` to `[3:08]`.
- **Policy `pi`.** A mapping from states to actions. Source: `[3:16]` to `[3:44]`.
- **Episode.** The path you get when you run a policy. Source: `[3:52]` to `[4:48]`.
- **Utility `U`.** Discounted sum of rewards along an episode. Source: `[4:55]` to `[5:16]`, `[35:21]` to `[35:53]`.
- **Value `V_pi(s)`.** Expected utility if you start in state `s` and follow `pi`. Source: `[5:47]` to `[6:13]`.
- **Q value `Q_pi(s, a)`.** Expected utility if you first take action `a` in state `s` and then follow `pi`. Source: `[6:13]` to `[6:31]`.
- **Discount factor `gamma`.** A design choice between 0 and 1 that controls how much future reward matters. Source: `[18:30]` to `[18:41]`, `[39:34]` to `[41:03]`.
- **Policy evaluation.** The recurrence that computes `V_pi` and `Q_pi` for a fixed policy. Source: `[47:50]` to `[49:14]`.
- **Value iteration.** The recurrence that computes optimal value by maximizing over actions. Source: `[59:21]` to `[1:02:23]`.
- **Relaxation.** Removing constraints to create an easier problem whose answer becomes a heuristic. Source: `[1:15:07]` to `[1:20:25]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- Utility and value are different objects. Utility is one realized path; value is the expectation over all possible paths. Source: `[35:21]` to `[37:38]`.
- `gamma` is not a Markov violation or a risk model. The lecture calls it a design choice and says it only changes how future reward is weighted. Source: `[39:34]` to `[41:03]`.
- Policy evaluation and value iteration share the same recurrence shape. The only change is whether you average over one policy or maximize over actions. Source: `[47:50]` to `[49:14]`, `[59:40]` to `[1:02:23]`.
- Cycles force the discount discussion. The lecture says `gamma < 1` or an acyclic graph keeps the computation well-behaved. Source: `[1:12:46]` to `[1:13:05]`.
- The search recap at the end is the same engineering move as before: make the problem easier, solve the relaxed version, and reuse that structure. Source: `[1:15:07]` to `[1:20:25]`.

## Connections
- This is deterministic search with uncertainty grafted on top. The model now includes chance nodes and probabilistic transitions. Source: `[2:12]` to `[2:32]`, `[3:24]` to `[3:38]`.

- The tram example is the same pattern with a different surface. Failure probability turns a simple route choice into an MDP. Source: `[21:49]` to `[28:10]`.
- The final recap ties back to Search 2. Relaxation still serves as the heuristic factory, and structured perceptron still converges on equivalent paths. Source: `[1:14:28]` to `[1:22:46]`.
