# L09: Non-Linear Features

## Summary
- The lecture shows that you can get non-linear predictors without changing the learning algorithm, just by changing `phi(x)`. Source: `[0:06]` to `[3:26]`.
- Quadratic features, piecewise-constant region features, and periodic features are all just different feature maps. Source: `[1:51]` to `[7:19]`.
- The word "linear" is ambiguous here: the score is linear in weights and features, but not in the raw input `x`. Source: `[7:37]` to `[8:52]`.
- For classification, non-linearity is about the decision boundary. A circle can be built from a linear separator in feature space. Source: `[9:01]` to `[13:05]`.
- The closing summary is the useful one: you can make up your own features, but you pay in feature explosion if you are not careful. Source: `[13:10]` to `[13:58]`.

## Key Concepts
- **Hypothesis class.** The set of predictors the learner is allowed to choose from. Source: `[0:25]` to `[0:32]`.
- **Feature map `phi(x)`.** The representation that turns raw input into the vector the model actually sees. Source: `[0:36]` to `[0:58]`.
- **Quadratic predictor.** Add `x^2` to the feature vector and you can fit curved regression functions. Source: `[1:51]` to `[3:26]`.
- **Piecewise constant features.** Partition the input space into regions and use indicator functions for membership. Source: `[4:16]` to `[5:53]`.
- **Periodic features.** Add terms like `cos(3x)` when the problem has cyclic structure. Source: `[6:19]` to `[7:19]`.
- **Decision boundary.** In classification, non-linearity means the boundary is not a line. Source: `[9:01]` to `[9:54]`.
- **Quadratic circle classifier.** With features `[x1, x2, x1^2 + x2^2]` and weights `[2, 2, -1]`, the boundary becomes a circle centered at `(1,1)`. Source: `[10:29]` to `[11:48]`.
- **Feature space view.** The non-separable circle in input space becomes a linear cut in 3D feature space. Source: `[12:06]` to `[13:05]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The learning algorithm does not care whether `phi(x)` is hand-engineered or passed in directly. It only sees the transformed features. Source: `[8:34]` to `[8:55]`.
- Non-linearity is not a property of the optimizer here. It is a property of representation. Source: `[8:17]` to `[8:46]`.
- The feature-engineering trick scales badly. Quadratic features cost `d^2`, and region indicators can blow up to `B^D`. Source: `[3:30]` to `[3:52]`, `[5:53]` to `[6:09]`.
- The circle example is the cleanest mental model: non-linear in input space, linear in feature space. Source: `[11:54]` to `[13:05]`.
- The lecture is really teaching a split-brain definition of "linear." Good for optimization, bad for raw intuition. Source: `[7:37]` to `[8:14]`.

## Connections
- This is the hand-engineered predecessor to learned representation methods in neural networks.
- `L07` optimizes over these features; `L09` shows how much the features themselves matter.
- `L08` reuses the same predictor form but changes the objective to care about groups.
- The feature-map trick is the same basic move behind kernels and other basis-expansion methods.
