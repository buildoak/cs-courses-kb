# L20: Markov Decision Processes 2 - Reinforcement Learning

## Summary
- RL starts when the environment model is unknown. You get states and rewards back, not transition tables. Source: `[7:41]` to `[8:03]`, `[10:49]` to `[11:35]`.
- The agent/environment loop is online. You act, observe reward and next state, update your mental model, and keep going. Source: `[11:43]` to `[12:36]`.
- Monte Carlo methods come in model-based and model-free forms. Model-based estimates the MDP; model-free estimates `Q` values from experience. Source: `[13:17]` to `[30:49]`, `[39:16]` to `[39:28]`.
- SARSA is on-policy, Q-learning is off-policy, and Q-learning is the RL analogue of value iteration. Source: `[47:54]` to `[55:05]`.
- Exploration is the bottleneck. The lecture recommends epsilon-greedy as a pragmatic balance, then points to function approximation and deep RL for large state spaces. Source: `[56:07]` to `[61:12]`, `[1:03:08]` to `[1:05:13]`, `[1:11:09]` to `[1:13:56]`.

## Key Concepts
- **RL.** Learning while acting in the world, without knowing transition or reward models. Source: `[7:41]` to `[7:59]`.
- **Agent / environment.** The agent chooses actions; the environment returns rewards and next states. Source: `[11:43]` to `[11:59]`.
- **Episode.** A sampled trajectory generated by a policy. Source: `[3:56]` to `[4:48]`.
- **Online learning.** Data is produced by the policy you are currently running. Source: `[10:49]` to `[12:36]`.
- **Exploration policy / control policy.** The behavior policy used to gather experience. Source: `[27:17]` to `[28:17]`.
- **Model-based Monte Carlo.** Estimate transitions and rewards, then solve the MDP. Source: `[21:00]` to `[30:06]`.
- **Model-free Monte Carlo.** Estimate `Q_pi` directly from sampled returns. Source: `[22:42]` to `[29:04]`, `[39:16]` to `[39:28]`.
- **On-policy / off-policy.** On-policy learns about the same policy it behaves with; off-policy learns a different target policy. Source: `[27:17]` to `[29:04]`.
- **SARSA.** On-policy temporal-difference method. Source: `[47:14]` to `[53:11]`.
- **Q-learning.** Off-policy update toward `Q_opt`. Source: `[49:05]` to `[55:05]`.
- **Exploration-exploitation trade-off.** Randomness is necessary, but too much or too little hurts. Source: `[56:07]` to `[61:12]`.
- **Epsilon-greedy.** Mix mostly-greedy actions with occasional exploration. Source: `[1:01:12]` to `[1:01:40]`.
- **Function approximation.** Replace tabular state/action storage with a generalizing model. Source: `[1:03:08]` to `[1:05:13]`.
- **Deep RL.** Use a neural net for `Q_opt` on huge state spaces. Source: `[1:11:09]` to `[1:12:38]`.
- **Policy gradient / actor-critic.** Other approaches the lecture mentions briefly. Source: `[1:13:13]` to `[1:13:27]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- RL is not passive estimation. The policy you choose affects the data you get, so learning and control are coupled. Source: `[11:20]` to `[12:36]`, `[20:08]` to `[20:17]`.
- Model-free Monte Carlo can ignore transitions and rewards, but only because it is reusing sampled returns from the policy it actually ran. Source: `[26:45]` to `[30:06]`, `[39:16]` to `[39:28]`.
- Q-learning does not need to follow the optimal policy to learn it. That is why the lecture calls it off-policy. Source: `[49:05]` to `[55:05]`.
- Exploration is not a tuning footnote. Without it, the agent can miss reward hidden in states it never visits. Source: `[37:41]` to `[38:09]`, `[56:07]` to `[61:12]`.
- Deep RL is mostly about scaling the same old `Q_opt` idea to pixels and huge state spaces. The novelty is size, not a new problem statement. Source: `[1:11:09]` to `[1:12:38]`.

## Connections
- This lecture is the learning half of the MDP story from L19. MDPs give the model; RL removes the model and learns it from experience. Source: `[7:41]` to `[8:03]`, `[11:20]` to `[12:36]`.
- Monte Carlo, SARSA, and Q-learning line up with policy evaluation and value iteration. Source: `[39:16]` to `[39:28]`, `[47:54]` to `[55:05]`.
