# L04: Markov Decision Processes

## Summary
- Markov decision processes turn search into a stochastic decision problem: the world can branch after an action, so the object is expected utility rather than a single path.
- The formal MDP pieces are state, start state, actions, transition probabilities, rewards, end states, and a discount factor `gamma`.
- Policy evaluation asks how good a fixed policy is; value iteration asks for the best policy by replacing the policy action with a maximization over actions.
- The lecture's core recurrence is the Bellman style backup: expected immediate reward plus discounted future value.
- Convergence is guaranteed when `gamma < 1`, or when the state graph is acyclic.

## Key Concepts
- **State.** The current situation of the agent, plus whatever context is needed to predict the future.
- **Action.** A choice available in a state.
- **Transition probability `T(s, a, s')`.** The chance that action `a` in state `s` leads to `s'`.
- **Reward `R(s, a, s')`.** The immediate payoff for taking a transition.
- **End state.** A terminal state with no further decisions.
- **Discount factor `gamma`.** The weight given to future reward relative to immediate reward.
- **Policy `pi(s)`.** The action to take in each state.
- **`V_pi(s)`.** Expected utility when following policy `pi` from `s`.
- **`Q_pi(s, a)`.** Expected utility if you take `a` in `s` and then follow `pi`.
- **`V_opt(s)`.** Best achievable expected utility from `s`.
- **`Q_opt(s, a)`.** Best achievable expected utility after taking `a` in `s`.
- **Policy evaluation.** Solve for `V_pi` when the policy is fixed.
- **Value iteration.** Solve for `V_opt` and the induced optimal policy by taking a max over actions.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The lecture is not introducing a new graph model. It is reusing search and replacing deterministic successors with probability distributions.
- The discount factor is not just bookkeeping. It is what makes cyclic problems numerically stable enough to solve by iteration.
- Policy evaluation is a stepping stone, not a separate destination. Value iteration is policy evaluation with the action choice moved inside the backup.
- The "tram" example shows the important thing: the best policy is about expected future utility, not immediate reward.
- The same recurrence can be implemented as a simple loop over states, which is why MDPs are such a clean fit for dynamic programming.
