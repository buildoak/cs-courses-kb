# CS224N Gems

Cross-lecture synthesis from the 19 lecture articles in `lectures/`.

## Top Insights
- **CS224N is a chain of bottleneck removals, not a parade of architectures.** Word windows give way to corpora, static vectors to contextual encoders, single-source summaries to attention, pretraining to post-training, and leaderboard scores to phase-aware evaluation. The pattern repeats again in hardware and interpretability: widen the interface, then make the remaining structure cheaper to move through. - **Evaluation is part of the model, not a postscript.** The course keeps showing the same failure mode in different clothes: intrinsic scores do not guarantee usefulness, BLEU and ROUGE are brittle, benchmark suites are vulnerable to contamination and self-bias, and human-centered NLP has to account for cognitive load, harms, and actual users. Sources: [L02](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L02.md) L5-L32, [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L07.md) L5-L37, [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L10.md) L5-L34, [L11](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L11.md) L5-L37, [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L14.md) L5-L40, [L17](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L17.md) L5-L35, [L19](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L19.md) L5-L39.
- **The course keeps turning hard structure into soft scores, then re-imposing structure later.** Word2vec, GloVe, attention, beam search, reward models, and preference optimization all rely on smooth proxy objectives. Parsing, masking, tree constraints, and judge discipline are the later guardrails that keep the proxies from drifting too far from the task. - **Capability and controllability split fast.** Pretraining gives broad latent ability, post-training turns it into behavior, efficient training makes adaptation feasible, and interpretability tries to recover legibility after the fact. The model gets better before we know how to ask it what it is doing. Sources: [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L09.md) L5-L40, [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L10.md) L5-L34, [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L12.md) L5-L35, [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L14.md) L5-L40, [L19](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L19.md) L5-L39.
- **The hardware and data pipeline decide who gets to participate.** Mixed precision, ZeRO, FSDP, LoRA, and prompt-only control are not side quests. They decide whether adaptation happens at all, and whether it stays inside a research lab or becomes a general capability. ## Surprising Connections
- **Polysemy, context, and machine-native concepts are one arc.** L02 treats a word vector as an average over senses, L09 makes context the default representation, and L19 says human concepts may be the wrong ontology altogether. Same problem, three levels of abstraction. Sources: [L02](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L02.md) L5-L32, [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L09.md) L5-L40, [L19](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L19.md) L5-L39.
- **Speech BCI is seq2seq with a harsher alignment problem.** L06 and L07 show translation with flexible source-target reordering; L13 shows a monotonic, latency-bound variant where CTC, GRUs, beam search, and language-model rescoring have to cooperate just to recover words from neural noise. Sources: [L06](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L06.md) L3-L33, [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L07.md) L5-L37, [L13](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L13.md) L5-L38.
- **Dependency parsing and self-attention ask the same question from opposite ends.** Parsing hard-codes tree structure and global constraints; transformers let every token inspect every other token and then pay the quadratic bill. One system encodes syntax explicitly, the other lets syntax emerge from soft pairwise access. Sources: [L04](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L04.md) L3-L32, [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L08.md) L5-L41, [L16](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L16.md) L5-L34, [L18](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L18.md) L5-L34.
- **RLHF/DPO and benchmarking are both preference compression.** One collapses human judgment into reward models and policy updates; the other collapses task quality into a score. Both are vulnerable to gaming, contamination, prompt sensitivity, and judge bias. Sources: [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L10.md) L5-L34, [L11](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L11.md) L5-L37, [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L15.md) L5-L37, [L17](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L17.md) L5-L35.
- **Reasoning, agents, and interpretability all fail for the same reason: fluency is not faithfulness.** L14 questions whether rationales caused the answer or merely explained it, L19 questions whether saliency and tracing really localize meaning, and L17 warns that human feedback can encode the wrong values cleanly. Sources: [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L14.md) L5-L40, [L17](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L17.md) L5-L35, [L19](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L19.md) L5-L39.
- **Attention, alignment, and interpretability are all memory interfaces.** Attention is a query over learned memory, post-training steers model behavior through preference memory, and interpretability tries to map internal state back into human-readable concepts. Sources: [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L07.md) L5-L37, [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L10.md) L5-L34, [L19](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L19.md) L5-L39.

## Under-Appreciated Ideas
- **SGD noise is a feature, not a bug.** L02 says the noise can help optimization, and the rest of the course keeps choosing approximate mechanisms when they buy speed or stability. Sources: [L02](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L02.md) L5-L32, [L03](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L03.md) L5-L32, [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L08.md) L5-L41, [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L12.md) L5-L35.
- **Treebanks, benchmarks, and preference datasets are infrastructure.** They are not just data. They define what counts as a parse, a score, a task, or a comparison, and they become shared assets that outlive the immediate model. Sources: [L04](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L04.md) L3-L32, [L07](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L07.md) L5-L37, [L11](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L11.md) L5-L37, [L17](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L17.md) L5-L35.
- **The biggest win of DPO is operational simplicity.** L10 and L15 keep circling back to data freshness, implementation cost, and the fact that the easiest usable objective often beats the theoretically heavier one you cannot run. Sources: [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L10.md) L5-L34, [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L15.md) L5-L37.
- **LoRA is a hypothesis about update geometry, not just a compression trick.** The lecture treats low-rank adaptation as plausible because many fine-tuning updates appear low-rank enough to capture the useful motion with a tiny adapter. Sources: [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L12.md) L5-L35.
- **The sensor often matters more than the model.** L13 is a good reminder that decoding inner speech or phonemes starts with recording quality, spatial resolution, and latency budgets long before model fashion shows up. Sources: [L13](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L13.md) L5-L38.
- **Human language may be symbolic because symbols are robust under noise.** L18 does not treat symbolism as a triumph of old-school AI; it treats it as a clue about reliable communication, compositionality, and the limits of pure distributionalism. Sources: [L18](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L18.md) L5-L34.
- **A useful explanation must survive a negative control.** L19's sanity checks are the better standard: if the visualization or trace still looks good after randomization, it is decoration, not evidence. Sources: [L19](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L19.md) L5-L39, [L11](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L11.md) L5-L37.

## Open Questions
- **Can evaluation stay cheap without becoming untrustworthy?** The course keeps paying for this one: benchmarks need to be fast enough for iteration, but once they become the target, contamination, judge bias, and prompt sensitivity start distorting the result. Sources: [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L10.md) L5-L34, [L11](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L11.md) L5-L37, [L17](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L17.md) L5-L35.
- **Can alignment stay fresh without an expensive human-label refresh cycle?** L15 suggests the problem is not choosing PPO or DPO once; it is keeping the data and reward loop alive as the model changes. Sources: [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L15.md) L5-L37, [L17](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L17.md) L5-L35.
- **Can reasoning traces be made faithful enough to trust?** L14 is skeptical of rationales that merely sound right, and L19 extends the same skepticism to saliency and causal tracing. Sources: [L14](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L14.md) L5-L40, [L19](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L19.md) L5-L39.
- **Can transformer-era systems recover the useful structure of trees and symbols without giving up flexibility?** L04, L08, L16, and L18 all leave the same residue: structure still matters, but the old rigid form is probably not the final answer. Sources: [L04](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L04.md) L3-L32, [L08](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L08.md) L5-L41, [L16](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L16.md) L5-L34, [L18](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L18.md) L5-L34.
- **Can interpretability discover machine-native concepts that humans can actually use?** L19 explicitly asks for a bridge between human vocabulary and machine state, but the bridge may need new words, not just better maps. Sources: [L19](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L19.md) L5-L39, [L18](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L18.md) L5-L34.
- **How much adaptation is genuinely low-rank, and where does LoRA stop paying rent?** The lecture treats low-rank structure as real, but it is still an empirical claim about gradient geometry, not a law. Sources: [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L12.md) L5-L35.
- **Can BCIs decode inner speech without collapsing privacy?** L13 makes the assistive case first, then lands on the harder question: what happens when the system gets good enough to read what was never meant to leave the head? Sources: [L13](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L13.md) L5-L38.
- **Is the next jump more about architecture, data freshness, or evaluation discipline?** The course never lets any one layer claim victory on its own; progress keeps being a stack problem. Sources: [L09](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L09.md) L5-L40, [L10](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L10.md) L5-L34, [L11](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L11.md) L5-L37, [L12](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L12.md) L5-L35, [L15](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L15.md) L5-L37.
