# CS224N Gems

What kept repeating across CS224N was not a single model family. It was a repair loop: choose the right structure, choose the right proxy, keep gradients and memory under control, then check whether the thing you measured still matches the thing you wanted.

## Top Insights
- The course keeps replacing hand-built structure with learned structure, but it never stops caring about structure. Words move from dictionaries to corpora, syntax moves from rules to treebanks, translation moves from fixed windows to encoder-decoder attention, and philosophy plus interpretability ask whether the surviving structure is semantic or just useful. ## Surprising Connections
- Word2vec's observed-minus-expected gradient and DPO's preference fitting are cousins. Both turn counts or rankings into relative pressure rather than absolute labels. [L01:21](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L01.md#L21), [L10:23](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L10.md#L23), [L15:26](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L15.md#L26).
- Dependency parsing, seq2seq decoding, and agent trajectories are all action sequences over state. The stack/buffer parser, the decoder, and the web agent just expose different state machines to supervision. [L04:3](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L04.md#L3), [L06:3](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L06.md#L3), [L14:39](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L14.md#L39).
- CTC, masked language modeling, attention, and speech decoding are different answers to alignment. Blanks, masks, soft retrieval, and beam search are all ways to survive variable-length structure. ## Under-Appreciated Ideas
- Treebanks are infrastructure, not just labels. Once they exist, they become reusable resources for training, evaluation, and cross-lingual comparison. [L04:20](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L04.md#L20).
- Backprop's shape convention is an engineering compromise, not pure math. It exists so gradients can be subtracted from parameters without extra reshaping, and the whole thing is memoized chain rule, not magic. [L03:21](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L03.md#L21).
- RNNs are less interesting as "memory machines" than as sequential compute bottlenecks. Teacher forcing, truncated BPTT, and bidirectional encoders are practical accommodations to that fact. [L05:3](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L05.md#L3), [L06:3](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L06.md#L3).
- Small knobs matter more than they look. The 3/4 unigram exponent, max pooling as detector logic, and the two-channel embedding trick are all quiet design choices with outsized effects. [L02:21](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L02.md#L21), [L16:23](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L16.md#L23).
- The GRU choice in speech decoding is a latency/data tradeoff, not a downgraded LSTM. CTC is the right fit because the output is monotonic and the alignment gap is huge. [L13:26](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L13.md#L26).
- Optimizer state is the memory hog, not just the model weights. That is why mixed precision, ZeRO, FSDP, and LoRA are first-class model-adaptation ideas rather than infra trivia. [L12:5](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L12.md#L5).
- Human feedback is a pipeline, not a label. Human-centered evaluation, RLHF, AI feedback, and preference refresh all depend on the same brittle data loop. [L15:5](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L15.md#L5), [L17:5](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L17.md#L5).
- Residual connections and layer norm are optimization scaffolding, not decorative wiring. The model families are different, but the lesson is the same: make gradients boring. [L08:27](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L08.md#L27), [L03:21](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L03.md#L21).
- Benchmark monoculture is a real research tax. Contamination, self-bias, and English-heavy task suites can make progress look cleaner than it is. [L11:24](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L11.md#L24), [L17:24](/Users/otonashi/thinking/building/cs-courses-kb/CS224N/lectures/L17.md#L24).
