# L12 Efficient Neural Network Training

This lecture is a hardware lecture wearing an NLP badge.

## Summary
- The lecture starts with mixed precision: FP16 cuts memory and BF16/FP32 handling keeps the training numerically sane. Source: `[0:53]` to `[12:17]`.
- Distributed data parallelism is the baseline multi-GPU pattern, but the real memory savings come from ZeRO stage 1 and stage 2, which shard optimizer state and gradients. Source: `[17:33]` to `[28:05]`.
- ZeRO stage 3, also presented as FSDP, shards parameters too, which makes communication unavoidable but manageable by overlapping all-gather and reduce-scatter with compute. Source: `[28:10]` to `[1:00:30]`.
- Once full fine-tuning no longer fits, parameter-efficient fine-tuning becomes the escape hatch, and LoRA is the main concrete method the lecture teaches. Source: `[38:08]` to `[52:31]`.
- The closing flowchart is pragmatic: use mixed precision first, then ZeRO stage 2, then ZeRO stage 3, then checkpointing, and only then LoRA. Source: `[53:11]` to `[54:14]`.

## Key Concepts
- **FP32.** Standard 32-bit floating point with a large range and good precision. Source: `[2:29]` to `[3:28]`.
- **FP16.** Half-precision floats that cut memory in half but shrink range and precision. Source: `[3:31]` to `[5:03]`.
- **BF16.** A lower-precision format with FP32-like range, used on newer architectures. Source: `[11:25]` to `[12:17]`, `[53:11]` to `[53:19]`.
- **Mixed precision training.** Run the forward and backward passes in half precision, keep master weights in FP32, and use loss scaling to avoid underflow. Source: `[6:20]` to `[9:26]`.
- **DDP.** Each GPU gets a slice of the data, computes gradients, and synchronizes with all-reduce. Source: `[17:33]` to `[17:45]`, `[32:32]` to `[33:06]`.
- **ZeRO stage 1.** Shard optimizer state while keeping parameters and gradients replicated. Source: `[19:25]` to `[24:21]`.
- **ZeRO stage 2.** Shard gradients too, so each worker only instantiates the gradient it needs. Source: `[24:39]` to `[28:05]`.
- **ZeRO stage 3 / FSDP.** Shard parameters as well, turning forward and backward passes into a repeated all-gather / reduce-scatter schedule. Source: `[28:10]` to `[32:24]`.
- **LoRA.** Low-rank adaptation freezes the base model and learns small rank-R updates, usually on attention projection matrices. Source: `[45:11]` to `[52:31]`.
- **Gradient checkpointing.** A fallback when memory still does not fit after sharding and mixed precision. Source: `[53:27]` to `[53:35]`.

## Non-Obvious Insights
- The big memory bill is optimizer state, not just model weights. The lecture spends a lot of time on what Adam keeps around in addition to the parameters themselves. Source: `[17:56]` to `[18:12]`.
- ZeRO stage 1 and stage 2 are effectively free in communication terms because all-reduce decomposes into reduce-scatter plus all-gather. Source: `[24:05]` to `[24:21]`, `[27:37]` to `[28:03]`.
- FSDP does not remove communication. It makes communication tolerable by overlapping it with the forward and backward passes. Source: `[55:10]` to `[59:16]`.
- LoRA is not just a compression trick. It is justified by the observation that fine-tuning gradients tend to have low intrinsic rank. Source: `[45:20]` to `[46:47]`.
- Efficiency is also a participation problem: if only a few organizations can afford full fine-tuning, model adaptation becomes concentrated and less plural. Source: `[38:57]` to `[42:56]`.
