# CS224N

Stanford's natural language processing course with the deep-learning spine exposed instead of hidden. The Spring 2024 archive is the source of truth here, with a public YouTube playlist for the full lecture run.

## Syllabus

### Core arc

The course moves through the standard NLP stack, then pushes into the current LLM-era questions:

- Word vectors and distributional semantics
- Backpropagation and neural network basics
- Dependency parsing
- Recurrent neural networks
- Sequence-to-sequence models and machine translation
- Attention, final project framing, and LLM introduction
- Transformers and pretraining
- Post-training topics like RLHF, SFT, and DPO
- Benchmarking and evaluation
- Efficient training
- Speech brain-computer interfaces
- Reasoning and agents
- Life after DPO
- ConvNets, TreeRNNs, and constituency parsing
- Responsible NLP
- NLP, linguistics, and philosophy

### Assessment

The Spring 2024 archive breaks grading into:

- **Assignment 1, 6%**: Introduction to word vectors
- **Assignment 2, 14%**: Neural network foundations, tensor derivatives, dependency parsing
- **Assignment 3, 14%**: Neural machine translation with seq2seq, attention, and subwords
- **Assignment 4, 14%**: Self-supervised learning and fine-tuning with transformers
- **Final project, 49%**: Default BERT-style project or custom human-language / deep-learning project
- **Participation, 3%**: Guest lectures, surveys, Ed participation, and course karma

### Course mechanics

- Lectures are Tue/Thu in NVIDIA Auditorium, with livestreaming on Canvas.
- Lecture notes cover roughly the first half of the course.
- Assignments are in Python with NumPy and PyTorch.
- The public archive warns that assignments change year to year and old submissions should not be reused.
- Support sessions include a Python review, PyTorch tutorial, and Hugging Face tutorial.

## Instructor Bios

### Christopher Manning

Thomas M. Siebel Professor in Machine Learning, Professor of Linguistics and Computer Science, and Senior Fellow at Stanford HAI. He founded the Stanford NLP Group and his research spans neural NLP, word vectors, dependency parsing, attention, machine translation, pretraining, and language understanding. He also coauthored foundational NLP and information retrieval textbooks, and the CS224N video series is part of his public teaching footprint.

### Shikhar Murty

Spring 2024 CS224N head TA and a regular lecturer on the course's later systems-heavy material. Stanford Profiles lists him as a Research Asst - Graduate in Computer Science. The Spring 2024 office-hours page groups him under NLP, Human-AI interaction, language models, compositionality, reasoning, and grounding, which matches the more current-LLM parts of the course arc.

## Why It Matters

Inference from the syllabus arc:

- It is the cleanest public bridge from classical NLP to modern LLM practice.
- The class does not stop at theory; it makes you implement the ideas in PyTorch.
- The syllabus covers the whole production chain: representation, modeling, pretraining, post-training, evaluation, and efficient training.
- The project track gives a realistic way to go from course material to a custom language system or a BERT-style baseline.
- The later lectures pull the course out of a stale textbook shape and into the current research and systems conversation.
